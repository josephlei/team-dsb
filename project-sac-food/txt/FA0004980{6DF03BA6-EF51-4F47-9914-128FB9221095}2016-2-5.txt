Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

# 1b

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION

OBSERVATIONS: Food safety certificate was not available at time of inspection. Provide a copy of certificate on-site within 30 days.
- 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION.
Note: "List of approved and accredited food safety certification examinations" handout provided during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee
who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Proof of current food handler card for two employees was not available during inspection. Correct within 30 days.
- 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Cut tomatoes (43F-44F) in container on prep table observed with insufficient ice around to maintain below 41F.
Maintain ice as high as food container on all sides of container. Corrected by adding more ice during inspection.
Observed ambient temperature of the walk in cooler measured at 42F-43F. Food items, including raw meats and a large container of
soup, measured at 43F during inspection. Maintain all potentially hazardous food at/below 41F for cold food. Service unit and
monitor to ensure food temperature drops to 41F within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Observed one deep container of cooked pasta being cooled with cover inside 1 door upright refrigerator
measured at 62F. Per operator, food item was prepared one hour prior to inspection. Cool food in shallow pans without cover.
Corrected during inspection.
Note: "Cool food fast for food safety" sticker provided during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

OBSERVATIONS: Observed improper storage of the following food items:
- prepackaged raw pork stored above cooked food items inside 2 door freezer by ice machine
- one box of raw chicken stored above prepackaged raw pork and beef inside walk in cooler
Store food according to minimum cooking temperatures: raw chicken on the very bottom rack -> then raw beef/pork -> seafood ->
vegetables and ready to eat food on the top rack in the refrigerator. Correct today.
Note: "proper food storage in refrigerator and freezers" sticker provided during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: The following locations were observed with dust/mold like residue build up:
- fan guard inside 1 door upright refrigerator in main cook's line
- fan guards inside walk in cooler
- metal racks inside walk in cooler
Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Hand sink at bar missing both cold/hot valves. Repair within 30 days. - REPEAT VIOLATION.
Note: per manager, cold/hot water valves have been ordered and will be installed within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Toilet roll dispenser observed empty. Loose rolls are available in area. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)
