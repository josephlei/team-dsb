#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Lamb curry and chicken curry are stored in steam table at 110F and 117F, respective. Corrected; food items were
reheated on stove to 165F during inspection. Steam table was adjusted during inspection. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Employees have not obtained California food handler cards. Obtain within 7 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: A piece of raw fish fillet is observed improperly thawing in a bowl on the kitchen prep table, fillet was still
semi-frozen. Corrected; fish fillet was placed under running cold water during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Torching of raw whole chicken is performed outside of facility instead of underneath approved type 1 ventilation
hood inside kitchen. Corrected.
Open bags of garlic powder, flour, and sugar are stored on kitchen storage shelf. Transfer food into containers with tight fitting lid
and label within 2 days.
Raw chicken and raw fish fillets are stored above potato fries in upright freezer. In addition, raw chicken is stored above fish fillets
in 2-door upright freezer. Rearrange food according to EMD food storage handout posted on side of 2-door upright freezer to
prevent cross contamination. Correct within 2 days. Note: Food contamination was not observed. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Old grease build-up is observed on floor below fryers. Clean and maintain within 3 days.
Gray build-up is observed on wall and floor behind the 3-compartment warewash sink. Clean and maintain within 3 days. (REPEAT
VIOLATION)
Dust build-up is observed on fan guards inside the beverage merchandiser. Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Facility lacks chlorine sanitizer test strips. Provide within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: A wood knife storage block with slots is used in kitchen; storage block cannot be disassemble for cleaning and
sanitizing. Remove unapproved knife storage block from use today. Note: A wall-mounted magnetic knife storage strip is
recommended.
Barbecue sauce is observed on several new single-use disposable gloves stored in a bundle on the kitchen prep table. Corrected;
contaminated gloves were voluntarily discarded by operator during inspection.
Plastic bags with ink printing are used to store raw chicken, peas, and raw goat inside upright freezer. Replace with clear food
grade bags within 2 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION)
Scotch tape is used on kitchen door handle. REmove scotch tape today. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Facility lacks a probe thermometer. Provide within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)
