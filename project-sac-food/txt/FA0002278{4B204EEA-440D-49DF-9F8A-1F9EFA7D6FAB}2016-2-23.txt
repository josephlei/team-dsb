#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Cheese pizza 122F-130F and chicken strips 131F stored inside warmer unit. Per employee, they do save leftovers
hot food items that haven't been saved from previous day. Per school district HACCP plan, all food served should be between
135F-150F. Potentially hazardous foods may not be stored at room temperature unless time as a public health control (TPHC) is
used. Store foods at/below 41F or at/above 135F. TPHC handout provided & discussed with operator. If this method is to be used,
procedure must be submitted to inspector & maintained on site. Food items that were stored at room temperature during service
were voluntarily discarded during inspection.
(Note: During last routine inspection on 10-13-15, employees notified inspector that all leftovers were discarded after lunch service.)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Observed helpers come to kitchen area after eating their lunch and start donning single use gloves without first
washing their hands. Helpers were asked to wash their hands before donning gloves. Corrected.
- Observed dishwasher handle dirty dishes, and then handle clean dishes without washing hands in between. Employee was
educated about handwashing.
Hands must be washed whenever contaminated, in between different tasks, and/or prior to glove use. Handwashing handout was
provided. Ensure all employees and helpers are adequately trained in all aspects of food safety that apply to their duties.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Water in high temperature dishwasher reached 153F-157F at utensil surface after several cleaning/rinsing cycles.
Per employees, they do not measure temperature at utensil surface area inside dish machine but only look at thermometer located
on top of dish machine. Hot water should be at least 160F on utensil contact surface (measured inside dish machine). Correct within
24 hours. REPEAT VIOLATION
(Also, see violation #34)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found one dead, dry cockroach inside cabinet storing clean trays next to handwash sink. Remove and clean area
today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Observed spider webs beneath clean drain board of dish machine. Clean within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Facility is missing an irreversible registering temperature indicator to measure a utensil surface temperature
inside high temp dish machine. Provide within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Dumpster lid open when not in use. Keep lids closed between use. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)
