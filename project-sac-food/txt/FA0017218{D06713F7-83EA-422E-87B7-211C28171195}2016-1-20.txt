#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Whipped butter 75F stored on the back prep table. Per operator, whipped butter was stored out since 8/9am and
will be used to spread bread. Potentially hazardous foods may not be stored at room temperature unless time as a public health
control (TPHC) is used.
Minor Violation: - Alfredo sauce 130F-132F inside soup warmer. Operator increased warmer heat setting.
- Chicken strips 46F stored inside reach-in cooler across from pizza oven.
Store foods at/below 41F or at/above 135F. TPHC handout provided & discussed with operator. Per operator, he will be purchasing
regular butter instead of whipped. Food was voluntarily discarded during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION

OBSERVATIONS: Missing food safety certificate. Operator will be retaking food safety class. Obtain within 60 days and maintain
original on-site available for review.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee
who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open food items (beans, chicken, ravioli, cheese, etc) stored inside 1-door & 2-door True freezer. Store food items
covered. Operator started covering foods during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open bag with floor stored on the floor next to walk-in cooler. Operator corrected during inspection. REPEAT
VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: - Brownish residue pooling inside both Keg coolers.
- Grease residue build up on the floor around grease trap.
- Mold like residue and old food build up inside both floor sinks located at server's area.

Clean and maintain within 5 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: - Facility is utilizing container without handle to scoop out ice. Also, found broken scoop in a container stored on
top of ice machine. Provide scoop with handle and store handle up. Correct within 2 days.
- Deteriorated door gaskets of reach-in cooler across from pizza oven. Replace within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Probe thermometer was reading 59F inside ice water. Thermometer was calibrated during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Soiled cloth towel hanging next to pizza prep reach-in cooler. Store soiled cloth towel in sanitizer bucket with 100
ppm chlorine or 200ppm quat ammonium when not in active use (use only to wipe counter tops).
(Note: Hands shall be properly washed at designated handwash sink whenever contaminated.) Cloth towel and handwashing
handout was provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: - Drain pipes at front area lack proper air gap. Provide at least 1 inch air gap within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Mop sink missing backflow prevention device between Y connector and a hose. Install backflow prevention device or remove Y
connector within 7 days.
Backflow prevention handout was provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44a

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Observed cluttered area with clothing between wall and walk-in cooler (near window). Clean and organize this
area, and removed all unnecessary items within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of
the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Flooring is deteriorated and observed without proper coving at kitchen area. Concrete flooring is exposed
especially near warewash sink, dish machine, reach-in cooler by pizza oven, and dough prep area. Facility shall upgrade to
approved flooring and coving within one year. Approved floor handout provided. (Last time deteriorated flooring was noted during
reinspection.)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
