# 11

REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

OBSERVATIONS: Red curry and white rice in warmers and measured at 109 F and 130 F using a thermocouple. These were cooked
and cooled yesterday. Employee stated that these were reheated before being put inside the warmers about 30 minutes before.
Food cooked, then cooled needs to be reheated to at least 165 F before being put in hot holding. Curry and rice were reheated to
over 165 during the inspection.
- REPEAT VIOLATION If this violation is observed again on the next inspection, a charged reinspection will be conducted.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee touch raw ground pork, wash the gloves he was wearing without using soap and then wipe
his hands on a towel (clean) hanging on his apron. Inspector stopped employee and asked him to wash his hands properly before
changing gloves. This was done before employee touched any food or food contact items to prevent any possible cross
contamination. Operator stated that she will be more vigilant with her employees' handwashing. She will also not allow her
employees to hang cloth towels to be hung on their . Inspector had a lengthy discussion on handwashing with the operator.
Operator stated that she will be more vigilant with her employees' handwashing. She will also not allow cloth towels to be carried by
her employees. Handwashing and glove use handout was also provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: One container of ready-to-use eating utensils was found unclean. Wash and sanitize containers and contents
today. Clean regularly. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Soiled wiping cloths with no sanitizer found on counter tops. Multi-use wiping cloths used to wipe service
counters or other surfaces that may come in contact with food or food contact items shall be kept in clean water with sanitizer.
Corrected during the inspection. Set up of sanitizer buckets before staring food prep was recommended.

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Comb, deodorant and lint remover were found beside and above food items on dry storage racks. Designate
approved storage areas for personal items away from areas for food prep and storage of food and food-contact items. Corrected
during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Vinyl covering under cook's line cold top was found detached and lifted from the floor with debris accumulation
under it. Clean area of debris today. Repair and seal the floor within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
