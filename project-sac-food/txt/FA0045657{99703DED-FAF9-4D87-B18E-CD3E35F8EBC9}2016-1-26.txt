DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Employees unaware of proper handwashing and shawarma cookings without interruption requirement. One
shawarma rotator was not rotating and one was turned off for immediate use and all manager noted that shawarma was cooking
without interruption. One rotator was non-functional. Handwashing and Shawarma requirement handout and education was

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Employee bottle beverages were found in multiple ares throughout food prep areas. One open cup of tea was
stored on the counter top at cook's line. All employee beverages must be stored in a cup with straw and lid and located below food
prep areas. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee at meat department handle cash and then donning gloves without washing hands first. Hands
must always be washed prior to glove use. Hands must be properly washed between different tasks. Gloves may not be re-used.
Hands must be washed with soap and warm water, and dried with a single use paper towel between handling raw meat and touching
other food items or utensils. Corrected

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Bulk ham and raw beef stored between 43F-44F inside meat department upright cooler. Manager lowered
thermostat to colder setting during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: - Dry food residue build up on meat slicer at meat dept. Food-contact surfaces and equipment used for potentially
hazardous foods should be cleaned as needed throughout the day but must be cleaned no less than every 4 hours to prevent the
growth of microorganisms on those surfaces. Clean today before use and maintain.
- Black residue build up on cloths used to line wooden trays storing bread dough. Per manager, cloths are replaced once a week.
Maintain cloths clean at all times. Replace more often. Maintain clean at all times. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Raw beef feet improperly thawing inside prep sink at meat dept. This practice is not allowed by law. Potentially
hazardous food must be thawed in an approved method: under refrigeration, under cold running water while submerged in water, in
microwave, or during the cooking process. Corrected. Thawing sticker was provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Improper food storage:
- raw shelled eggs stored above cheese at retail dairy cooler
- uncooked chicken fillets and nuggets stored above potato round food items inside retail freezer
- raw beef stored above cooked chick peas inside walk-in cooler
- raw beef stored next to frozen vegetables inside chest type freezer at cook's line
- puff pastry stored above raw beef inside retail freezer
Store food in the following order from bottom to top: raw chicken, raw beef, raw pork, seafood, raw shelled eggs, vegetables then
ready to eat food. Correct today.

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Window cleaner stored above prep table at cook's line. Store chemicals away and below food and food related
items. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Boxes with tomato at produce retail area and cutting board at meat dept. stored on the floor. Store at least 6
inches off the floor. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Found several unlabeled bulk bin containers with flour, sesame seeds, black seeds, etc at front cabinet area.
Label today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 32a

FOOD PRESENTATION AND LABELING

OBSERVATIONS: - Found unlabeled packaged shredded carrots, pistachios, beef, dry apricots/plums at retail area (some where in
freezer). Some labels have incomplete facility information. Provide labels on each package that include: common name of food, list
of ingredients in descending order of predominance by weight, quantity, and name and place of business of the manufacturer,
packer, or distributer. Correct within 3 days. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION
- Unlabeled display case with freshly baked bread. Place label on display case within 7 days.
Note: Most packaged food items have been labeled.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food offered for human consumption shall be honestly presented in a way that does not mislead or misinform the
consumer. Food packaged in a food facility shall bear a label that complies with the labeling requirements prescribed by the Sherman Food,
Drug, and Cosmetic Law which include: common name of the food, ingredients list, quantity of contents, name and address of
manufacturer, nutritional information as specified in 21 C.F.R. (114087, 114088, 114089, 114089.1(a, b), 114090, 114093.1)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
