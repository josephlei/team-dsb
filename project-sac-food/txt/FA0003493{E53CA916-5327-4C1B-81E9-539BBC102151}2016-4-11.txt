#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Cooked rice in insert on rack in 6-inch pan at 85 F. Rice had finished cooking around 1 p.m. Rice had scoop and
was in use. Rice broken into shallower containers and relocated into walk-in cooler. All potentially hazardous foods (PHFs) must be
maintained at 135 F and above or 41 F and below. If cooked foods are not offered for immediate sale, they must be either maintained
under temperature control (e.g. steam table) or they must be rapidly cooled.
Cooked beef in insert on grill was observed at 128 F. Beef reheated to 203 F on grill and relocated to steam table.
MINOR VIOLATION:
Shredded cheese observed at 43 F on cold top. Correct by storing smaller portions on cold top and the remainder in reach-in
refrigerator under cold top or in walk-in refrigerator.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Inserts of cooked potatoes 4-6" in depth cooling on rack with ice only on bottom of insert at 80 F. Potatoes had
finished cooking at 1 p.m. Cool entire rack in walk-in refrigerator to speed up process. More ice added to pans and pans relocated to
walk-in refrigerator. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Potato slicer has built up food debris. Clean and sanitize today and maintain on daily basis.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Dust accumulation near make-up air return on ceiling in kitchen. Clean and maintain within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Salsa containers and paper cups that lack handles are being used as scoops in bulk food bins. Use scoops with
handles and keep handle out of food product. Correct within 1 day. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Carbon dioxide (CO2) tanks are not secured. Secure tanks to rigid structure within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Mop is in mop bucket. Store mop with head upright to facilitate drying. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Back door is being propped open. Discontinue propping door open immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Restroom lacks self-closing device. Correct within 7 days.
