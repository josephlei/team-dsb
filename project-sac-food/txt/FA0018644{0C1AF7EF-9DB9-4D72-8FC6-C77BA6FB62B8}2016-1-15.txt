# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Maximum temperature measured at the plate inside the chemical dish machine reached the minimum required 120
F only once (120.1 F) in six cycles. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Dust/mold observed on metro rack shelves and fan guards inside the walk-in refrigerator. Heavy dus
accumulation also observed on ceiling vent duct above dry storage. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Stopper mechanism of left-most (sanitizing) compartment of three compartment sink is inoperable. Manager
stated that they wash and rinse using the sink and always sanitize using the automatic dish machine. Repair stopper mechanism (or
provide a plug for the drain) so that facility may have a way to manually warewash (wash-rinse-sanitize-air dry) in the event the dish
machine fails. Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Peeling rust/coating observed on two metro wire racks inside the walk-in refrigerator. Clean within 24 hours and
correct peeling issue within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: a. Light inside the refrigerator beside the flat grill is inoperable. Correct within 2 days.
b. Baffle filters in bot ventilation hoods were installed incorrectly (horizontally). Correct within 24 hours.

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: a. Water flow from faucet at three compartment sink was very weak. Also, water flow can only be completely shut
off using the bypass valve. Correct within 2 days.
b. Waste waster drain line from ice machine is not aligned with the floor sink and water was observed dripping on the floor instead.
Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Shared garbage dumpster outside was found with lids open. Work with co-tenants and/or landlord to always keep
lids closed when not being filled to prevent vermin infestation and possible dirty water (leachate) run-off to storm water drain. Close
garbage dumpster lids as soon as possible today, and clean the debris on the ground inside the trash enclosure within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Loose toilet paper rolls found on railing inside the men's restroom. Manager stated that they were replacing the
toilet paper dispenser but the wrong item was provided to them. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
