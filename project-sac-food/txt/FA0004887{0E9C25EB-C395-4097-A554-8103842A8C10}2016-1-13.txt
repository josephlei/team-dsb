#9

COOLING METHODS

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: Creamy tomato basil soup (at 75 F) and beef steak noodle soup (at 67 F) were observed being improperly cooled in
closed deep containers inside the small walk-in refrigerator (ambient temperature inside at 40 F). Manager stated that he thinks the
soup was cooked before lunch. Food items were temped at about 4:00 PM. With the soups stored in the manner described above,
the time temperature critical limits would not be complied with. Manager voluntarily discarded the creamy tomato soup (3 qts) and
beef steak noodle soup (2 qts) during the inspection.
- SECOND REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Potato salad and pasta salad found stored on the bottom shelf of the one-door upright refrigerator were measured
with a thermocouple at 50 F and 48 F, respectively. Further investigation revealed that the ambient air temperatures inside this
refrigerator ranged from 39 F at the top shelf (right under the fan) to 49 F on the bottom shelf. Large plastic containers were found
blocking the air flow to the bottom parts of the unit. Operator shall rearrange stocked food to ensure optimal cold air flow to all
parts of the unit. Manager stated that the thermostat was already set to maximum coldness. Have the unit serviced if it cannot keep
all contents at 41 or below.
- Manager moved pasta and potato salads into the two door upright refrigerator (ambient air temp at 33 F) during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Heavy dust accumulation observed on fan being used i9n the back prep area. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Gaskets on doors of upright refrigerators were found torn. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be

foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Soiled wiping cloth with no sanitizer found on back prep counter top. Correctly-set up sanitizer buckets were
available. Multi-use wiping cloths used to wipe service counters or other surfaces that may come in contact with food or food
contact items shall be kept in clean water with sanitizer. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
