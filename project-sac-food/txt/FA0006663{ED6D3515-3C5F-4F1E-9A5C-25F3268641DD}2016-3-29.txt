#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Rear handwash station was blocked by boxes and mop bucket and sleeves of cups in the
sink. Do not place any item in or around all handwash stations. Boxes and other items were relocated at the time of inspection.
MINOR VIOLATION - Front handwash station is not functional and marked "Do Not Use". Repair front handwash station to become
fully functional within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Staff was not aware of the proper warewashing procedure. Education was provided at the time of inspection to
wash (with soap), rinse (with water), sanitize (with chemical) then air dry.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: One food handler certificate was not available. Provide a copy of the certificate and maintain it on site. Correct
within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Half and half (43-49F) in the glass case reach-in was out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all potentially
hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Spillage of whole coffee beans observed on the counter under the cappuccino machine. Clean this area free of
beans immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.
Wall in the back area is observed with food particles (namely jelly filling and sugar glaze) . Clean wall within 24 hours to prevent
attracting vermin.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Community shared dumpster lids were open. Keep lids closed at all times to prevent vermin infestation.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Toilet paper dispenser in the restroom is empty. Per owner, customers and non-customers that use the restroom
is stealing the supplies. Owner is not providing toilet paper as they enter the restroom. Toilet paper is stored near the hallway
leading to the restroom. Keep dispenser stocked at all times. Lock access to the restroom if necessary to maintain supplies
available at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Mop is stored in the mop bucket. Hang mops after use to allow drying. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Square hole observed on the ceiling above the carbon dioxide tanks in the back area. Seal hole with approved
materials to prevent vermin entry/infestation. Correct within 3 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
