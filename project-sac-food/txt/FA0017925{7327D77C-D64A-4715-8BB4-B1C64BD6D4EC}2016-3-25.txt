#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Measured beans at 110 F, beef at 115 F and chicken at 102 F in steam table. All items were placed on grill to be
reheated to 165 F and over.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Employees did not demonstrate acceptable knowledge on warewashing procedures, thermometer calibration, and
reheating temperatures. Education provided during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Uncovered coffee next to chips on chest freezer. Use cups with lids and straw and store away from food
products.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee crack raw shelled eggs this rinse hands for about 5 seconds at handwash sink. Employee
was directed to rewash hands for at least 15 seconds.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor in storage/warewash room noted with debris buildup under shelving and ice machine. Clean within 24
hours.
Non food contact portion of meat slicer noted with old food debris. Wash and sanitize today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Gaskets to prep cooler are deteriorated. Replace/secure within 30 days. Ambient air temperature of unit is below
41 F.
Unapproved plastic cups used as scoops in seasoning containers next to 1-door freezer. Provide scoops with handles within 3
days. Repeat Violation.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Both probe thermometers were inaccurate. One reads 200 F and other 220 F in ice water. Calibrated
thermometers to 32 F in ice water during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Multi-use wiping cloths stored on prep cooler. Maintain wiping cloths inside sanitizer bucket. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
