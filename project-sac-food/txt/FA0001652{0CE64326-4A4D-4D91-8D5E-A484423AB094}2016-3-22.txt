#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Raw shell eggs out near cook line and inside the reach-in refrigerator under food prep counter were measured
with a thermpocouple at 62 F and 53 F, respectively. Ambient temperature inside that reach-in refrigerator was measured at 39 F.
Do not leave potentially hazardous food (PHF) items out of temperature control longer than necessary. Manager unilaterally
discarded the eggs (7 pcs) during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water valve on food prep area handwash sink faucet not working correctly - will not turn on dispense hot
water about 90% of the time. Provide warm water at 100 F minimum for handwashing at all times. Correct within 24 hours.
- SECOND REPEAT VIOLATION - (Observed and written up during the last three inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: a. Raw shell eggs (2 of which had broken shells) were found on top of pancakes in plastic bags inside the
reach-in refrigerator under the food prep counter. Egg yolk residues were found on pancake bags. Manager unilaterally discarded
the pancakes during the inspection.
b. Apple and strawberry pies were found stored uncovered in open containers inside the walk-in freezer where there were leaks
coming from the ceiling (no actual contamination observed). Pies in cartons were covered during the inspection.
- SECOND REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Heavy dust accumulation observed on ceiling vents/ducts above the Q-ing oven. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Monitoring thermometers in the walk-in refrigerator and reach-in refrigerators at the front counter area were
situated at the rears of unit and/or were difficult to read. Code requires monitoring thermometers for refrigeration units to be located
at the warmest parts of the units and where they are easily readable. Correct within 3 days.
- SECOND REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Sanitizer level in wiping cloth bucket was measured at 0 ppm chlorine. Use the test strips to check sanitizer level
regularly and maintain at 100 ppm minimum at all times. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: a. Waste water drain line from McCafe machine and from drive thru soda machine were found with the ends inside
the floor sinks. Provide air gaps at least 1 inch above the flood rims of the floor drains within 2 days.
- REPEAT VIOLATION b. Faucet with an attached hose inside the garbage enclosure (pooled dirty water observed near faucet) lacks an anti-backflow
device. Provide and install hose bib vacuum breaker within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Loose toilet paper roll found stored on top of empty dispenser inside the women’s restroom. Corrected during the
inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)
