#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: CONTAINER WITH POTATOES, SAUSAGE AND EGG MIXTURE, COVERED WITH PLASTIC INSIDE THE WALK IN
REFRIGERATOR. TEMPERATURE OF FOOD ITEM MEASURED AT 47 DEGREES F. PER MANAGER, FOOD ITEM WAS PLACED INTO
THE WALK IN AT 9:30AM THIS MORNING. FOOD ITEM DID NOT EXCEED THE CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS. HOWEVER, IMPROPER
COOLING, AS FOOD ITEM WAS COVERED WHILE STILL IN THE PROCESS OF COOLING. INSTRUCT STAFF NOT TO COVER FOOD
ITEMS THAT ARE IN THE MIDST OF THE COOLING PROCESS. ALLOW FOOD ITEM TO COOL DOWN TO 41 DEGREES WITHIN 6
HOURS FIRST BEFORE COVERING THE CONTAINER. CORRECTED DURING INSPECTION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)
