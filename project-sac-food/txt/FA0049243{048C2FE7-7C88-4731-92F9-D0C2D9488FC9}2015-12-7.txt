#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: Lacking towels in the restroom. Note: towels observed in the garbage can. Corrected at the time of inspection.
Major violation.
Minor violations:
Towel dispensers (fold and pull) do not fit towel rolls, so towels are balanced on nearby surfaces. Keep towels in the towel
dispensers (new dispensers of if needed) to keep clean and dry.
Law states: 113953.2. A handwashing facility shall be provided with the following in dispensers at, or adjacent to, each
handwashing facility:
(a) Handwashing cleanser.
(b) Sanitary single-use towels or a heated-air hand drying device.
Out of the dispenser is not considered sanitary because the bulk towels must be touched to tear a towel to dry hands and the
towels are unprotected from previous users/other sources of contamination.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas.
Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and
sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953,
113953.1, 113953.2)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Front hand sink water temperature measured 74 deg. F. Back handsink temperature measured 114 deg. F.
California Health and Safety Code section 113953 states:
Handwashing facilities shall be equipped to provide warm water under pressure for a minimum of 15 seconds through a mixing
valve or combination faucet. If the temperature of water provided to a hand washing sink is not readily adjustable at the faucet, the
temperature of the water shall be at least 100ºF, but not greater than 108ºF.
Front sink turned up and back sink turned down at the time of inspection. Check at least seasonally.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)
