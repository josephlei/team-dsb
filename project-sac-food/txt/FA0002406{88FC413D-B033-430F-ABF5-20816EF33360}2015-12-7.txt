# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Six rodent droppings observed in toilet room of employee restroom. Approximately 10-15 rodent droppings behind
washer and dryer in small room with employee restroom sink. No other droppings were observed in kitchen, food storage, food
preparation or dishwashing areas. Droppings were removed during inspection. There is a dryer vent that leads to small caged room
directly behind the washer and dryer that is not part of the kitchen. The vent is open in allowing for possible vermin entrance
(photographs taken). Droppings were observed in the small caged area. Address the following:
-Remove dryer vent and seal hole in wall.
-There is a small white pipe behind the washer that is open. Cap off this pipe.
-The washer and dryer are no longer in use. Remove from school. Unused equipment can lead to vermin harborage.
-The small caged area has two openings in the metal screening. Seal holes in screen.
-Provide a door sweep on restroom door leading to kitchen.
A re-inspection will occur next week to follow up on rodent droppings.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Ice buildup observed on back wall of Delfield two door freezer. Ice buildup observed on wall on right hand side of
Beverage air freezer. Ice building up on food box. Repair freezers to prevent ice from building up. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Exhaust fan in employee restroom is not operational. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: -Gaps and holes around the pipes going from three compartment sink to wall. Seal holes and gaps to prevent
vermin entrance and harborage. Correct within 7 days.
-School has an food dry storage room that is located outside the back door (left hand side when coming from the kitchen). This
building is not vermin proof. There are two spots in the ceiling that have been removed exposing raw wood. The ventilation fan is
not screened. Repair ceiling with materials that are smooth and easily cleanable. Screen off the ceiling vent. Repair over winter
break. Submit photographs for proof of correction. Failure to receive corrections will result in a charged re-inspection.
