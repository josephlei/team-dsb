Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: 8 food handler cards remain unavailable. 3RD REPEAT VIOLATION. Valid certificates for all employees must be
maintained on site at all times. Correct within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Slime-like residue in soda gun at bar. Corrected.
Ice bin at bar near entry unclean. Ensure bins are washed -> rinsed -> sanitized -> air dried daily. Correct prior to next use.
Prep sink observed with standing stagnant water and slime accumulation. Wash -> rinse -> sanitize -> air dry immediately and
between uses.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Warm water unavailable at pre-mixed faucets in both restrooms. Water only reached 74F after being run for
several minutes. A minimum of 100-108F is required at all times. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Three chemical bottles stored on metal prep table above container storing bread in kitchen. Store all chemicals
below/away from food or related equipment at all times. Corrected.
Two cans of Hot Shot insect killer observed in kitchen. These chemicals are not approved for use inside restaurant. Remove from
food related areas today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Mop sink with attached split valve lacks anti-siphon device on end of split attached to hose. 2ND REPEAT
VIOLATION. Install within 3 days.
Slow draining handsink in restroom. Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44b

APPROPRIATE USE OF EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Dishes observed in 1-comp prep sink. Sink is only to be used for food preparation, see $14a. Review with
employees today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not clean their hands in a sink used for food preparation, warewashing, or in a service sink or a
curbed cleaning facility used for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A handwashing facility shall not be used for purposes
other than handwashing. Food preparation sinks, handwashing lavatories, and warewashing equipment shall not be used for the cleaning
of maintenance tools, the preparation or holding of maintenance materials, or the disposal of mop water and similar liquid wastes. Open-air
barbecue or outdoor wood-burning oven shall be operated on the same premises as, in reasonable proximity to, and in conjunction with, a
permanent food facility that is approved for food preparation. (113953.1 (b-d), 114123, 114143 (a,b), Exception 114125)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Restroom doors propped open. Allow doors to self-close at all times.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Torn sheet vinyl flooring near prep unit. Repair if possible within 30 days. New flooring will be required at a later
date by EMD with approved materials. Current flooring is not approved type due to seams throughout.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
