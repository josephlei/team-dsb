Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Pizza (112F) in the hot holding unit, jack cheese chicken taquitos (125F) on the roller were
out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. Pizza
was voluntarily discarded at the time of inspection. Taquitos were reheated on the roller.
MINOR VIOLATION - Steak jack cheese (131F) and jalapeno cream cheese (132F) taquitos on the roller were out of proper holding
temperature. Maintain PHF at proper holding temperature. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Staff was not aware of the proper reheating procedure, proper warewashing procedure and thermometer
calibration process. Education provided during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 1b

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION

OBSERVATIONS: Food safety certificate just expired. Register to an approved class within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee
who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Bag of balloons and spray bottle of cleaner were stored in the back handsink. Cease the practice of storing any
item in the handsink. Such practices may result in a MAJOR VIOLATION. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Handwash stations in both restrooms lacked hot water. Maximum temperature reached was 60F. Adjust
handwash station to dispense water at 100-108F at all times. Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Containers of bagged chips are stored on the storage room floor. Store all food items at least 6" off the floor.
Correct within 24 hours. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 31

CONSUMER SELF-SERVICE OPERATIONS

OBSERVATIONS: Lids in the self serve beverage station was improperly stored. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Raw, non-prepackaged food of animal origin, such as beef, lamb, pork, poultry, and eviscerated fish, shall not be
offered for consumer self-service. Consumer self-service operations for ready-to-eat foods such as buffets and salad bars shall be provided
with a suitable food dispensing utensil for each container displayed or effective dispensing methods that protect the food from
contamination. Except for drinking cups, food employees and consumers shall not be allowed to use soiled tableware, including single-use
articles, to obtain additional food from the display and servicing equipment. Consumer self-service bulk beverages shall be dispensed in
approved manner. (114063 (a,d), 114065, 114075)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Excessive debris is accumulating under the self serve beverage station. Clean floor immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Wet mop is still stored in the mop bucket. Hang mops after use to dry. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 47c

INSPECTION REPORT AVAILABILITY

OBSERVATIONS: Inspection report provide upon request was not the most recent. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A copy of the most recent routine inspection report conducted to assess compliance with this part shall be
maintained at the food facility and made available upon request. The food facility shall post a notice advising consumers that a copy of the
most recent routine inspection report is available for review by any interested party. (113725.1)
