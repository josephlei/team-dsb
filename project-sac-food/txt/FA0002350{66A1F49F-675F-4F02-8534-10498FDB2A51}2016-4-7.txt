# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS:
Observed the following German cockroach activity:
-1 dead nymph on floor next to 2-door cold-top unit and utensil prep table. Body was removed at the time of inspection.
-1 dead nymph on floor under 3 compartment warewashing sink. Body was removed at the time of inspection.
-1 dead nymph on floor behind cold drawers located across from 3-comp sink. Body was removed at the time of inpsection.
-1 dead cockroach in gap between wooden post and bottom of shelving. Remove body within 24 hours.
-fecal focal points observed in multiple areas around gaps between wood located behind cold drawers across from 3-comp sink.
Facility received treatment from pest control company on 3/30/16 and 4/1/16. Document provided by pest control company
indicated that 15 glue traps were placed within in facility - only 1 was observed at the time of inspection. According owner, traps
were placed in hard to reach places. Ensure traps are checked frequently to monitor for any activity. Pest control company also
treated facility with residual pesticides.
Clean all areas (see #33) and seal all gaps, cracks and crevices within facility to prevent vermin harborage. A reinspection may be
conducted to ensure compliance.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards

uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Both restrooms lack ventilation. Recommend installing screens on windows in both restrooms and keep
windows open or provide mechanical ventilation. Correct within 7 days. NOTE: Additional repeat violations may result in
reinspections until compliance is met. <<3RD REPEAT VIOLATION>>
Ventilation hoods lack 6" overhang of equipment. Equipment extends to the edges of hoods. Correct within 21 days. <<REPEAT
VIOLATION>>
NOTE: Facility has purchased a new fryer and electric griddle that are smaller than equipment present at previous inspection which
extended 3" on each side outside of the hoods. Fryer has been replaced already, which increased the hood overhang, but still does
not extend 6" beyond cooking surface. Facility will be replacing the 1-door lowboy freezer that the current griddle stands on, with a
unit or prep table that is smaller so that all equipment can be moved under the hood. Consult Ansul fire suppression company for
any requirements or changes that will need to be made to Ansul system.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient ventilation
to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or light-switch
activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in areas where open
food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with light shields. (114149,
114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)
