Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Rear preparation area hand wash was completely out of paper towels (dispenser open). Keep hand wash stations
fully stocked at all times and fully accessible. Corrected by refilling dispenser at time of inspection.
NOTE: A secondary hand wash station that is shared by main cooks line and rear preparation area was fully stocked with soap and
paper towels.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Measured precooked diced chicken in the center cold top unit (below the hood system) at 41-48F which was filled
above the limit line. Manager voluntarily discarded 4 pre portioned bags at time of inspection and moved the item from a plastic
container to a metal container.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found metal shelves in the walk in cooler which were below the six inch requirement for storage. Store all food
items six inches off the ground.
Found an improperly stored bag of onions with the bottom corner touching the floor. Corrected at time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Dust build up observed on the ceiling right above the HVAC vent (right above the hand wash station in rear area).
Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Condensation leak observed inside the reach in unit of zone 1-main cold top preparation unit. Repair within 30
days.
NOTE: Do not store any food below the condensation leak.
Cutting boards on Zone 3 cold top unit cannot be fully cleaned (cutting marks). Replace within 7 days.
Found chipped plates at the servers area. Replace/remove any damaged plates today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Scoop for chips stored on top of warmer unit. Store scoop in sanitary manner. Correct now.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Unable to locate a monitoring thermometer inside the zone 3 fresh cold top reach in unit. Keep a monitoring
thermometer in the warmest section of the unit at all times. Correct today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Loose roll of toilet tissue stored on handle of larger toilet stall in mens restroom. Keep toilet tissue stored in
dispenser at all times. Correct now.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)
