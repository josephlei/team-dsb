#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Cooked beans in a container (stored 10-12 inches deep) cooling in freezer measured from 74-95F. Practice
proper cooling methods at all times such as utilizing shallow trays no more than 2" inches and/or utilize ice sticks to allow rapid
cooling. Item was made at 12:30pm. Temperature was measured at 2:20pm and item was reportioned and a chill stick was added
for further cooling.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as
an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002,
114002.1)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Areas observed with old food/grease/residue like accumulation:
-door hinges on pizza reach in cooler 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION
-bottom side to shelvings above steam table/cold top cooler lines in cook's line
-wall below entrance to dish machine
Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: 1 of several cold hold drawers in cook's line is damaged. Correct immediately.
Damaged door gasket observed on 2 door stand up cooler near salad station. Correct within 30 days.
Bowl utilized as a scoop in flour container at batter station. Utilize handled scoops and store handles upright. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Sanitizing bucket in cook's line measured below minimum of 200ppm. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Missing air gap between drainage line and rim of floor sink below 1 of 2 hand sinks at bar. Correct within 7 days
to prevent potential backflow of grey/waste water.

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Personal belonging such as jackets/backpack stored on top of packaged single use cups/misc. items in break
room. Relocate below and away from noted items. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers or
other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Damaged flooring in cook's line nearest batter station. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving
shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture
of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1,
114271, 114272)
