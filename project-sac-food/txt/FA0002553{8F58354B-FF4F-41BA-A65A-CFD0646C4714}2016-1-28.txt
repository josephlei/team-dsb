Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 1a

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Staff was not aware of the proper warewashing procedure. Staff was previously following wash, sanitize then
rinse. Proper warewashing requires all dirty equipment/utensils to be washed (with soap), rinsed (with water), sanitized (with
chemical) then air dried. Implement proper practice immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Unapproved bag used to store walnuts. Store all unpackaged food items using approved food grade
bags/containers. Cease the use of the white shirt bags (e.g. "Thank You" plastic bags) to store unpackaged items. Correct
immediately.
Open bags of flours, powders and nuts observed within the facility. Bins below the wooden table were lacking covers to heavily
exposed open bags of flour/powders. Store all open packages of food items in container with tight fitting lids.. Correct within 3
days. REPEAT VIOLATION.
Frozen strudels are stored directly on the wire rack inside the ice machine. Cease this practice immediately. Open packages of
food item must be stored in a manner that is protected from cross contamination. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Two spray bottles of unknown liquid were lacking labels. Provide label to all containers of chemicals. Correct
within 3 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.
Bottle of soap is stored next to a container of a squeeze bottle of caramel in the front area handwash station. Cease this practice
immediately. Store food and food related items (e.g. cups for iced beverages) away from this area immediately. Relocate caramel
and cups immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Many containers are lacking labels. Provide labels to ALL CONTAINERS storing food. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 31

CONSUMER SELF-SERVICE OPERATIONS

OBSERVATIONS: Lids to hot beverages were stored improperly in the front register area. Store these lids faced down to prevent
the inside surface becoming contaminated. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Raw, non-prepackaged food of animal origin, such as beef, lamb, pork, poultry, and eviscerated fish, shall not be
offered for consumer self-service. Consumer self-service operations for ready-to-eat foods such as buffets and salad bars shall be provided
with a suitable food dispensing utensil for each container displayed or effective dispensing methods that protect the food from
contamination. Except for drinking cups, food employees and consumers shall not be allowed to use soiled tableware, including single-use
articles, to obtain additional food from the display and servicing equipment. Consumer self-service bulk beverages shall be dispensed in
approved manner. (114063 (a,d), 114065, 114075)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor along the wall throughout the back area and behind the oven, glazing table and wooden table is in need of
cleaning. Clean the floor free of food spillage, crumbs, spilled flour. Clean within 48 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Foil are used as liners above the oven and under 3-comp sink. Remove this unapproved material immediately.
Cardboards observed between the glazing metal container and wall to the right of the oven. Remove this cardboard immediately.
Upright reach-in refrigerator is in need of cleaning on the lowest shelf. Clean the inside of the lowest shell within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Two wet wiping cloths observed on the counter near the upright rack of surplus donuts in the front area. Store all
wet wiping cloth in sanitizer at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Mop is stored in the mop water. Hang wet mop to allow drying. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located

114281, 114282)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Employee jackets are stored on top of unopened bags of flour. Store all personal items under or away from all
food and food related items at all times. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Gaps are still observed under and above the metal screen door at the rear exit door. Eliminate the gaps to
prevent vermin entry/infestation. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
