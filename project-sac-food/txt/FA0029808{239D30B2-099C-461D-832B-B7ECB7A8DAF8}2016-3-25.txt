# 11

REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR- Observed employee removing corn dogs from oven and about to place them in the self service hot
holding unit (final cooking temperature was measured at 116F). Always cook to a final internal cooking temperature of 135F.
Employee was asked to place corn dogs back in oven and were reheated to an internal temperature of 154F.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Measured sliced tomatoes at the condiment bar at 50F and inside the 5bar reach in unit at 50F. Corrected by
moving sealed container into the walk in cooler and two containers with sliced tomatoes were voluntarily discarded.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Slight residue inside of ice machine. Clean and sanitize today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor sink in cabinet by donuts observed with residue build up including the surrounding floor. Clean and
maintain within 24 hours. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Cardboard being used as a liner for bottled and canned drinks inside the walk in cooler and on the shelves by
restroom. Cease using cardboard today. REPEAT VIOLATION
Ambient air temperature of condiment bar reach in unit was at 50F. Do not place any potentially hazardous foods in unit until it is
able to hold an ambient temperature of 41F or below.
