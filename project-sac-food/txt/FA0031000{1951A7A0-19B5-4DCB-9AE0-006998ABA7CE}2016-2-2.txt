Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee beverage (coffee cup) without lid/straw stored above cold-top unit. Keep employee
beverages below/away from food & food prep surfaces and provide lid/straw. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Cafe has 2 sinks - 1 handwashing sink with soap and paper towel dispensers located directly next to espresso
machine and 1 dump sink located at front counter. Both sinks are located approximately 5 feet from each other. Both sinks have
splash guards on sides.
According to employee, sink next to espresso machine is used to rinse out steamed milk pitchers after an espresso drink has been
made. Soap is taken from dispenser located at this sink and then hands are washed at the other sink.
Observed steamed milk pitcher hang drying on interior side of sink next to espresso machine. Observed employees wash hands in
both sinks. Officially designate 1 of the 2 sinks as a handwashing sink and the other as the dump sink where pitchers will be rinsed
in between warewashing in the kitchen. Relocate soap and paper towel dispensers to the sink designated as the handwashing sink.
Correct within 24 hours.
NOTE: Handwashing sink is to be used for handwashing ONLY.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed fruit cups containing cut melon stored partially in ice located at cafe register. Ensure ice completely
surrounds all food to ensure it stays at 41F/below. Correct immediately.
Observed cut cantaloupe and honeydew melon (43-44F) and shredded cheese (46F) stored in cold-top insert. Cooked potatoes (46F)
stored in cold-top reach-in. Correct immediately. NOTE: Potatoes were voluntarily discarded.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed slime accumulation on metal baffle in upper part of ice machine. Empty ice and wash, rinse, sanitize
within 2 days.

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water at 3 compartment sink in bar measured 105. Provide 120F within 24 hours. NOTE: Bar was not in use
at the time of inspection. Send utensils to kitchen to be washed until repaired.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed 8 fruit/drain flies around mop sink and approximately 20 flies around dish machine. Eliminate
immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Gasket for 1-door dessert freezer located under meat slicer is unclean. Correct within 3 days.
Container holding utensils on rack next to 3 compartment sink is unclean. Clean container and rewash utensils today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed small leak at prep sink and at mop sink faucets. Repair within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Mop stored with head on floor. Hang to dry when not in use. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Missing ceiling tile above door in liquor room. Replace within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)

# 49

PERMIT REQUIREMENTS

OBSERVATIONS: Health permit expired on 1/18/16. Post new permit within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall not be open for business without a valid permit. Permit shall be posted in a conspicuous place
within the food facility. (114067(b, c), 114381, 114387)
