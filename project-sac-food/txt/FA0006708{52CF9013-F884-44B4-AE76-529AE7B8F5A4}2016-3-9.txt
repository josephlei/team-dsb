FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Food handler cards not available for all employees. Provide copies of all food handler cards within 30 days
(REPEAT VIOLATION).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Water bottle inside one door reach in freezer over food used in restaurant. Store all personal beverages below
and away from food used in restaurant. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Debris inside utensil holders on back prep table. Send to dishwasher for cleaning and sanitizing on a regular
basis. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Raw pork over vegetables inside one door stand up refrigerator and three door reach in refrigerator. Raw chicken
over beef inside walk-in refrigerator. Keep all raw food below any cooked or ready to eat food. Store raw meat based on final
cooking temperature. Correct within 24 hours.
Head of cabbage stored directly on refrigerator shelf. Store food inside food grade container. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Boxes of food stored on floor inside walk-in refrigerator. Bags of rice stored on floor in dry storage room near
back door. Keep all food at least six inches above the floor. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Grease and food accumulation on floor under cooking equipment and prep refrigerators. Clean and maintain
within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Utensils stored in stagnant water at 52F. Utensils that are stored in water and come into contact with potentially
hazardous foods must be kept in one of the following ways:
1. hot water at/above 135F
2. ice water at/below 41F
3. Clean, dry and sanitized surface and rotated at least every 4 hours or sooner if needed
4. In a pH adjusted solution (vinegar or lemon). pH indicator strips will need to be provided if using a pH adjusted solution. pH must
be below 4.6
REPEAT VIOLATION
Tongs stored on oven handle. Store on a clean and sanitized surface. Correct within 24 hours.
Cup used as scoop inside rice. Provide a scoop with a handle.
Handles of scoops in contact with sugar, salt, etc under prep table and in dry storage room. Store scoop in a manner to prevent
handle from touching the food. Correct within 2 days.

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS
