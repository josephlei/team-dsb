#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR- REPEAT VIOLATION
The following potentially hazardous foods were not being time tracked:
-chicken patties
-jalapeno poppers
-hash browns
Ensure timers are being set as soon as potentially hazardous food is removed from temperature control (ie. fryer).
Continued non compliance will result in additional re inspections at a fee of $268.00.

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be
marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within
the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was
used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Loose paper towels in mens restroom. Provide paper towels in working dispenser within 24 hours.
NOTE: Employee was working on dispenser at start of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Maximum hot water temperature at ware wash machine after 3 tries stayed at 95F. Ensure hot water of at least
120F is provided during the rinse cycle. Correct within 48 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 22

SEWAGE AND WASTEWATER DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Observed standing waste water from mop sink on floor just outside of rear exit door. All waste water must be
disposed via sanitary sewer. Education provided at time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Liquid waste shall be disposed of through the approved plumbing system that discharges into the public sewerage
or into an approved private sewage disposal system. Grease traps and grease interceptors shall be easily accessible for servicing. (114197,
114201)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Three boxes of raw frozen beef patties stored over a box of croissants inside the walk in freezer. Store raw beef
below any ready to eat foods. Correct now.

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Found multiple broken food containers being used to hold food (broken at tabs). Remove any broken food
containers today.
Gasket on McCall branded reach in freezer is in disrepair. Replace gasket within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Sanitizer concentration at wiping towel container at front area (below soft serve machine) measured at 0ppm quat.
ammonia. Provide sanitizer of 200ppm quat. ammonia at all times. Correct now.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Grout behind cove base is deteriorating (cove base below the easy access doors to walk in cooler- shown to
manager at time of inspection). Repair within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)

# 47c

INSPECTION REPORT AVAILABILITY

OBSERVATIONS: Most recent inspection report not available for review.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A copy of the most recent routine inspection report conducted to assess compliance with this part shall be
maintained at the food facility and made available upon request. The food facility shall post a notice advising consumers that a copy of the
most recent routine inspection report is available for review by any interested party. (113725.1)
