Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Blenders are being stored adjacent to hand washing sink. Either provide greater physical distance (minimum of 1
foot) between hand washing sink and where blenders are stored or install a splash shield to prevent contamination to blended
drinks when hands are washed. Correct within one week.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: Time log observed for milk but not for half and half (which are stored on ice in bucket on counter). Half and half at
56 F. According to owner, half and half was removed from temperature control 45 minutes ago. Discard time written on half and half
container. Ensure that discard time is logged for foods being held out of temperature control and that foods items are discarded
within four hours.
Note: Grab and go sandwiches were not being held at room temperature (they were in refrigerated display case). Discussed with
owner about logging discard time if sandwiches are removed from temperature control.

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be
marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within
the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was
used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Food debris on shelf in iced coffee refrigerator. Clean and maintain within 2 days.
Floor sink under front counter has mold build-up. Clean and maintain within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: A sanitizer bucket is being used as shield in floor sink for dishmachine and blocks air gap. Remove bucket today
and do not block air gap. Reposition drain pipes to floor sink if needed.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44a

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Both office and mop storage room are disorganized and have unused equipment. Clean and maintain within one
week. Remove any unused equipment.
