#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: White noodles at 76F, boba balls at 74F at prep table in kitchen. Corrected during inspection by moving to
refrigerator.
One egg rolls at 75F at prep table next to rice cooker.
Chicken at 52F under water at prep sink, according to manager they were washing it at the beginning of the inspection, it was sitting
there 50 minutes later. Moved to refrigerator during inspection.
<REPEAT VIOLATION>

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Food handler cards not available for employees. Provide within 2 weeks and maintain copy of food handler cards
for all employees at facility.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Employee didn't wash hands properly, he put soap and rinsed his hands without making foam to clean hands.
Wash hands correctly.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Old food debris build up on meat slicer. Clean (wash, rinse and sanitize) before using.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Chicken broth thawing improperly in the bucket on prep table near the freezer, it was still frozen. Correct
immediately.
<REPEAT VIOLATION>

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Raw chicken stored above and along with uncooked beef and pork in reachin freezer. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: 2 Glass door refrigerators are approved only for prepackaged food items but are in use to store non prepackaged
food items. Replace the units with ANSI or NSF approved unit or move the items to other approved unit within 30 days.
Facility had one unit that was observed at the time of last inspection but one unit (single door coca cola unit) is brought after the
last inspection. Remove this unit within 2 weeks.
Plastic container is placed under ice machine to collect water, which may be leaking. Repair if needed within 7 days.
Vinyl sheet used as liner on the prep tables, it's folding/raised high at the sides/edges and deteriorating. Remove and replace with
approved liner within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: -Gap between the walls in corner behind the prep sink and at wall behind the prep refrigerator.
-Hole in wall under the prep sink.
Seal gaps and holes within 2 weeks.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
