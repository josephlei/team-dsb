#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Container with fried egg rolls 51F-52F inside walk-in cooler. Per employee, egg rolls were sitting out next to
cook's line for some time before being placed inside walk-in cooler.
- Fried pieces of chicken and chicken wings 118F-120F and rice 117F-132F held on wok. Per one employee, they are cooking these
foods while employee was working with raw shrimp. Another employee noted that these food items were cooked about 20 minutes
ago.
Do not store food items out without temperature control. Cook/fry foods only when needed since there is no hot holding unit located
inside prep line. Maintain all hot food items at/above 135F and cold food items at/below 41F. Do not store food items on the counter
top without temperature control. Bring out on a needed bases and store rest in refrigerator unit. REPEAT VIOLATION
Minor Violation: Fried chicken 43F, bbq pork 43F, bean sprouts 45F, raw chicken 44F, raw beef 43F, wontons 43F, cooked pasta 43F,
chow mein noodles 43F, and cooked chicken 43F inside walk-in cooler. Ambient temperature inside walk-in cooler was 41F.
Employee lowered thermostat of walk-in cooler to lower setting during inspection and temperature dropped to 38F within 15 minutes.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: Observed inconsistent use of TPHC for food items stored at self-service buffet line:
- At time of arrival, operator noted that all foods are logged on TPHC once new batch is placed on buffet line.
- Employee fried chicken and added them to fried chicken that was stored at buffet area (log was not updated).
- Employee fried teriyaki chicken and placed on buffet line without logging new batch of food item on the log.
- Employee placed noodles on buffet line and logged food item on the log.
Discussed TPHC with operator to ensure they use it properly and have consistent procedures so all employee can follow them
properly. Operator notes that every batch will be logged as soon as it is placed on buffet line. Four hours have not been expired for
any food item at buffet line. Corrected. Provide written procedure to EMD (916) 875-8513 ATTN: Elena within 7 days and post copy so
it is visible to all employees.

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be
marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within
the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was
used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: - Open foods stored inside walk-in cooler, reach-in cooler and next to cook's line. Keep food items covered to
prevent from possible contamination. Corrected. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Found containers with vegetables nesting on top of other vegetables at cook's line. Also, container with fried shrimp nesting on
top of another container with fried shrimp at walk-in cooler. Cease practice immediately. Corrected. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 31

CONSUMER SELF-SERVICE OPERATIONS

OBSERVATIONS: Open bowl with hot sauce was stored at customer self-service area. Cover was provided during inspection.
REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Raw, non-prepackaged food of animal origin, such as beef, lamb, pork, poultry, and eviscerated fish, shall not be
offered for consumer self-service. Consumer self-service operations for ready-to-eat foods such as buffets and salad bars shall be provided
with a suitable food dispensing utensil for each container displayed or effective dispensing methods that protect the food from
contamination. Except for drinking cups, food employees and consumers shall not be allowed to use soiled tableware, including single-use
articles, to obtain additional food from the display and servicing equipment. Consumer self-service bulk beverages shall be dispensed in
approved manner. (114063 (a,d), 114065, 114075)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: - Greasy and sticky door handles, doors, light switches, and faucet handles at employee restroom.
- Old food/sauces build up on the floor inside walk-in cooler.
- Grease residue build up on hood filters.
Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: - Broken light shatter proof guards above reach-in cooler, and dish machine area. Some light bulbs were found
burnt. Replace light bulbs and guards within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Drain pipe from soda dispenser nesting inside floor sink. Provide at least 1 inch air gap within 7 days.

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Toilet paper stored next to empty toilet paper dispenser inside employee restroom. Store toilet paper in dispenser
at all times. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: - Popcorn ceiling observed above server's water station and above foods at buffet line. Paint ceiling with
washable paint to ensure it is smooth and can be easily cleaned or provide approved ceiling.
- Deteriorated ceiling observed inside side dry storage room and above dish machine.
- Holes/gaps observed on ceiling above dirty side of dish machine area and near cove base inside walk-in cooler. Patch/fill with
approved material.
Repair within 30 days.
NOTE: Ensure al flooring beneath cook's line is smooth and easily washable.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
