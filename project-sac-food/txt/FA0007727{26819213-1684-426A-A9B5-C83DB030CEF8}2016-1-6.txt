#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Crepe mixture 48F inside ice bath. Ensure level of ice is same as level of food mixture. Maintain at/below 41F at all
times. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: Improper use of time as a public health control (TPHC): At 9:55am found TPHC log that stated potatoes were
placed at cook's line at 10am. During conversation with employee it was determined that food was placed out at 9:45am. Food items
must be labeled with time it was removed from temperature control. Food must be promptly discarded at 4th hour. Failure to comply
may result in further enforcement action. Corrected during inspection by writing correct time.

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be
marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within
the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was
used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: - Open containers with food items stored inside several reach-in coolers at cook's line. Keep food items covered
during storage. Correct today.
- Container with wet tomatoes was stored on drain board next to handwash sink. Do not store any food or equipment next to
handwash sink to prevent it from possible contamination. Use all equipment properly as designated. Corrected.
- Tubes observed in direct contact with ice inside front ice bin. Keep food like consumable ice separated from tubes to prevent from
possible contamination. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found packaged cheese temporary stored on the floor while unloading inside kitchen area. All food and food
related equipment must be stored at least 6 inches off the floor. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))
