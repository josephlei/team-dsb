Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Cooked chicken stored inside cold-top unit #1 insert located nearest to front handwashing sink measured 48F.
Container was overfilled and 1 insert was missing from the cold-top, allowing cold air to escape. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed heavy food debris accumulation on tooth of can opener. Clean immediately.
Observed pink/yellow mold-like slime on corner of ice machine deflector plate. Empty ice and wash, rinse, sanitize machine before
refilling. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS:
Observed the following areas unclean:
-water from condensation observed on bottom of front counter display reach-in units. Clean standing water today and repair as
needed.
-standing water on bottom of cold-top unit #2 nearest to espresso station. Clean today and repair unit as needed.
-food debris accumulation on bottom shelf of area where crutons are portioned. Clean within 3 days.
-heavy dust accumulation on HVAC air vent above soda syrup storage in warewashing area. Clean within 3 days. <<REPEAT
VIOLATION>>
-black grime under shelves and equipment in back kitchen. Move equipment and clean underneath within 7 days.
-heavy black grime accumulation inside mop sink. Clean within 7 days.
-sludge-like build-up in hard to reach corner under dish machine and chemical bottles. Clean within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Paper cup without a handle used as scoop and stored inside container of uncooked oatmeal behind front counter.
Provide a scoop with a handle within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Ventilation hoods above catering cold-top prep area are nonfunctional / not installed. Lighting is very low. Repair
/ replace lights within 7 days. <<REPEAT VIOLATION>>

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Sanitizer in bucket stored next to front counter handwashing sink measured less than 100 ppm QA. Maintain at a
minimum of 200 ppm at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Mop stored head down in mop sink. Hang to dry when not in use.
Mop heads are heavily soiled / dark grey. Change mop heads or clean today. Recommend placing mop heads in a bleach / water
solution to soak overnight to clean on a routine basis.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)
