#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS:
MAJOR VIOLATION
Observed packages of lobster meat (62F) and garlic bur blanc (42-51F) stored in container on top of an ice bath unattended on prep
table across from seafood walk-in cooler. Items were relocated to walk-in cooler. Keep ice filled to the level of product to keep
cold. Corrected.
MINOR VIOLATION
Cut tomatoes (45F) stored in salad station cold-top insert and shredded cheese (44F) stored in salad station cold-top reach-in were
out of proper holding temperatures. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Cooked lacked knowledge of proper cooking temperature of fish. Employee stated that salmon should be
cooked to 125-130F. Education was provide that all fish must be cooked to a minimum internal temperature of 145F., continue to
train employees. NOTE: Final temperature of salmon measured 147F.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Server set down large serving tray directly on top of handwashing sink while talking to adjacent table of
customers. Tray was easily moved. Keep handwashing sinks accessible at all times. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas.
Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and
sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953,
113953.1, 113953.2)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed black and pink mold-like build-up on chute of ice machine. Empty ice and wash, rinse, sanitize and air
dry within 3 days. <<REPEAT VIOLATION>>

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use
with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact
surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

OBSERVATIONS: Observed standing water inside dry food storage area (corrected) and under bar soda lines (correct today).

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed torn gasket in lowboy reach-in located across from fryers and char grill. Replace within 14 days.
<<REPEAT VIOLATION>>

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Handwashing sinks in both men's and women's restroom are slow to drain. Correct within 3 days.
Drain for 3 compartment sink is not lined up over drain grate hole so water splashes onto floor when drained. Correct within 7
days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Both dumpster lids left open. It appears they have been left open by hauler when dumpsters were emptied.
Recommend contacting hauler to correct in the future. Close dumpster lids within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be kept
in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at all
times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and other
conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed broken tiles and missing grout inside walk-in freezer. Repair within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving
shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture
of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1,
114271, 114272)
