#9

COOLING METHODS

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: Pozole and enchilada sauce (at 44 to 45 F) cooked yesterday were found being improperly cooled in closed
containers stacked one on top of the other inside the walk-in refrigerator. Manager voluntarily discarded the pozole and enchilada
sauce during the inspection (see VC&D below).
- REPEAT VIOLATION Note: If another major cooling violation is observed again on the next inspection, a mandatory charged reinspection shall be
conducted.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Shredded cheese, diced tomatoes and guacamole were in inserts on the front counter make line cold top were
measured at 45 to 49 F using a thermocouple. Ambient temperatures inside the unit (front part) were at 44 to 45 F. Note that the
food items in inserts on the back row of the unit (including garbanzos, facility-made chipotle ranch dressing, Mexican cheese) were
at 41 F. It was recommended that inserts be covered with lids when not in use and to not overfill them.
- REPEAT VIOLATION - The above refrigeration unit shall be serviced/repaired to enable them to keep contents at 41 F or below at all times. Fax proof of
correction (e.g., receipt, invoice, work order, photographs) to EMD [Attn: Bo Faustino] at (916) 875-8513, or email to
faustinob@saccounty.net, within 3 days to avoid a charged reinspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Two spray bottles with unidentified liquid contents found on chemical storage rack near warewash area. Label all
chemicals and bottles. Correct today.
- REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Small condiment cup was being used as scoop inside the walk-in refrigerator. Provide approved scoop with a
handle within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Container for kitchen tools and utensils were unclean (shown to manager). Wash and sanitize container and
contents today. Corrected during the inspection. Clean regularly.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: No available probe thermometer for measuring internal food temperatures in the facility. Provide a unit that can
measure from 0F to 220F within 2 days. Use regularly to monitor food temperatures to ensure compliance with Health Code
requirements on temperature holding for potentially hazardous foods. Operator provided an infra-red thermometer since the last
inspection. This only measures surface temperatures and the Code requires a probe thermometer.
Two monitoring thermometers in front make line cold top were found at the rear of unit and was behind stored food items. Code
requires monitoring thermometers for refrigeration units to be located at the warmest parts of the units and where they are easily
readable. Corrected during the inspection.

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Soiled wiping cloths with no sanitizer found on counter tops. Multi-use wiping cloths used to wipe service
counters or other surfaces that may come in contact with food or food contact items shall be kept in clean water with sanitizer.
Corrected during the inspection.
- REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Shared garbage dumpster outside was found with lids open. Work with co-tenants and/or landlord to always keep
lids closed when not being filled to prevent vermin infestation and possible dirty water (leachate) run-off to storm water drain.
Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Ceiling panel is missing above the rear ventilation hood. Correct within 7 days.
- REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
