#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Shredded cheddar cheese 47F-52F stored at cold top cooler. Shredded cheese 53F-56F, sliced tomatoes 52F,
shredded mozzarella cheese 57F stored inside cold drawers beneath cold top cooler. Food was voluntarily discarded during
inspection. Ambient temperature of this cooler was 50F (see violation #35).
- Cream cheese 60F stored at server's area without time and temperature control. Voluntarily discarded during inspection.
Minor Violation
- The following food items stored between 47F-49F at cold top cooler: sliced tomatoes, diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, melon fruit
cup, sausages, ground meat, and diced ham. Food was submerged in ice bath.
- The following food items stored between 47F-49F at cold drawers beneath cold top cooler: tuna, melon fruit, blue cheese crumbles,
sliced turkey, and egg whites. Food was relocated inside walk-in cooler.
- Pancake batter 48F stored inside Everest cooler. Ambient temperature of this cooler was 40F.
- Pancake batter 43F stored inside walk-in cooler. Ambient temperature of this cooler was 41F.
- Potato salad 49F stored inside Ascend reach-in cooler. Ambient temperature was 50F.
All potentially hazardous foods must be at/below 41F or at/above 135F to prevent bacterial growth.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Found several open cups with soda stored on top of reach-in cooler and next to steam table. All employee
beverages must be stored in a cup with lid and straw and located below food prep areas. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee brake raw shelled egg and then handle spatula without first washing hands. Employees must
wash their hands when contaminated prior to preparing food. Note: No direct hand to food contact observed at time of inspection.
Education and handwashing handout was provided. Employee washed hands properly during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

# 19

CONSUMER ADVISORY FOR UNDERCOOKED FOOD

OBSERVATIONS: Consumer advisory is not provided for pouched eggs. Provide verbal consumer advisory immediately. Consumer
advisory handout provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Undercooked or raw food may be served to the comsumer if either of the following conditions are met: the consumer
specifically orders the food prepared raw or less than throughly cooked, or the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the
time of ordering, that the food is raw or less than throughly cooked. Confectionery food containing more than 0.5% alcohol may be served to
the consumer if the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the time of ordering, that it contains more than 0.5% alcohol.
(114012, 114093)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: - Raw beef patties stored above fruit inside walk-in freezer.
- Raw shelled eggs stored above produce inside walk-in cooler.
Store all food properly as shown on proper food storage sticker. Provided. Correct immediately.
- Open food items stored inside Everest reach-in freezer. Keep food items covered during storage. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Boxes with potatoes stored on the floor inside walk-in freezer. Keep at least 6 inches above floor. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Unlabeled bulk food containers stored at dry storage room. Label today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Drain pipe beneath reach-in cooler at coffee prep area nesting inside floor sink. Provide at least 1 inch air gap
within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44b

OBSERVATIONS: - Unused steam table is cluttered inside dry storage room.
- Found multiple unused equipment (from old owner) stored outside behind rear door (below overhead protection).
. Food code requires that the premises shall be free of litter and items that are unnecessary to the operation and maintenance of the
facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. Remove all unnecessary equipment/clutter, organize and clean
affected areas to deny vermin of possible harborage. Correct within two months.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not clean their hands in a sink used for food preparation, warewashing, or in a service sink or a
curbed cleaning facility used for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A handwashing facility shall not be used for purposes
other than handwashing. Food preparation sinks, handwashing lavatories, and warewashing equipment shall not be used for the cleaning
of maintenance tools, the preparation or holding of maintenance materials, or the disposal of mop water and similar liquid wastes. Open-air
barbecue or outdoor wood-burning oven shall be operated on the same premises as, in reasonable proximity to, and in conjunction with, a
permanent food facility that is approved for food preparation. (113953.1 (b-d), 114123, 114143 (a,b), Exception 114125)
