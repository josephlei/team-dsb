#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: Both hand wash sinks in the kitchen were totally blocked. Hand wash sink (corner) at cook's area was blocked by
carts with meat slicer and toaster; sink near the dishwasher blocked by a huge plastic container with glasses. Keep hand wash
sinks accessible at all times. Corrected during inspection. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Screw cap (bottled water) of employee stored next to mixer at cook's line. Provide drinking containers with lid and
straw. Store employee beverage away from food prep area. Correct today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: - Pink slime and dirt at ice chute of ice machine. Clean and sanitize ice machine today.
- Juice nozzle was stained. Replace nozzle if it's hard to clean. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Lack of hot water in one of the employee restrooms at the hallway. Provide a minimum of 100 F and a maximum of
108 F when water temperature is not readily adjustable. Correct within 24 hours. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Bag of bread mix was left open after use. Corrected during inspection.

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: - Unclean floors beneath the ice machine. Clean floors today.
- Mold like dirt on metal sheet wall beneath drain board at dishwashing area. Clean within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Testing material to measure temperature at plate level during the final rinse of the high heat dishwasher not
available during inspection. Provide lollipop thermometer or thermolabel. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Dust build-up on fan guard of the standing fan at cook's line. Clean within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Blue digital probe thermometers (2) were not accurate. Thermometers were replaced during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Lids of all garbage dumpsters (4) were partially open. Fully close lids to prevent vermin harborage. Corrected
during inspection.
