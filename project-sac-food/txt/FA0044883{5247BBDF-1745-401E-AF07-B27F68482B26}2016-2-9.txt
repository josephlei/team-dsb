Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Missing about 9 employees food handler cards. Obtain within 24 hours. Repeat Violation.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Liquid eggs in ice bath measured at 46 F. Open cartons of milk in server reach-in cooler measured at 45 F.
Maintain at 41 F and below.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: Chicken on time as public health control on cooks line. Chicken stored at room temperature. Cook stated
sometimes after 2 hours chicken will be relocated into walk-in freezer to cool for later use. Once products are placed on time as
public health control then that products must be discarded within 4 hour time frame. Education provided during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be
marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within
the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was
used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found open bags of flour, rice, sugar, grits in back prep area and food storage rack. Provide container with tight
fitting lids and label or ensure product packaging is tied to prevent contamination. Correct today. 2nd NOTICE.
Raw chicken over raw fish in drawer cooler across stove. Ground poultry over steak in drawer cooler under hood. Education
provided during inspection. Refer to food storage sticker on walk-in cooler door.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Bleach stored on shelf next to flour in back prep area. Bleach was relocated during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Condensation found on bottom shelf of server prep cooler across handwash sink in kitchen. Remove
condensation today, protect food from contamination. Repair unit within 7 days. 2nd NOTICE.
Couple gaskets to drawer cooler under hood are deteriorated. Ambient air temperature below 41 F. Correct within 30 days.
Unsealed wood used as shelf under prep table near mop sink. Provide smooth, non-absorbent, durable, and easily cleanable
material within 2 weeks.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Tong stored on oven handle. Store tongs in sanitary manner. Correct immediately.
Clean equipment stored in unclean bus tubs on bottom shelf of prep table behind dishwasher area. Clean today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Couple sanitizer buckets measured at 0 ppm chlorine. Maintain min. 100 ppm chlorine. Corrected during
inspection. 2nd NOTICE

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Drain pipes extends into floor sink near ice machine. Provide air gap min. 1 inch from drain pipe to rim of floor
sink within 3 days. 2nd NOTICE.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Community shared dumpster lids found open. Grease buildup not on floor around tallow bin. Clean floor in
enclosure and keep lids closed. Repeat Violation.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Personal items (laptop, purses) stored on top of clean plates on rack across ice machine and on top of ice
machine. Clean and sanitize plates personal items were placed on immediately. Store personal items in designated area below and
away from food and food related equipment.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)
