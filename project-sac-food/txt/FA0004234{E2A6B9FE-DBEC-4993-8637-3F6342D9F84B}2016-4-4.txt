#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Liquid eggs (45F) inside the refrigeration unit near the office was out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all
potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open bag of M&Ms in a box on the shelf right of the ice machine. Store all open packages of food items in
container with a tight fitting lid. Correct immediately.
Containers of shredded cheese, jalapenos and other food items stored in clear plastic lids were stored on top of each other. Cease
the practice of nesting containers of food without a barrier in between the containers. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Two spray bottles of "sanitizer" on rack near the coffee area near the handwash station were of different colored
liquids, neither of which were measurable sanitizer. Provide accurate label to containers with chemicals. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor under cooking and refrigeration equipment is in need of thorough cleaning (due to murky water or food
debris/grease accumulation) especially under the french fry station, under the freezer to the left of the flat grill. Clean floor
immediately.
Coffee spillage observed under the coffee maker across the cash registers. Roll of paper towel had absorbed some of the spillage.
Clean this area immediately. Discard roll of paper towel immediately. Spillage of orange smoothie(?) inside the glass case display
refrigerator ito the left of the cash registers. Cleaned during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Hood filters for the deep fryers were being cleaned at the time of inspection. Filters must be in place at all times
when fryers are turned on. Correct immediately to prevent fire hazard.
Reach-in cooler storing flats of raw shell eggs and liquid eggs on the line is lacking a shatter proof light guard on the light fixture.
Provide shatter proof light guard within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Bucket of sanitizer in the coffee area/flat grill area was observed at 0ppm chlorine. Maintain sanitizer at least
100ppm chlorine or 200ppm quat ammonia at all times. Solution was changed at the time of inspection.
Wet wiping cloths were stored on the counter near the coffee station and the wrapped food item hot holding unit. Store all wet
wiping cloths in sanitizer at all times, when not in use. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: One mop was stored on the floor and another was stored in the mop bucket. Hang all wet mops after use to allow
drying. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)
