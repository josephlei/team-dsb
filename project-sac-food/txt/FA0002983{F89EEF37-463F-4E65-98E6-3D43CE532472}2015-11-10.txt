#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Sprout stored on the counter measured at 55F, noodle (57F), tofu (54F), and another container of noodle (72F). All
food items were brought out of refrigerator less than 2 hours before according to employees. Sprout was transfered to ice bath
during inspection and the rest of food items relocated to refrigeration unit.
MINOR VIOLATION:
Raw chicken inside cold top insert measured at 45F and beef at 47F. Both items were stored above food line. Keep at/below food
line. Corrected.
All potentially hazardous foods must be kept at/below 41F or at/above 135F at all times.
NOTE: SEVERAL FOOD ITEMS THAT ARE ON THE MENU WERE STORED ON THE COUNTER AT ROOM TEMPERATURE AT
VARIOUS LOCATIONS BUT CLAIMED TO BE FOR EMPLOYEE USE ONLY. FOOD ITEMS THAT ARE ON THE MENU CANNOT BE
CLAIMED FOR PERSONAL ONLY. KEEP PHF'S REFRIGERATED/ IN HOT HOLDING UNITS AT ALL TIMES.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Several knives stored inside bucket (clean knives container) on prep table are not washed and accumulated with
food debris. Wash, rinse, and sanitize immediately.
Bulk food containers on dry storage shelf are accumulated with grease and in need of proper cleaning. Wash, rinse, and sanitize
inside 3 compartment sink. Correct today.
Interior of ice machine accumulated with black residue. Remove ice, wash, rinse, and sanitize within one day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed unapproved grocery plastic bags used to store frozen foods. Use clear food grade plastics to store
foods. Correct within one day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: A bucket of food product stored on the floor inside kitchen. Store all food products at least six inches above the
floor. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Bulk food containers stored on dry storage shelf are unlabeled. Label within two days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Heavy dust accumulation on ceilings and grease/ food debris on storage shelves observed inside 3 door (glass
door) upright refrigeration unit. Remove foods from unit, clean, and sanitize within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Scoop handle is in contact with sugar inside large bulk container. Store handle away from food item. Corrected.
A bowl is used as a scoop inside flour container. Replace with scoop with handle. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)
