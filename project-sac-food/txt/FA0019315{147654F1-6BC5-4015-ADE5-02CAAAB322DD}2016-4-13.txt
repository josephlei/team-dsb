#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: A large pot of dipping marinade used for dipping the whole chicken was on the turned-off stove and measured at
102F. No cooling intervention was being used at the time. Corrected; item was turned off ~30-60 minutes before and an ice paddle
was placed in the pot.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: BBQ pork (146F) that was finished cooking earlier was held inside the turned-off oven without temperature

temperatures. Use of time as a public health control (TPHC) may also be used when food is removed from temperature control.

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: One dead German cockroach was inside the unused oven. Corrected. Note: Pest control service's last visit was
on 3/10/16.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Floor tile grout lines are deteriorated and low below the wok line. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving
