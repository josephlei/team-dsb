Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee wash hands for less than 10 seconds. Ensure proper hand washing is done at all times.
Correct now.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Ware wash area paper towel dispenser is empty. Provide paper towels now.
NOTE: A hand wash sink is located within reasonable distance from ware wash area and pizza preparation area.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Quat. ammonia dispenser observed releasing a concentration of less than 100ppm but more than 0 ppm. Adjust
dispenser to ensure 200ppm quat. ammonia are dispensed. Correct within 24 hours. Quat. ammonia should be added manually to
any dish/food contact surface is washed.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hand nozzle on pizza preparation area hand wash station is malfunctioning causing a lack of warm water. Replace
nozzle and provide warm water between 100-108F within 48 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found a bag on container inside walk in cooler and a bag of dustin flower in dough prep. area stored on floor.
Store all food and food related items six inches off the floor. Correct now.

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Observed flour build up on the cove of the floor in the dough prep. area after an employee had finished cleaning.
Ensure floors are fully cleaned after every use. Correct now.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Loose C02 containers by rear area computer. Secure all pressurized cylinders today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Unable to locate a measuring thermometer inside the smaller cold top reach in refrigerator. Provide a thermometer
that reads between 0-220F today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Sanitize concentration at two wiping towel containers measured at 150ppm quat. ammonia. Provide sanitizer of
200ppm quat. ammonia now.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Water leak observed below the 2 compartment sink. Repair within 14 days.

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Dumpster lids observed open. Keep closed when not in use.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Rear door lacks self closing device. Provide within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: A section of vinyl flooring is deteriorated around the floor sink below the ware wash sink and around the mop
area. Repair within 24 days. 3RD REPEAT VIOLATION. Send proof correction (via email to: estradam2@saccounty.net) prior to the
24 days or a re inspection will be conducted at a fee of $268.00.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)

# 47c

INSPECTION REPORT AVAILABILITY

OBSERVATIONS: Most recent inspection report not available. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A copy of the most recent routine inspection report conducted to assess compliance with this part shall be
maintained at the food facility and made available upon request. The food facility shall post a notice advising consumers that a copy of the
most recent routine inspection report is available for review by any interested party. (113725.1)
