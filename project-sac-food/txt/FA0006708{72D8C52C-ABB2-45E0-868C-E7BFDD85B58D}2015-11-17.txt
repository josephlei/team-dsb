#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: -Flat noodles at 74F on top of prep refrigerator. Spring rolls on top of prep refrigerator closest to fryer at 66F.
Items were not under temperature control. Time stamps or logs were not available. These items are considered potentially
hazardous foods and must either be kept cold at/below 41F or hot at/above 135F. If facility wants to keep these items at room
temperature, the time the food was prepped or removed from temperature control must be documented along with the discard time
which is 4 hours after it is removed from temperature control. Logs and education was provided.
-Cut tomato at 68F in prep refrigerator. Tomato was past the top of the insert. Keep food height below 3/4 full inside insert.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Food handler cards missing or have expired. Provide copies of food handler cards for all employees. Correct
within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Employee observed washing bowls used to prep dishes going to wok in designated hand sink. Soap and water
was used. Employee were asked to sanitize dishes. All dishes must be washed, rinsed and sanitized at three compartment sink or
dishwasher. Do not use hand sink as a dishwashing sink. Education provided. Owner stated he will also put a sign over sign
reminding employees to not use this sink for dishwashing.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

and/or bags. Correct within 3 days.
-Raw meat stored above vegetables inside walk-in refrigerator. Store all raw meat below and away from vegetables and ready to eat
food. Correct within 24 hours.

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Vegetable boxes stored on floor of walk-in refrigerator. Bags of onions stored on cardboard in dry storage room.
Store all food at least six inches above the floor. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Grease and food accumulation on floor under cook's line. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
-Debris on floor in dry storage room. Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Old food cans used to store utensils and knives on back prep table. Replace with containers that can be washed
and sanitized in the dishwasher.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE
