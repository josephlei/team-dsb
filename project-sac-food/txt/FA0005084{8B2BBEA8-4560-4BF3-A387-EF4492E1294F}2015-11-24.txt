#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Tofu in condiment tray for miso soup was measured at 53 F using a thermocouple. Tofu was found inside the prep
cold top during reinspection. Ambient temperature inside the unit was at 36 F. Manager voluntarily discarded the tofu (< 1/2 lb)
during the inspection. Do not leave potentially hazardous food (PHF) items out of temperature holding longer than necessary.
- REPEAT VIOLATION - A mandatory charged reinspection shall be conducted if this violation is observed again during future inspections.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Food handler cards of two food workers were expired. Correct within 25 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

# 16

SHELLSTOCK IDENTIFICATION TAGS

OBSERVATIONS: Shellfish tag for oysters found inside the walk-in refrigerator was found on a shelf outside the unit. Keep tags with
the original shellfish bag until completely used up. Corrected. Handout on shellfish tag requirements was provided to the operator.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Shellstock shall be obtained in containers that bear legible source identification tags. Shellstock tags shall remain
attached to the container that they are received in until the container is empty. Shellstock tags shall be maintained at the food facility in
chronological order correlating to the sale date for 90 calendar days from the date of harvest. The source of the shellstock on display shall
be identified. Shellstock that are portioned or prepackaged shall have a copy of the corresponding shellstock tag. (114039, 114039.1,
114039.3, 114039.4)

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Food containers, including several squeeze bottles containing various sauces and oils, were not labeled. Correct
during the reinspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Dust accumulation observed on ceiling, lights and wall fan above the cook's line. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
