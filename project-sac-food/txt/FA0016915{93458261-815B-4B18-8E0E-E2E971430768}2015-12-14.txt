Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Coffee beans dumped in hand sink have made it slow to drain during hand washing. Remove coffee beans so
water from hand washing can drain rapidly. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: -Dust accumulation on fan guards inside one door refrigerator holding sandwiches and milk under espresso
machine. Clean and maintain within 3 days.
-Buildup observed on side of one door refrigerators under espresso machine. Buildup on walls and floor under espresso machine.
Buildup on floor under three door stand up refrigerator, ice machine and freezer. Clean and maintain within 3 days.
-Accumulation on shelving inside cabinet and garbage areas. Clean and maintain within 3 days.
-Milk accumulation on bottom of three door stand up refrigerator. Clean and maintain within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Thermometers missing in refrigerator under espresso machine and drive through. One of the thermometers is
broken in one door refrigerator under espresso machine. New thermometers have been ordered. Place upon receipt.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Mops stored face down in mop basin. Store on mop rack where the mop heads don't come into contact with the
basin or walls to allow for proper air drying. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)
