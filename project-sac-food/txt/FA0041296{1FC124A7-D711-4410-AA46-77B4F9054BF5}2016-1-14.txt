Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Found the following potentially hazardous foods (PHFs) at improper holding temperatures:
- Measured tempura batter used for dipping raw meat (60f) on shelf across grill.
- Four raw shelled eggs (55f) inside of bowl on shelf across grill.
- Pan of shrimp (54f) inside of upright Ascend refrigeration unit located across from two compartment sink.
Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHFs) at/below 41f or at/above 135f at all times. Corrected on site, as tempura batter used
for dipping raw meats and four raw shell eggs were voluntarily discarded into waste bin by manager. (SEE VC&D IN NOTES) The pan
of shrimp was allowed to be relocated into walk in refrigeration unit.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: One employee missing food handler card. Correct within 30 days. Handout and education provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Uncovered employee beverage next to cutting board and prep cooler in sushi area. Provide a straw and lid for all
employee drinks and place drink on bottom shelf. Corrected on site.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: One covered pan of cooked rice (84f) was found to be improperly cooling inside of prep line reach in refrigeration
unit located across from wok line. Do not cover food item when in the process of cooling any potentially hazardous foods (PHFs).
Corrected on site, education provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed black residue inside ice dispenser shoot of ice machine located next to back door. Properly wash,
rinse, sanitize and air dry within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: - One unlabeled container of salt inside of dry storage room. Correct within 24 hours.
- One unlabeled container of seasoning on shelf above prep line. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: - Cabinets need to be clean beneath sushi bar. Clean within 24 hours.
- Gaskets in need of cleaning below prep cooler doors at front of facility. Clean within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: - Measured internal ambient temperature inside of upright Ascend refrigeration unit to be at 53f. Corrected on site,
as cold dial was turned up to attain a 40f internal temperature reading.
- Condensation on inside of reach in refrigeration against wall in sushi bar. Clean within 24 hours.
- Leaky faucet at hand wash sink next to soup station. Repair within 3 days.
- Several leaks below sink as well as the dishwasher unit adjacent to it. Repair within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Measured <100 ppm chlorine sanitizer concentration inside of sanitizer bucket located near cook's line. Corrected
on site.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Moldy found inside of floor sink located below wait station located near Coke station at front of restaurant. Clean
within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: - Unclean moldy water beneath dishwasher unit. Clean within 24 hours. Repeat violation.
- Moldy corners of cove base tiles beneath dishwasher unit. Clean within 24 hours.
- Two broken cove base tiles beneath two compartment sink at sushi prep area. Correct within 1 week.
- Food accumulation on floor behind ice machine next to back door. Clean within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)

CODE DESCRIPTION: An enforcement officer may impound food, equipment or utensils that are found to be, or suspected of being,
unsanitary or in such disrepair that food, equipment, or utensils may become contaminated or adulterated. An enforcement officer may
inspect and impound any utensil that is suspected of releasing lead or cadmium into the food. (114393)
