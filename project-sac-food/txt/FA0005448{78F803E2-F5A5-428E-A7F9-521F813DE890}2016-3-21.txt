Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

# 11

REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

OBSERVATIONS: Reheating of salsa containing cooked onions and garlic observed at steam table. Per employee, cold salsa from
the cooler was placed in the steam table 30 minutes prior. Corrected by reheating to 165F on the stove top. Education provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Employee lacks knowledge of proper ware washing and reheating procedures. Correct within 24 hours. Education
provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee touch brillo pad in contact with puddle of unclean water on ware washing sink drain board
and proceed to touch lid to cold top insert. Corrected. Education provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following items were out of proper holding temperature:
*cooked onions/ cold top insert near kitchen entrance/ 47F
*soup/ reach in cooler near kitchen entrance/ 45F
*raw chicken/ reach in cooler near kitchen entrance/ 44F
*whole milk/ upright cooler in front service area/ 46F
Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Improper food storage observed in the following areas:
*sealed bag of raw beef in contact with sealed bag of tortillas in prep cooler near entrance to kitchen.
*shelled eggs stored over bags of bread in upright cooler in back room.
*sealed tube of raw hamburger meat stored over sealed bag of spinach in upright cooler in back room.
Corrected. Education and sticker provided.

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: The following areas observed unclean:
*grease and food build up on sides of deep fryer and cook top.
*food debris on floor below waffle iron make area.
*heavy crumbs on floor near window sill in front service area.
*old food debris in non-functional and unused floor drain below automatic dish machine.
*sticky residue on both sides of swinging door at entrance to kitchen.
*Sticky, brown residue on handles to refrigeration units, ware washing and hand sink handles.
Correct today and maintain.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Utensils improperly stored:
*Strainer spoon with dried food debris stored hanging above cook top.
*in use spatulas and ladle, with food debris, stored without intervention.
*skillets with food debris, per employee-left overnight on shelf, stored with clean skillets.
Correct immediately. Education provided.
Sleeves of cups stored on floor in water heater closet. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)
