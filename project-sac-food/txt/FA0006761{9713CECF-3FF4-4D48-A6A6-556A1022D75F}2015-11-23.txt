# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Buildup on piercing part of can opener. Clean and sanitize today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: -Heavy debris, standing water inside floor sinks where soda machine from dining area drains into and the one
where the soda syrup boxes are stored and in dishwashing area. Clean and maintain within 7 days (REPEAT VIOLATION).
-Heavy grease accumulation on wheels of fryers and equipment under the ventilation hood. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
-Heavy grease accumulation on floor and walls around where chicken seasoning is stored and chicken/fry fryers. Clean and
maintain within 7 days.
-Debris on floor under soda machine in drive through, food prep area across from three compartment sink and cash registers. Clean
and maintain within 7 days.
-Cardboard boxes holding plastic food lids have visible debris and water damage across from walk-in refrigerators. Replace
cardboard boxes when there is visible food debris/water or replace with plastic container that can be washed, rinsed and sanitized
at three compartment sink. Correct within 5 days.
-Heavy water, grease and food accumulation around chicken fryers and breading station. Keep area clean to prevent vermin
infestation (REPEAT VIOLATION).
-Debris inside closest with water heater. Clean and maintain within 7 days (REPEAT VIOLATION).
NOTE: Routine inspection conducted on 6/11/15 stated that next repeat violation received under this violation will result in a

within 2 weeks.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Dishwasher after the rinse cycle dumps water that drains on floor in front of machine into drain in the middle of
the prep area. Machine must directly drain into floor sink to prevent standing water. Correct within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Caulking peeling apart inside one of the microwaves. The inside of the other microwave has caulking that is rough
and not smooth and easily cleanable. Replace microwaves within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: -Gap observed under the back door of facility. Provide a door sweep to close gap to prevent vermin entrance.
Correct within 14 days.
-Hole in wall under the "labor poster". Repair wall with materials that are smooth, easily cleanable and non-absorbant. Correct within
60 days.
-Gaps around pipes from hand sink near chicken breading station. Gap around pipe from wall under the three compartment sink.
Provide plumbing collars to seal gaps around these pipes to prevent vermin harborage.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Broken tiles behind flat grill and in front of one door freezer under ventilation hood. Broken tiles around chicken
breading area. Broken tiles around floor sinks under ventilation hood and by walk-in freezer. Standing water and food debris
observed in the broken tiles. Repair tiles within 60 days.
-Damaged ceiling tiles at end of three compartment sink. Replace within 30 days.
-Missing ceiling panel in front of cook's line. Provide within 30 days.
-Cover is cracked on drain by 2 door Delfield freezer near chicken fryers. Replace within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
