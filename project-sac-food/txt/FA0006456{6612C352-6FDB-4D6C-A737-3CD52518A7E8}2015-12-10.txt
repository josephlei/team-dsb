REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

OBSERVATIONS: Soup, on the steam table at 81 deg. F. This was pre-warmed on the burner in the back prior to putting on the
steam table.
Properly and quickly warm foods that are hot held to 165 deg. F prior to placing on the hot holding unit. This will kill any bacteria.
Soup was removed from steam table and reheated on the burner until it reached 165, then placed back on the steam table during
the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Back glass reach in cut melon at 47 deg. F. Repeat violation - see previous notice. Store only non-potentially
hazardous foods in this refrigerator or make repair to ensure that it can hold food at or below 41 deg. Correct within 1 day.
As previously discussed, recommend holding hard boiled eggs and all cut melon in the True two-door reach in. Corrected by
moving the eggs and cut melon into a working refrigerator.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)
