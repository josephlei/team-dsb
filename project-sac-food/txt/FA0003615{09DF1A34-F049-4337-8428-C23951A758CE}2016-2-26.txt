#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJORMeasured the following potentially hazardous foods out of proper holding temperature:
-bean sprouts placed above the fill line in larger cold top unit (52F)
-cooked garlic in oil at the cooks line (67F)
Maintain at/below 41F at all times. Corrected by moving both items inside the reach in refrigerator. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Missing/Expired food handler cards. Provide within 15 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found a glue trap with dead German cockroaches in the back corner floor of cooking equipment, one dying
juvenile cockroach on glue trap located underneath the smaller cold top unit. Eliminate pest immediately and continue aggressive
pest control. Education provided at time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Found a small fist size bag filled with frozen raw beef thawing at room temperature. Use approved thawing
practices. Corrected at time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found frozen bagged chicken stored next to frozen raw beef inside the reach in freezer in kitchen. Keep chicken
and beef separated to prevent cross contamination. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Found 5 small plastic containers which were labeled "pork blood" by the manufacturer but in fact had curry inside.
Label containers accordingly. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: The following areas are unclean:
-floor behind the True branded merchandiser
-floor in the back dry storage room
-floor by the water heater.
Clean and maintain within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: True branded merchandiser at front area (by ice machine) lacks a monitoring thermometer. Provide one today
(reads between 0-220F).

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)
