#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed breaded pork chops and breaded chicken breasts sitting in fry basket at 90 F and 103 F respectively.
Employee stated she was waiting for someone to order item and then she would reheat on grill. Once food is cooked, it must be
offered for immediate service or must be hot-held at 135 F or above using equipment items such as steam table or on grill.
Discontinue practice immediately. Chicken and pork reheated on grill to 165 F.
Observed cooked, breaded pork chops in walk-in refrigerator at 53 F. Employee states pork chops were cooked yesterday. Food
discarded in garbage. See VC&D form.
MINOR VIOLATION:
Observed the following food items to be out of temperature:
* Cut melon at 43 F in 2-door undercounter True refrigerator behind front counter.
* Shucked oysters at 44 F in 2-door True reach-in refrigerator
* Prawns on cold prep top at 44 F.
* Pooled eggs at 45 F on cold prep top
All potentially hazardous food items must be held at 41 F or less or 135 F and above.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Employees lack knowledge about cooking temperatures and hot-holding temperatures. Ensure employees
understand that they must keep cooked foods hot at 135 F and above using approved methods (unless food is offered for immediate
service). If food must be reheated, it must be reheated to at least 165 F. Address today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Observed cooked flat, white noodles delivered earlier today around 11 to be at 50 -68 F in large container with a
cloth towel on top. Cool foods rapidly in an approved manner without cover. Employee transferred noodles to a flat pan with no
cover.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

sanitizing solution. Directed employee to wash, rinse, sanitize, air-dry cutting board in dishmachine. Corrected. Ensure knives and
cutting boards are properly washed, rinsed, sanitized and air-dried after preparing raw meats and in between different types of raw
meat.
Bulk food containers have debris on containers and lids. REPEAT VIOLATION. Clean and sanitize today.

(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 16

SHELLSTOCK IDENTIFICATION TAGS

OBSERVATIONS: No tag being stored with shucked oysters at beginning of inspection. Employee was able to produce tag and tag
was stored with oysters. Ensure correct tag is stored with shellfish at all times. If shellfish are stored in multiple locations, store tag
with product in each location. Refer to handout provided in Chinese regarding shellfish.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Shellstock shall be obtained in containers that bear legible source identification tags. Shellstock tags shall remain
attached to the container that they are received in until the container is empty. Shellstock tags shall be maintained at the food facility in
chronological order correlating to the sale date for 90 calendar days from the date of harvest. The source of the shellstock on display shall
be identified. Shellstock that are portioned or prepackaged shall have a copy of the corresponding shellstock tag. (114039, 114039.1,
114039.3, 114039.4)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed raw, uncovered fish and a box of pork being stored next to vegetables in walk-in refrigerator. Store raw
meats away from vegetables and other food items. Correct today.
Observed vegetables being stored in plastic "Thank you" bags. Discontinue storing food items in plastic bags with ink writing on
them. Store in approved, food-grade containers/bags. Correct today.
Observed some food items to be uncovered. Cover food items to prevent contamination. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed a can of WD-40 being stored next to bulk food bins. Store chemicals away from food and food-related
equipment and items. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Observed grease accumulation in between prep tables and on floor under cookline. Clean within 2 days and

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed several cutting boards, especially cutting board used for lobster, to have many knife marks in board.
Discontinue using cutting boards and obtain new cutting boards within 2 days.
Observed deteriorated gaskets on 2-door upright True refrigerator in kitchen and 2-door True undercounter refrigerator in front
counter area. Replace in an approved manner within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Observed grease accumulation on hood. Clean within 7 days and maintain on regular basis.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Observed wiping cloths on prep surfaces to not have any sanitizer on cloths. Store wiping cloths in sanitizing
solution when not in use. REPEAT VIOLATION. Corrected during inspection.
No sanitizer observed in wiping cloth bucket in kitchen area. Employee corrected and provided new water with 100 ppm of bleach.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed plastic bag wrapped around bottom of drain pipe for steam table. Plastic bag extends to floor sink
where pipe drains to, eliminating air-gap. Discontinue practice immediately and remove bag. There must be minimum of 1" air gap
between drain pipe and floor sink.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Men's restroom hand sink lacks hot water. Insta-hot water heater does not appear to be working. Provide a
minimum of 100 F hot water to hand sink within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Observed mop to be drying in bucket. Store mop upright to facilitate drying. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee purse being stored on shelf near cookline next to and above food items. Also observed
employee food being stored with restaurant food items in 2-door undercounter refrigerator behind front counter. Store employee
belongings and employee food items away from food and food-related equipment and items. Ensure employee food items are labeled
accordingly (e.g. "Employee food"). Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)
