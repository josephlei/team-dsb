HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Broken soap dispenser at handwash station located near storage room. Box with soap was located on top of sink.
Repair/replace soap dispenser within 48 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 32b

COMPLIANCE WITH TRANS FAT BAN

OBSERVATIONS: Nutritional Facts panel or manufacturer specification sheet (based on single serving) was not available for cream
cheese cake, pancake batter mixtures, soups, waffle mixture, pumpkin pancake seasoning, etc. Several soups, waffle mixture,
pumpkin pancake seasoning contained partially hydrogenated oils as an ingredient. Nutritional brochure is not complete. (Note
nutritional brochure notes that side of sour cream has 6 grams of trans fat but per information on the bucket states 0 grams.)
*Panels must be available for verification that all food served does not contain artificial trans fat. Foods that have 0.5 grams of trans
fat per serving or higher shall not be served from facility. Failure to provide labels will result in destruction of food in question at the
next routine inspection. Trans fat ban handout was provided.
Note: Verified trans fat amount in whipped butter that contain partially hydrogenated oil, pecan and pumpkin pie.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No food containing artificial trans fat may be stored, distributed, served by, or used in the preparation of any food
within a food facility. Every food facility shall maintain on the premises, the label for any food that contains fat, oil, or shortening, for as long
as this food is stored, distributed, served by, or used in the preparation of food within the food facility. Food sold or served in a
manufacturer's original sealed package is exempt from this requirement. (114377)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Household Oster toaster in use at cook's line. All equipment must be commercial grade ANSI/NSF approved.
Remove or replace within 4 months.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: - Ice scoop at server's area stored inside unclean container. Cleaned during inspection.
- Unsecured Helium pressurized cylinder stored at dry storage room. Secure to rigid structure within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Monitoring thermometer missing inside reach-in cooler at cook's line. Provide within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: - Several drain pipes observed nesting on top of floor sink.
- Drain pipe from reach-in cooler storing raw shelled eggs draining condensation on the floor.
Route all drain pipes to approved floor sink and provide at least 1 inch air gap within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Grease residue and grease absorber build up observed on the ground around tallow bin. Remove grease
absorber after use and maintain area clean at all times. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: One toilet stall is non-functional at men's restroom. Another stall was provided for use. Repair within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)
