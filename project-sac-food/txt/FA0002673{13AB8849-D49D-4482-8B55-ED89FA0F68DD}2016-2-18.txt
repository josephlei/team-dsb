#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following food items are stored out of proper holding temperature:
-raw liquid eggs in ice bath at 47F
-precooked cut potato in walk-in refrigerator at 44F
-raw bacon in walk-in refrigerator at 43F
-roast beef in walk-in refrigerator at 47F
-pastrami in walk-in refrigerator at 45F
-cooked mushroom in walk-in refrigerator at 47F
-potato soup in walk-in refrigerator at 45F
-milk in walk-in refrigerator at 44F
-cottage cheese in walk-in refrigerator at 43F
-shredded cheese in walk-in refrigerator at 44F
-cooked turkey breast in walk-in refrigerator at 48F
-cooked pork in walk-in refrigerator at 48F
Correct immediately. Refrigerator service technician is in route to facility for walk-in refrigerator repair. Per Manager, excessive
amounts of ice was observed on walk-in condenser unit yesterday. Additional ice water was added to ice bath storing raw shell
eggs. (REPEAT VIOLATION-3RD NOTICE) Failure to correct repeat food temperature violation may result in billable reinspection(s).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open containers and plates of sliced ham, chocolate chips, hard-boiled eggs w/o shell, and frozen fruits are
stored uncovered in reach-in and walk-in refrigerators. Cover food containers to prevent contamination. Correct today. (REPEAT
VIOLATION)
Raw shell eggs are stored above fully cooked ham steak inside the walk-in refrigerator. Also, raw bacon and pork links are stored
above precooked cut potato in same unit. Rearrange raw shell eggs below cooked food to prevent cross contamination. Correct
today. Note: Food contamination was not observed during inspection. (REPEAT VIOLATION)
Open boxes of brown and powder sugar are stored in cabinet at the front service area. Transfer food into containers with tight
fitting lid and label within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Excessive grease and old food debris/residue build-ups are observed on floors behind cooking equipment, ice
machine, and on wall behind dish machine. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
Minor dust build-up is observed on fan guards and ceiling inside the walk-in refrigerator. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
Brown build-up is observed on floor beside the front beverage upright refrigerator. Clean within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Scoop handle is stored submerged in food inside the bulk salt bin. Correct today. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Damp mop is stored on the floor beside the 2-compartment warewash sink. Provide wall-mounted rack for mop
storage within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for
the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet
separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After
use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Wall behind ice machine is damaged. Repair within 30 days.
Vinyl flooring around floor sink near the front ice machine and in warewash area is separating. Repair/replace within 60 days.
(REPEAT VIOLATION-2ND NOTICE)
Wall behind warewash spray sink is damaged. Repair/replace within 60 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving
shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture
of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1,
114271, 114272)
