#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: Handwash sink at food prep area was found completely out of paper towels; facility was out of spare paper towel
rolls. Handwash sinks must always be accessible and fully stocked with soap and paper towels so that handwashing by employees
is made not only possible, but also likely. Operator had employee get some paper towels from the closest Mountain Mike's
restaurant during the inspection. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Shredded mozzarella and ham inside reach-in portion under pizza prep cold top left side of unit) were measured at
48 F and 45 F, respectively, using a thermocouple. Ambient air temperature was also at measured as high 48 F on the left side of the
unit. Buffalo wings on the right side inside the unit were temped at 45 F, the same as the ambient measurement obtained on that
side. Ice buildup around the inserts on top of the unit was pointed out to the operator. This is the fourth consecutive routine
inspection in which food temperature holding violations were observed. Fax proof of correction (e.g., receipt, invoice, work order,
photographs) to EMD [Attn: Bo Faustino] at (916) 875-8513, or email to faustinob@saccounty.net within 7 days. Operator has
ignored this requirement in the past and failure to comply this time shall result in a charged reinspection. Mozzarella, ham and
buffalo wings were moved into the walk-in refrigerator. Do not store potentially hazardous foods in it until it is capable of keeping
contents at 41F or below.
- THIRD REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: a. Bowl is being used as scoop for bulk pizza sauce inside the walk-in refrigerator. Provide approved scoop with
a handle within 2 days.
b. Household-grade microwave oven still in use by the facility. Remove from facility or replace with a commercial unit
(ANSI-certified) within 7 days.
- THIRD REPEAT VIOLATION - (Observed and written up during the last FOUR routine inspections)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Scoop for bulk salad greens was found stored inside the container with handle submerged in the product.
Scoops shall be stored in a more sanitary manner to prevent hand contamination. Correct today.
- SECOND REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Five soiled wiping cloths with no sanitizer found on counter tops. No sanitizer bucket was found set up at start of
inspection. Multi-use wiping cloths used to wipe service counters or other surfaces that may come in contact with food or food
contact items shall be kept in clean water with sanitizer. Corrected during the inspection.
- REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
