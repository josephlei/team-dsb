# 11

REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR- Reheated soup at table top hot holding unit was measuring at 88F, meatballs at steam table measured at
133-134F. Always reheat to 165F internal temperature PRIOR to placing on hot holding unit. Corrected by reheating in microwave at
time of re inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)

OBSERVATIONS: Managers food safety certificate not available. Per owner, he has enrolled in class for next month. If certificate is

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Fan guards in walk in cooler noted with dust build up. Clean within time stated on routine inspection dated
11-24-15.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Slow water leak observed on mens restroom hand wash faucet. Repair within time stated on routine inspection
dated 11-24-15.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 49

PERMIT REQUIREMENTS

OBSERVATIONS: Observed food preparation (soups and meatballs) with a suspended health permit. No food preparation is
permitted when the health permit is suspended. All food must be kept in refrigeration units during suspended permit. Food
preparation may begin once health permit gets re instated.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall not be open for business without a valid permit. Permit shall be posted in a conspicuous place
within the food facility. (114067(b, c), 114381, 114387)
