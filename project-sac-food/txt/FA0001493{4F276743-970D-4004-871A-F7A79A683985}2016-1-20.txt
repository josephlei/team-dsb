#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Grilled chicken (102-139F), spicy grilled chicken (92-123F), egg rolls (114F) in the steam
table, boba (72F) near the front area handwash station were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all potentially hazardous
foods (PHF ) at proper holding temperature at all times. Chicken and egg rolls were reheated at the time of inspection. Per owner,
boba was cooked at about 10:00am. As a result, it will be discarded at 2:00pm. (NOTE: As of today, these items will be using time
as a public health control instead of temperature control)
MINOR VIOLATION - White rice (44F) in the walk-in was out of proper holding temperature. Maintain PHF at proper holding
temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Bowls of noodles in the front area refrigerator are stacked on top of each other and a container of onions is
nested on top of a containe of cabbage. Cease this practice immediately. Do not stack containers of food items without barriers in
between containers. Correct immediately.
Slab of pork belly was stored in an unapproved container in the upright freezer in the back area. Cease the use of these types of
bags (bags with writing or colored bags) to store unpackaged food. Correct immediately.
Container of batter was stored below containers of raw meat in the left side of the walk-in cooler. Store food items accordingly to
prevent cross contamination. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Two containers of creamer on the rack, large containers of sauces under prep sink and container of seasoning
on top rack across cook's line were lacking labels. Provide labels to all containers with food within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Wet wiping cloth was stored on the counter near the steam table. Store all wet wiping cloths in sanitizer at all
times when not in use. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Employee jacket was stored on top of an "open" soda syrup near the back door. Store all personal items under
