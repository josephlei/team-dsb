#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Measured pastrami in steam table at 117 F. Manager reheated during inspection to 167 F.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Manager stated canned gravy is heated in microwave and chili is cooked in steam table. Steam table is not
approved to cook/reheat food products. Steam table is to maintain hot food. Facility previously used stove in kitchen for heating
these products.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed manager clean meat slicer. Manager stated she uses wet cloth(hot water only) to wipe of food debris
and then sprays sanitizer. Ensure all food contact equipment are washed (soap and water), rinsed, sanitized then air dried.
Implement procedures immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Quaternary ammonia (QA) sanitizer in 3-compartment sink measured at 0 ppm. Keep empty until ready to use or
maintain 200 ppm QA at all times.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Low cold and hot water pressure from handwash sink faucet in employee restroom. Correct within 3 days.

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Febreeze next to chocolate syrup bottles, unwrapped straws and cups on shelf below front counter. Relocate
chemicals to designated area below and away from food and food related equipment. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Quaternary ammonia test strips are not available. Provide within 3 days. 2nd NOTICE.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Monitoring thermometer located in rear of keg cooler that is also used to hold dairy products. Store at warmest
part of unit and easily visible (near door). Correct within 24 hours. 2nd NOTICE.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 44a

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Inoperable microwave on top of stove and household Hamilton Beach slow cooker on shelf near rear exit in
kitchen. Remove unnecessary equipment within 72 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of
the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Floor in kitchen has been painted with black latex paint. Manager stated she applied paint recently. Paint is not
an approved to seal cement floor. Contact Env Health Plan Review at 916-874-6010 to determine what floor sealant that can be
used. Contact this inspector with information on material that is used.

or2) Remove stove within 72 hours and keep the same permit.

within the food facility. (114067(b, c), 114381, 114387)
