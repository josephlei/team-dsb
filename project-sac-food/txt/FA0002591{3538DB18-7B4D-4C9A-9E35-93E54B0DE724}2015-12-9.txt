Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 1a

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Operator did not demonstrate acceptable food safety knowledge on proper warewashing. Observed hose with
sprayer attached to handwash sink at donut prep area. Also, found multi-use utensils stored on drain board of this sink. Per
operator, they have hose attached to this sink because utensils and some other food equipment is washed at this sink. Cease
practice immediately. Handwashing sink shall be used only to wash hands. Use 2-comp warewash sink in the back area to wash,
rinse, sanitize and air dry all utensils and food contact equipment (pans, bowls, etc.). Ensure all employees are adequately trained in
all aspects of food safety. (Also, see violation #6)
Note: Highly recommend all employee’s attend the FREE “How to get a green” food safety class held at EMD. Call for schedule and
to reserve seating.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Black hose attached to faucet at donut prep area handwashing sink. Hands can be washed but very
inconveniently since hose is stored deep inside the sink. Remove hose immediately. Handwash station shall be easily used at all
times.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water at 2-comp warewash sink reached only 112F. Per operator, sometimes customers adjust hot water on
the water heater that is located inside restroom. Provide at least 120F to warewash sink. Recommend posting a sign notifying
customers not to play with water heater. Operator adjusted hot water during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Box with foam cups stored on the floor at warewash room. Store at least 5 inches off the floor. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

OBSERVATIONS: Old food residue, grease, and debris build up on the following equipment:
- soap dispenser at donut prep handwash sink station
- proofer box door and shelvings
- all shelves at donut prep area
- Hobart mixer stand
- racks holding donuts
- floor sink at front area
- mop bucket
- unused refrigerator
Clean and maintain 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: - Facility is missing drain plugs for two compartment warewash sink. Provide within 5 days. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Sprayer with hose attached to faucet at 2-comp sink. Hose with sprayer observed to be stored inside warewash sink. Per operator,
he needs sprayer to rinse equipment. Remove hose with prayer and install proper faucet with sprayer within 14 days. Ensure faucet
can reach all 2-compartment sinks.
- Mold like residue build up inside sanitizer side of warewash sink and drain board for air drying equipment observed cluttered with
boxes. Clean and sanitize sink, and keep drain board clean and usable. Correct today before starting warewashing.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found old food residue caked on scale and some dough prep equipment. All equipment must be properly washed
and stored clean. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Operator used cloth towel without sanitizer to wipe food contact surfaces and sanitizer bucket was not set up. Set
up sanitizer bucket with 100 ppm chlorine and store cloth towels in this sanitizer solution between use. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water

surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 44a

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Found multiple unused and dusty equipment and other non-food related items at warewash/storage room
especially on shelf next to mop sink and unused corner of unused rinse station. Organize this room, remove all unnecessary items,
and clean all equipment within 14 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of
the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Wet mop nesting inside mop bucket. Provide hanger and hang mop to air dry. Correct today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: - Prayer station with liquid in a cup and burning sticks in a container found on shelf above dough prep table where
donuts were stored. Operator removed prayer items during inspection. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Personal items (pillow, clothing, food items, etc.) stored on shelf above single use utensils/cups at warewash room storage area.
All personal items must be stored below and away from food and food related items. Reorganize today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Found gaps/openings around plumbing beneath warewash sink, mop sink, and several other areas at the
warewash/storage room. Patch/fill with approved material within 14 days. REPEAT VIOLATION
Missing cover for light switch at dry storage room storing donut mixes. Install to eliminate gap within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
