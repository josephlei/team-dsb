Routine

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Food handler cards could not be located today - manager is out. Food handler cards must be available for review
at all times. Ensure that staff are aware of food handler card location and have access to them.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Two drinks located in prep area next to smoothie blender. Ensure that all eating and drinking is done separate
and away from all food and food prep activities. Keep drinks in an area designated for personal items that is separate and away
from food and food prep activities. Correct 1 day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Wall mounted soap dispenser is empty (broken?). Pump soap is available. Remove or restock wall mounted soap
dispenser. Correct 1 week.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Sausage patties on prep table 37-48F. Insert is overfilled. Suggest reducing amount of patties. Ensure that all
cold food is maintained at or below 41F at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Prep surfaces in front of prep tables have large quantity of food on them. Employee indicates that these area
wiped with sanitizer towels routinely. Ensure that these prep surfaces are washed rinsed and sanitized at least every 4 hours.
Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Several areas have dirt/mold food build up. Clean more thoroughly and more routinely:
Floors - along wall floor juncture.
Caulked area behind 2 compartment sink
inside all cabinets and along all shelving
Floor sinks

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Cabinet where coffee is made and handwash sink is installed is extremely deteriorated. Laminated has peeled
away in many areas leaving uncleanable surfaces. Counter is also bowing and has moisture damage. Replace this counter and
cabinet with approved commercial equipment. Ensure that handsink remains in current location. Sink may be replaced, but must
remain in same location and be operational whenever facility is open. Correct 60 days.
Correction will be confirmed at next routine inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Wiping towel noted on counter across from walk in. Two towels noted on floor. One towel bucket contained 0
ppm sanitizer, Ensure that all towels are kept in sanitizer bucket with at least 200 ppm Quaternary Ammonia whenever not in active
use. When towels are soiled, place in dirty linen container. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 44b

APPROPRIATE USE OF EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Prep sink is completely full of unused equipment and cannot be accessed for use. Remove all equipment from
sink and ensure that hot and cold water are functioning at this sink. All food prep activities such as washing tomatoes should be
done at this sink. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not clean their hands in a sink used for food preparation, warewashing, or in a service sink or a
curbed cleaning facility used for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A handwashing facility shall not be used for purposes
other than handwashing. Food preparation sinks, handwashing lavatories, and warewashing equipment shall not be used for the cleaning
of maintenance tools, the preparation or holding of maintenance materials, or the disposal of mop water and similar liquid wastes. Open-air
barbecue or outdoor wood-burning oven shall be operated on the same premises as, in reasonable proximity to, and in conjunction with, a
permanent food facility that is approved for food preparation. (113953.1 (b-d), 114123, 114143 (a,b), Exception 114125)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: SECOND NOTICE: Mop sink with hose attached lacks anti siphon device. Correct 1 week.
