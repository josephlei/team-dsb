Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700.
All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the
potential to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee beverage with screw-top lid stored on prep table. Provide a lid/straw and store below/away
from food and food related items (screw-top lids are not approved). Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent
contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to
eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination.
(113977, 114256 (a))

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee dry hands with cloth towel and place on prep table after washing utensils. Wash hands
whenever contaminated and use single use paper towels to dry hands. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw to
ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate hands.
(113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Bottle of dish soap lacks label. Provide within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination of
food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food facility
may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall be
clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Spray bottle of water hanging on wire rack next to 3-door reach-in lacks a label. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Wiping cloth without sanitizer stored on prep counter. Keep stored inside sanitizer solution when not in use.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

NOTES

Facility is using White Spred 'N Fill Icing which contains hydrogenated cottonseed oil. According to nutritional facts provided by
manufacturer, ingredient contains 0.88 grams trans fatty acid per 100 grams of product. Each cupcake (serving size) contains
approximately 1.5 oz of product, which contains approximately 0.37 grams of trans fat. This is in compliance with Cal Code section
114377, which limits trans fat to no more than 0.5 grams per serving.
NOTE: Facility is using ChemMark Klor 300 sanitizer with active ingredient of 6% sodium hypochlorite (chlorine based). When testing
concentration, solution tests negative for chlorine but positive for quaternary ammonia. Contact manufacturer to find out why this is
happening. The incorrect chemical may have been placed inside container from the manufacturer. Provide quaternary ammonia test strips
in the mean time and keep sanitizer at a minimum of 200 ppm QA.
