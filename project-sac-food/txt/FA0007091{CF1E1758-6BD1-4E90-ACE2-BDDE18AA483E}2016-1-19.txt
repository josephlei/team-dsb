#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Food at self-service warmer was out of proper temperature: asiago chicken 122F-132F, chicken wings 109F-131F,
butternut squash 116F-122F, rotisserie chicken 120F-132F, grilled vegetables 126F. Per employee, food was cooked before 11am
and log states temperature reached 170F. Some containers were overfilled and some temped 131F on the bottom of pan. Corrected
by reheating food above 165F.
- Ribs 128F-132F near door at double deck Alto-Shaam. Ribs were reheated during inspection. (Note all other food items have been
covered inside warmer unit.)
- Spanish rice 116F-134F at burrito prep steam table. Reheated during inspection.
Minor Violation: - Green beans 130F-132F and zucchini 132F inside self-service warmer.
- Butternut squash salad 48F, grilled mushrooms 48F, grilled zucchini 48F, roasted root vegetables 46F-50F, beet apple salad 50F at
salad bar cooler.
- Turkey croissant sandwiches 45F at open air cooler while cooler was on defrost cycle.
Maintain all hot food items at/above 135F and cold foods at/below 41F at all times. Do not store food items above insert fill line. If
possible add more water to steam units or place food into deeper containers. Turn thermostat to colder setting.
Note: Ambient temperature near doors of walk-in cooler was 42F. Turn thermostat to colder setting to ensure all foods stored
at/below 41F at all times. (Also, see violation #35)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee handle dirty dishes into dish machine and then unload clean dishes without washing hands in
between. Wash hands after handling dirty dishes before clean dishes. Wash hands whenever get contaminated. Employee was
education. Highly recommend installing soap dispenser at warewash area.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: Improperly maintained TPHC log since second batch of breakfast foods (sausages, oatmeal, eggs, etc.) was not
properly logged with time. Second batch of breakfast was removed at 11:47am from self-service area when log indicated 11am for
the first batch only. When time only, rather time in conjunction with temperature is used as a public health control, proper records
and documentation must be maintained. Food items must be labeled with time from it was removed from temperature control and
time when it should be discarded (4 hours later). Food must be promptly discarded at 4th hour. Per management, they will start
labeling time for every batch that gets placed on self-service area. Train all employees about proper TPHC use. Failure to comply
will result in major violation. Education and TPHC handout was provided.

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Deteriorated top door gasket of walk-in cooler. Replace within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: - Not properly functional thermometer inside walk-in cooler. Provide within 7 days.
- Missing monitoring thermometer inside small Alto-Shaam warmer. Provide within 5 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Sprayer head was nesting inside prep sink next to blast chiller. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)
