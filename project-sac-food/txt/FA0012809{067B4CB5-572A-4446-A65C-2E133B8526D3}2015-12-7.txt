#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following food items are stored out of proper holding temperature.
-Tacos in walk-in refrigerator at 51F
-diced & sliced tomatoes in walk-in refrigerator at 52F
-sliced cheese in walk-in refrigerator at 52F
-sliced turkey in walk-in refrigerator at 53F
-cooked chicken strips in walk-in refrigerator at 52F
-soft serve mix in walk-in refrigerator at 50F
Corrected; walk-in refrigerator thermostat was adjusted during inspection. After adjustment, ambient air temperature dropped to 48F
with minor fluctuation. All potentially hazardous foods in walk-in refrigerator were relocated into upright and reach-in refrigerators.
Maintenance was called during inspection and in-route to facility.
Note: Food temperature log indicated that walk-in refrigerator maintained food temperature at 38F at 9:15am. Per Manager, walk-in
refrigerator still felt cold at about 11 am when she conducted food inventory.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Old food residue is observed on dishes/utensils including scissors, can opener, sauce bottles, and scoops that
are stored on the clean dish storage rack. Corrected; dishes/utensils with food residue were placed back in the warewash sink for
cleaning during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Automatic dish machine lacks adequate chlorine sanitizer residual during final rinse cycle, 0 ppm measured.
Corrected; a nearly empty bulk sanitizer bucket was replaced during inspection. Sanitizer residual measured 50 ppm after
correction.
Note: Dish machine was not in-use at time of observation.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water at the 3-compartment warewash sink and automatic dish machine measured 116F and 102, respectively.
Adjust water heater to maintain hot water at 120F minimum. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Fan guards inside the upright refrigerators are unclean with dust build-up. Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Walk-in refrigerator is not capable of maintaining food temperature at 41F or below. Repair/service immediately.
Submit copy of service invoice to EMD attn: Toua Yang within 72 hours to avoid reinspection.
Minor rust build-up is observed on metal racks inside the upright refrigerator. Clean and remove rust build-up within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Clean utensils are stored in container with box cutter, roll of tape, markers, and other non-food related items.
Corrected; utensils were sent to warewash sink for cleaning during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

OBSERVATIONS: Minor leak is observed from plumbing behind the automatic dish machine. Repair within 7 days.
The warewash sink faucet is missing the hot water adjustment knob. Replace within 7 days.
