HANDWASHING FACILITIES

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: Major Violation
-Handwashing station by the bar lacked paper towels. Corrected during inspection.
Minor Violation (repeat)
-The handwashing station on the cook's line opposite the fryer is installed less than 24 inches away from the cold top prep table.
Provide a splash guard a minimum of 6 inches high or relocate cold top prep table further than 24 inches away. Correct within 7
days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas.
Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and
sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953,
113953.1, 113953.2)

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed one employee drink (open can of monster energy drink) inside the sushi reachin refrigerator.
-Observed one employee drink (bottle) stored above clean towels and the handwash station at the drink station.
Correct today. Ensure that all employee drinks consist of a cup with a lid and straw and are stored away/below all food, food
preparation areas and clean utensils.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent
contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to
eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination.
(113977, 114256 (a))

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed several packages of breaded shrimp at 47F-49F inside the cook's line reachin refrigerator nearest the
handwash station. Temperature of unit was lowered during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed two packages of semi frozen eel thawing on the cold top in room temperature at the sushi prep
counter top. Ensure that an approved method of thawing depicted on the proper thawing sticker given during time of inspection is
used for all future thawing.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed old food debris on the sides of the front reachin refrigerators at the sushi station.
-Observed dust accumulation on the underside of the walkin refrigerator's fan guards, fanguards of the true refrigerator in the dry
storage room, and fanguards of the true refrigerator by the rice cooker.
-Observed old debris on the soap and paper towel dispensers at the bar.
-Observed brown dust-like accumulation on the white metal shelf storing spices by the back door.
Clean and maintain all above areas within 7 days. Begin cleaning immediately during slow periods. Ensure that all food are
properly covered or removed before cleaning begins.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: -Upon arrival, ambient temperature for the east sushi cold display storing raw fish observed at 47F-50F. Fish and
other potentially hazardous food had already been storing in this unit. Food was relocated in the reachin refrigerator until unit
reached 41F or below. After 40 minutes into the inspection, ambient temperature now observed at 42F.
-Observed the reachin refrigerator nearest the handwashing station on the cook's line at 41F-45F. Unit was lowered during
inspection.
Ensure that the ambient temperature inside all refrigeration units used to store potentially hazardous foods are at 41F or below
before food is stored inside unit to avoid potentially hazardous foods increasing in temperature.
-Observed several grocery bags with ink used to store fish and shrimp inside the walkin freezer. Discontinue practice of using
inked grocery bags and begin using food grade plastic bags or containers. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed one unsecure co2 tank at the bar opposite the three compartment sink. Correct today.
-Observed 3 tongs hanging on the side of the oven door on the cook's line. Tongs were relocated to the middle shelf on the cook's
line. Ensure that all clean utensils are stored in a sanitary manner that avoids potential contamination.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized
surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and
dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid
structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)
