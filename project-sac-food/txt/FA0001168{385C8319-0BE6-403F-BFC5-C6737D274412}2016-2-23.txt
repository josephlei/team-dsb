#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following items were out of proper holding temperature:
*caramelized onions/ right side of cold top cooler across from cook's line/ 55F
*crumbled blue cheese/ right side of cold top cooler across from cook's line/ 53F
*house-made mayonnaise/ right side of cold top cooler across from cook's line/ 67F
Corrected by adding ice. Per employee, the food items were placed in the unit two hours prior.
MINOR VIOLATION
The following items, on both sides of cold top cooler and cold drawers, across from cook's line were out of proper holding
temperature: salmon, breaded chicken, cooked lentils, cooked brussel sprouts, cooked potatoes, mozzarella cheese, and pooled
eggs measured 48F-49F. Correct immediately. NOTE: all food items were placed on ice.
Half & half, cream, shell eggs, inside of left side of refrigeration unit at bar measured 49F. Corrected by relocating to walk in cooler.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Employees are unaware of testing method and sanitizer concentration for sanitizer being used in automatic dish
machine in kitchen. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Squash soup improperly cooling in deep 5 gallon container wihtin~6 inches of ice. Correct immediately. Education
provided. NOTE: soup measured 135F and was cooked 10 minutes prior.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

maintain within 3 days.

tightly seal the package. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open box of pasta and open bags of flour stored in outdoor storage room. Correct immediately. NOTE: outdoor
storage room is to be used to store non-potentially hazardous food items that are in manufacture sealed containers.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Debris in floor sink below bar signage. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Lack sanitizer test strips for automatic dish machine in kitchen. Correct within 24 hours.
NOTE: unable to test sanitizer concentration in automatic dish machine in kitchen due lack of availability of peroctanoic acid test
strips. Provide proof of sanitizer concentration within 24 hours to email/fax number provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: ~10 pairs of tongs stored on oven handles in back kitchen. Store utensils in a sanitary place. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Hole on wall surrounding plumbing below automatic dish machine in kitchen. Eliminate gaps with smooth, easily
cleanable, and non-absorbent material within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
