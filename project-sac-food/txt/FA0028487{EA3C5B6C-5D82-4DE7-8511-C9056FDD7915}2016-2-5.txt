Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Food handler cards for all employees not available for review. Provide within 2 weeks. NOTE: Per manager, there
have been several new employees. New employees must have proof of food handler card within 30 days of employment.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed approximately 8-10 rat dropping still on floor next to water heater and unapproved gas convection
ovens used to bake pastries. Some of the droppings and a black plastic bag that appeared to have been chewed were cleaned and
removed. Complete cleaning within 24 hours. <<2ND REPEAT VIOLATION>>

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed 2 torn gaskets inside 2-door reach-in below espresso machine and 3 torn gaskets inside 3-door
cold-top reach-in. Replace gaskets within 12 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Observed unapproved gas convection ovens located inside room outside of kitchen without ventilation hoods.
Discontinue use of ovens immediately. Consult a natural gas professional before disconnecting units. NOTE: Ovens were warm to
the touch at the time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Sink behind register is nonfunctional. Repair within 5 days.
Observed water/moisture accumulation inside compartment behind same sink. Investigate cause and repair within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

NOTE: Extremely large amounts of extra equipment (including refrigeration equipment and other restaurant supplies) is accumulated

CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of
the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Gap under door between kitchen and area with walk in refrigerators has been sealed with a plastic piece and tape.
Provide a permanent metal door sweep within 5 days. <<REPEAT VIOLATION>>
The gaps under the dump and hand wash sinks at front counter are sealed with spray foam. It is extremely bumpy and there are
numerous gaps for vermin to hide. Remove spray foam and use caulk or sealant to cleanly seal area under the sinks. Discontinue
use of spray foam. Correct within 12 days. <<REPEAT VIOLATION>>

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

OBSERVATIONS:
There are several large cutouts on the wall behind the sinks and the last cabinet on the right. These are open to gain access to the
plumbing. Install access panels to seal off cutouts. Provide a plumbers collar around the drainage lines and use caulk to seal all
gaps around collar and panel. Ensure panel is of a material that is smooth, durable and easily cleanable. Correct within 7 days.
<<REPEAT VIOLATION>>
Observed unapproved floors, walls and ceilings throughout the entire facility. Entire facility allows access for vermin entry and
harborage. Owner stated they are remodeling the kitchen to relocate prep areas. The following deficiencies were observed to
include, but not limited to:
-non-smooth / exposed concrete floor throughout kitchen
-corrugated metal flashing used for ceiling and wall material that has gaps and unsealed seams throughout kitchen
-deteriorated paint on wall above 3 compartment sink
-piece of wood siding patched in place on wall between prep table and cold-top unit. Multiple areas are covered with plastic
garbage bags and painters tape
-open hole in corrugated metal flashing around electrical conduit coming from ceiling, down to near prep table
-hole in wall, covered with a piece of cardboard taped to the wall adjacent to 3-door upright reach-in
-textured wall with matte or eggshell paint near kitchen side door that leads to restroom and large indoor seating area
Side room where unapproved convection ovens and walk-in cooler units are located has unapproved ceiling and large gaps in walls
around electrical conduit. Door to exterior of facility that is leading into this room is missing and has been replaced with a large
piece of burlap that is easily moved. This room is NOT approved to be used for any food preparation or storage.
Operator must contact Environmental Management Department Plan Review and building department with a plan as to how to make
this room vermin proof within 7 days. Failure to comply will result in additional enforcement action.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)

# 48

PLAN REVIEW CONSTRUCTION AND REMODELING

OBSERVATIONS: Two walk-in refrigeration and freezer units (as documented on routine inspection dated 1/22/15) were added to
facility without going through EMD plan review process. Both units are in an outdoor room that is not fully enclosed and not vermin
proof. No food storage or food related equipment is permitted to be stored inside of units until plans have been submitted and
approved by EMD Plan Review Department. They con be contacted at (916) 874-6010 for requirements on plan submittal.
Walk in units are non-functional and are being utilized to store dry food products and non-potentially hazardous foods inside.
Relocate all food product and food-related items that are not able to be washed/rinsed/sanitized within 12 days.
If dishwasher is to be added place that and all new equipment on plans when submitting to EMD Plan Review.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A person proposing to build or remodel a food facility shall submit plans for approval before starting any new
construction or remodeling of any facility for use as a retail food facility. (114380)
