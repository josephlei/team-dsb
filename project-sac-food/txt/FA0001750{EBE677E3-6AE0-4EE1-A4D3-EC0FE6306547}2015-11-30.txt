# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Spiderwebs under the dishwasher and area around the dishwasher. Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: -Waterproof thermometer or thermolabels are not available for high temperature dishwasher. Provide within 2
days (REPEAT VIOLATION).
-Quaternary ammonia test strips for sanitizer buckets are discolored and missing the indicator scale. Provide new ones within 2
days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Wood peeling on front shelving at service line, on shelving across from dishwasher, inside the cabinets to the left
of hand sink and shelving inside dry storage room. Repair shelving so that surfaces are smooth, easily cleanable and durable.
Correct over summer break.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Restroom door open. Keep closed to prevent vermin entrance and harborage. Correct within 24 hours (REPEAT
VIOLATION).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

# 44a

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Stand up mixer tagged for equipment pickup. All equipment not in use at school must be removed. Correct during
summer break.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of
the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)
