#9

COOLING METHODS

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: Improper cooling of pastrami. Pastrami at 50-51F in large covered stock pot inside walk-in refrigerator. Pastrami
was made yesterday. Employee voluntarily discarded item to trash during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as
an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002,
114002.1)

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Employee beverage without lid or straw located on ledge near meat slicer in sandwich prep area. Relocated
during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent
contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to
eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination.
(113977, 114256 (a))

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Sliced tomato and cheese at 43-47F on ice bath in cold top insert of prep rerfrigerator and mash potato at 130F in
steamtable. Maintain food cold at 41F or below or hot at 135F or above. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Handwash sink in men's restroom pulling away from wall. Repair within 7 days. -Repeat ViolationMalfunctioning 2-door prep refrigerator next to steamtable. Repair within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Deteriorated (exposed wood) wall next to and beneath steamtable. Repair within 14 days. Surface must be
smooth, nonabsorbent and cleanable.
Deteriorated flooring throughout facility, especially beneath meat slicer, near floor sink of ice machine, behind cooking equipment
(floor and cove base) and steamtable. Floor in entire prep area must be replace with approved flooring within 30 days. Current
VCT floor with rubber top set is no longer approved and may not be reinstalled. Further questions regarding approved flooring
please contact plan review at 874-6010. -Repeat ViolationNote - Determining factor for floor replacement is the renewal of lease. Owner claims that renew with take place within
approximately 3 months.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving

of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1,
114271, 114272)
