Routine

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 1a

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: -Onsite employees lacked knowledge as to what minimum concentration of quaternary ammonium for
warewashing was required. Warewashing handout provided during time of inspection. Ensure that a minimum of 200ppm
quaternary ammonium or 100ppm chlorine is used when manually warewashing.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 1b

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION

OBSERVATIONS: REPEAT VIOLATION
-Facility was unable to provide a valid copy of a Food Safety Certification during time of inspection. Correct within 15 days. Provide
proof of a valid Food Safety Certification or proof that an employee is registered for the course within 15 days via email or fax to
information provided on your inspector's business card. Failure to provide proof of correction may result in a reinspection with a
reinspection fee.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee
who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed scoop handle directly touching food product inside the bulk cornmeal container. Ensure that scoops
are stored in a manner that the handle never comes into contact with food products. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: -Old food residual observed on the front handwash station faucet and handles. Clean and maintain to avoid
potential cross contamination after washing hands. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: -Leaky faucet observed at the three compartment sink. Repair or replace within 14 days.
-Drain plug is loose and not holding sanitizer water in the three compartment sink without assistance or intervention. Repair or
replace within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: -Monitoring thermometer for the front lowboy was stored in the back of the unit and blocked with food containers.
Monitoring thermometers should always be in the warmest area of the unit and easily viewable at all times. Corrected during time of
inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: -Dumpster lids observed open during time of inspection. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: -Moist mops were stored directly on the floor. Ensure that mops are stored in a manner that allows the mop to air
dry. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 47c

INSPECTION REPORT AVAILABILITY

OBSERVATIONS: -Facility was unable to provide the most current copy of their inspection report during time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A copy of the most recent routine inspection report conducted to assess compliance with this part shall be
maintained at the food facility and made available upon request. The food facility shall post a notice advising consumers that a copy of the
most recent routine inspection report is available for review by any interested party. (113725.1)
