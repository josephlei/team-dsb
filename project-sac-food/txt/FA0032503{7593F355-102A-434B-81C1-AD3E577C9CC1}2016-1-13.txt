Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Pre packaged fish under fish display case: salmon steak 48F, yellow fin 47F, octopus 43F, salmon sashimi 46F
In dessert case: Bean cakes at 44f
Ensure that all potentially hazardous foods are maintained at or below 41F.
Correct 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 13

FOOD IN GOOD CONDITION, SAFE, AND UNADULTERATED

OBSERVATIONS: Damp box noted on shelving in produce area walk in - inside container of food noted with mold build up on lid and
edges. Discarded by manager.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not commit any act that may cause the contamination or adulteration of food or food contact
surfaces. Food shall be produced, prepared, stored, transported, and served, so as to be free from adulteration and spoilage. Food is
considered adulterated if it bears or contains any poisonous or deleterious substance that may render it impure or injurious to health.
Containers previously used to store poisonous or toxic materials shall not be used to store food, utensils, or single-use articles. Utensils
used for tasting shall be discarded or washed, rinsed, and sanitized between tastings. Ice used as a medium for cooling food and
beverages shall not be offered for consumption. Food shall be inspected upon receipt and prior to any use, storage, or resale. (113967,
113976, 113980, 113988, 113990, 114035, 114167, 114254.3)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: One slicer in meat department noted with some dried meat build up on the underside of sliding piece. Ensure that
slicer is completely broken down, cleaned and sanitized every four hours during constant use or between different types of food
product. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Some cleaning bottles noted by produce area without labels. Label all containers. Correct 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Shelves in walk in by produce area are disorganized with many items nesting on top of each other. Throughly
Clean shelving. Eliminate use of cardboard boxes as storage containers in walk in. Correct 1 week.

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Debris noted on floor under selves in dry storage areas, and floors of walk in . Clean more thoroughly and
routinely. Correct 1 week.
2 aisles of back room behind kitchen area have heavy clutter - appears very disorganized with many unused items including food
and cleaning products. Provide thorough cleaning and organization of these areas. Correct 1 week.
Knife rack by produce sink is dirty. Clean and sanitize routinely. Correct1 day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Throughout facility pallets and soda racks are being used to keep food and equipment off the floor. Pallets and
soda racks are not approved shelving - these items may only be used on a temporary basis. Floors below these items are not being
cleaned routinely resulting in debris build up beneath pallets and racks. Install approved shelving. Develop a plan for installation of
approved shelving
Two knives in produce area have handles covered with tape. This renders the handle an "uncleanable" surface. Remove tape or
replace knives. Ensure utensils are smooth and easily cleanable. Correct 1 week

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Wiping towels noted throughout produce area - towels are damp and contain 0 ppm sanitizer. Keep towels in
sanitizer bucket whenever not in active use. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Various drink cup and mugs located throughout back area: Cup inside 2 door freezer in back, mug on cart by
produce area. Coffee cup on shelves with pre packaged foods. Keep all employee personal items in a designated area that is
separate and away from food and food prep activities. Correct 1 day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)
