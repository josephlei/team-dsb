#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION: Far right end of packaged meat refrigerator containing packages of pre-cooked meats had
temperature ranging from 42-60F. All packages that were 41-50F were removed to cold walk in refrigerator. 37 packages identified
over 51F were removed from sale.
Grated cheese section of cold case overstocked and front 3-5 packages on many pegs were 41-54F. All packages of cheese above
41F removed to cold walk in. Manager states that grated cheese packages will not be overstocked in the future.
Hot dogs on top shelf in cold self service refrigerator 42-53F. 3 packages that were over 50F were removed from sale. All other
packages over 41F were removed to cold walk in.
Salad display case in produce section: Caesar salad bowl at 48F, southwest salad at 43, cut canteloupe cup at 46F.
Make repairs or replace equipment as needed - ensure that all food products are maintained at or below 41F at all times.
Check food temperatures regularly using a probe thermometer to ensure that food temperature is below 41F - do not rely on
refrigerator digital displays.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

DISCHARGE FROM EYES, NOSE, AND MOUTH

OBSERVATIONS: Employee in meat cutting area noted repeatedly wiping running nose with sleeve while cutting meat. If nose
discharge cannot be controlled through medication, employee should be removed from food preparation activities. Correct 1 day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees experiencing persistent sneezing, coughing, or runny nose shall not work with exposed food, clean
equipment and utensils, linens and single-use articles. (113974)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Soap dispenser in meat department is not functioning properly- must bang on it to get soap out. Repair or replace
dispenser. Correct 1 week.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Buildup noted on faucet and paper towel dispenser in meat department. Clean thoroughly. Correct 1 day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Hose in meat department leaking on to floor. Repair so that water is not dripping to floor when hose is not in use.
Keep floors clean and dry as much as possible. Correct 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)
