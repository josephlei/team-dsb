HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following food items are stored out of proper holding temperature:

Minor violation:
-cut barbecue pork in cold top at 46F

OBSERVATIONS: Employee lacks adequate knowledge of chlorine sanitizer concentration use requirement. Corrected; education

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

#2

COMMUNICABLE DISEASE EXCLUSIONS, RESTRICTIONS

OBSERVATIONS: Employee with open sore on his hand was observed in kitchen without gloves or bandage. Corrected; employee
was directed to wash hands, apply clean bandage, and wear gloves during inspection. Note: Employee was not observed preparing
food or handling food utensils during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees with a communicable disease shall be excluded from the food facility or restricted from working with
exposed food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Gloves shall be worn when contacting food and food-contact
surfaces if an employee has any cuts, wounds, or rashes on hands or arms. (113949.2, 113949.4, 113949.5, 113950, 113950.5, 113973(a))

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Employee's cup of tea and bottled beverages were observed on shelf above food prep tables. Provide beverage
containers with tight fitting lid and straw within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent
contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to
eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination.
(113977, 114256 (a))

contact was not observed. Corrected; employees were directed to wash hands and wear gloves during inspection.

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: A large bus tub of steamed rice (8"-10" deep) is observed improperly cooling on speed rack at 141F. In additional,
several large (6"-10" deep) tightly covered containers of cooked shredded cabbage with pork, chow mien noodles, and pork
wonton are observed improperly cooling inside the walk-in refrigerator at 63F-68F. Per employee, food items have been cooling for
less than 1.5 hours. Corrected; food items were portioned into shallow trays and placed back inside the walk-in refrigerator during
inspection.
Note: Proper cooling methods were discussed in detail with Manager during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as
an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002,
114002.1)

# 13

FOOD IN GOOD CONDITION, SAFE, AND UNADULTERATED

OBSERVATIONS: 3 cans of Restaurant King young baby corn have dented seams. Remove damaged canned food from use today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not commit any act that may cause the contamination or adulteration of food or food contact
surfaces. Food shall be produced, prepared, stored, transported, and served, so as to be free from adulteration and spoilage. Food is
considered adulterated if it bears or contains any poisonous or deleterious substance that may render it impure or injurious to health.
Containers previously used to store poisonous or toxic materials shall not be used to store food, utensils, or single-use articles. Utensils used
for tasting shall be discarded or washed, rinsed, and sanitized between tastings. Ice used as a medium for cooling food and beverages shall
not be offered for consumption. Food shall be inspected upon receipt and prior to any use, storage, or resale. (113967, 113976, 113980,
113988, 113990, 114035, 114167, 114254.3)

# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Automatic dish machine lacks adequate chlorine sanitizer residual during final rinse cycle, 0 ppm measured.
Corrected; dish machine dispenser tube lost prime and had to be primed by employee during inspection. Note: Dish machine was
not in-use at time of observation; dish machine monitoring well was empty with plug pulled out.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least 100F
or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact with
solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in the final
rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After cleaning and
sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101 (b-d), 114105,
114109, 114111)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open containers of cooked beef, chicken, tofu, and sauces are stored inside the cook's line reach-in cooler and
walk-in refrigerator uncovered. Cover food containers with lids to prevent food contamination. Correct within 2 days. Note: Food
contamination was not observed.

above egg rolls containing cooked cabbage and pork inside same unit. Corrected; food items were rearrange to prevent cross

uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Damp wipe cloths are stored on food prep table without adequate chlorine sanitizer residual, 0 ppm measured.
Sanitizer buckets were not set up. Corrected; sanitizer buckets were prepared during inspection. Note: Multi-use wipe cloths must
be stored in sanitizer solution when not in-use.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Prep sink lack adequate air gap above floor sink. Provide 1" air gap within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
