#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Diced tomatoes 59F, garlic mixed in with butter and oil 55F-56F, and rice 52F stored on top of ice bath at cook's
line. Per chef, some ice melted already. Food items at ice bath should be submerged in ice bath and level of food shall be same as
level of ice. Do not overfill these containers; stored about 3/4 or 1/2 insert full. Corrected during inspection.
- Container with garlic and oil 77F stored out on the counter top at cook's line. Per chef, this food has been out for about 1 hour.
Container was placed inside cooler.
Minor Violation:
- Sliced tomato (half) stored on top of cold top insert at 48F. Do not store any food items on top of cold top inserts. Store inside
insert or at reach-in cooler. Corrected.
- Whipped cream 47F stored inside 4-door beer cooler. Container was relocated inside kitchen cooler.
Potentially hazardous foods may not be stored at room temperature unless time as a public health control (TPHC) is used. Store
foods at/below 41F or at/above 135F.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Old food residue build up on meat slicer. Clean out meat slicer, rinse and sanitizer today before use. REPEAT
VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Automatic dish machine was dispensing sanitizer below 50 ppm (about 10ppm). Air bubble was observed in
chlorine tubing. Chef tried to adjust chlorine but level was still below 50ppm chlorine (about 25ppm). Sanitizer level tested at the
plate shall be at least 50 ppm chlorine to sanitize dishware. Chef called EcoLab and they would be in today to repair/adjust dish
machine.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water at automatic dish machine reached 157F. Per manufacture, hot water shall be 140F. Correct within 24
hours. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: - Bottles with juices stored inside bin with condensation beneath bar's 3-comp sink storing beer in ice. All food
must be protected from possible contamination. There should be no food and food equipment stored beneath plumbing. Employee
corrected during inspection.
- Raw chicken, ground meat, raw fish stored haphazardly inside 2-door upright freezer inside dry storage room. Organize as shown
on proper food storage sticker within 24 hours. Sticker was provided. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

- One burnt light tube above warewash sink.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Both restroom doors do not self-close completely. Adjust within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
