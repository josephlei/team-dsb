Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Whipped cream and half & half 43F-44F inside beer cooler. Per monitoring thermometer ambient temperature was
at 45F. Employee relocated dairy to properly functional cooler during inspection. REPEAT VIOLATION
Overfilled container with sliced cheese 43F-44F at cold top cooler. Also, shredded cheese 45F inside 3-door cooler.
Maintain all COLD PHFs at/below 41F at all times.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Old food residue build up on tooth of can opener. Clean immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 22

SEWAGE AND WASTEWATER DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Observed standing water inside floor sink where condensation from ice machine draining into (inside water heater
room). All other floor sink were properly functional and drained properly. Employee called plumber during inspection and they will
be in today working on repair. Fax repair order to EMD (916) 875-8513 attn: Elena within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Liquid waste shall be disposed of through the approved plumbing system that discharges into the public sewerage
or into an approved private sewage disposal system. Grease traps and grease interceptors shall be easily accessible for servicing. (114197,
114201)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: - Small debris build up on shelves storing single use cups/lids and plates, and on metal shelves holding can
opener.
- Residue build up inside beer cooler holding dairy. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Grease residue build up inside ovens.
- Debris build up on the floor beneath ice machine.
Clean and maintain within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Facility is utilizing unapproved scoop without handle (dressing cup) to scoop out croûtons. Provide and use
approved scoop with handle and store handle out of food. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Wet mops nesting inside mop bucket. Install hangers and hang mops to air dry. Correct within 5 days. REPEAT
VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Both rear doors do not self-close. Install/adjust self-closing device within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: - Loose rubber cove base observed beneath dish machine, bar's 3-comp/warewash sink, and near bin storing
soiled cloth towels. Seal with approved material within 30 days. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Holes/gaps observed in wall inside women's restroom, near exhaust hood, and behind dish machine. Seal with approved material
within 21 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
