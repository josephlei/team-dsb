Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700.
All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Mediterranean soup (64F) in the reach-in at the grill side, caramelized onions (53F) and
various sliced cheeses (55-64F) in the grill area cook's line top insert were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all
potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. All items were voluntarily discarded at the time of
inspection.
MINOR VIOLATION - Sliced turkey (41-43F), cream (45F) in walk-in #3 were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all PHF at
proper holding temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Two employee beverages on the cook' s line counter were lacking lids. All employee beverages consumed in
food prep areas must be equipped with lid AND straw at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent
contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to
eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination.
(113977, 114256 (a))

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Staff was observed to cut raw meat with gloved hands and observed to handle a plate of prepared food. No
direct food contact was observed with the contaminated gloved hand. Staff was immediately requested to remove gloves and wash
hands. Provide education to all cook staff regarding proper use of gloves. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw to
ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate hands.
(113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Soap and paper towel dispensers in the dishwashing area was located too far away from each other. Maintain
soap and paper towel together to promote proper handwashing at all times. Corrected.
Handwash station at the cook's line was observed with a few containers stacked near the paper towel dispenser. Do not place any
item in or around the handwash station that may prevent access to soap or paper towel dispensers. Items were removed during the
inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas.
Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and
sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953,
113953.1, 113953.2)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water in the employee restroom/locker rooms was not adequate at 64F. Repair to provide hot water at least
100F available in all handwash stations. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Containers of soup and marinade in walk-in #1 and boxes of nuts and beans in the basement were lacking lids.
Provide cover to all stored containers to prevent from becoming contaminated. Correct within 24 hours. (EXCEPT FOR FOODS
BEING COOLED)
Package of raw beef patties were stored above tri tip in walk-in #3. Store food items according to required cooking temperature
requirement to prevent cross contamination. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Spray bottle of chemical under the 2-comp sink in the banquet side and one spray bottle with blue liquid in the
bar were lacking an accurate label. Provide label to all containers with chemical within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination of
food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food facility
may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall be
clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Many containers of food (especially in the basement) were lacking labels (including those for employee
consumption only). Provide label to all containers with food item including spray and squeeze bottles in the cook's line and food.
Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor under the cook's line and under the dessert area counter was in need of cleaning due to an accumulation of
debris. Clean floor within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Cloth towel was observed on the belt loop of the one of the kitchen staff. Cease this practice immediately.
Utilize handwash station to wash contaminated hands. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Wet wiping cloth stored on the counter in the dessert area. Store all wet wiping cloth in sanitizer at all times
when not in use. Correct immediately.
Sanitizer in the dessert area was observed at less than 100ppm quat ammonia. Solution was changed at the time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Personal beverages are stored on or above the food prep areas. Cease this practice immediately. Store personal
items under or away from all food and food related areas/surfaces at all times to prevent cross contamination. Correct
immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers or
other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Holes/gaps observed above "coving" in the dessert area under the counter. Seal these gaps to prevent vermin
entry. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device and
have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food or
utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if required.
(114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: At least three coving tiles have fallen in the dishwashing area including two that displays large opening under the
dishwasher. Repair these tiles within 7 days.
