Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Two food handler certificates are not available. Register to an approved class within 7 days. REPEAT
VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Open beverage observed in the food prep table by the soda dispenser. Provide lid and straw to all employee
beverages at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Cooked rice (45F) in the upright 2-door reach-in cooler across the warewash station, cut beef (46F) in the makeline
reach-in were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature.
Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Cooked spanish rice is stored in a deep container and placed on the counter at room temperature to cool. This is
not an adequate method of cooling. Utilize one or more approved methods of cooling (e.g. shallow uncovered container, ice bath
with constant stirring, adding ice as an ingredient, blast chiller, etc.) to cool within a timely manner. Implement immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Approximately 20 rodent droppings were observed around the pump near the soda syrup boxes. Clean and
sanitize this area immediately. Provide rodent traps as deemed necessary around this area.
Trapped adults and nymph cockroaches observed on glue boards. Facility currently under weekly professional pest control
treatment. No untrapped, live activity was observed after detailed inspection. Provide aggressive treatment within 24 hours to
eliminate a potential infestation. A re-inspection will be performed within 3 days to determine if there is an active infestation
present. Any untrapped live vermin observed within the facility will result in facility closure (temporary suspension of permit) until
the problem has been resolved.

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Four containers of rice and noodles were stored on the table to the left of the 2-door upright reach-in. Store all
potentially hazardous foods (PHF) within a temperature controlled environment at all times to maintain proper holding temperature.
Correct immediately.
Unapproved white plastic ("shirt bags") are used to store food. Cease this practice immediately. Utilize only food grade bags at all
times. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.
Container of raw fish is stored below raw chicken in the upright freezer in the storage room. Store all food items accordingly to
prevent cross contamination. Correct immediately.
Broom heads (brush of a broom) were stored on top of bags of dried chili on the rack in the storage room. Store all non-food items
under or away from food items at all times to prevent cross contamination. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Spray bottle of unknown chemical (purple liquid) near the wooden table and spray bottle (clear liquid) above
buckets of rice/flour buckets were lacking labels. Provide labels to all container with chemical within 24 hours. REPEAT
VIOLATION. Store all containers of chemical under or away from all food and food related items. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Facility is in need of deep cleaning throughout the facility. Heavy grease build up is observed throughout all
cooking areas. Excessive food debris is observed on rack below the steam table, under the racks in the storage area, and
throughout all food preparation areas. Clean these heavily grease laden areas and food build up areas within 48 hours. A
re-inspection will be performed as a follow up to determine compliance. Failure to comply will result in another re-inspection or
facility closure.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

OBSERVATIONS: Two of three hood filters are in need of cleaning due to oil build-up. Clean these filters immediately.

light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Wet mop is stored in a bucket. Hang mop after use to allow drying. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)
