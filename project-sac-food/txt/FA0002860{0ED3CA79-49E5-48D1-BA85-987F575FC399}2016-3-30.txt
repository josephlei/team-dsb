FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: One Food handler card could not be located today. Provide organized accessible system for maintaining food
handler cards. Correct 1 week.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Handwash sink has "Broken" sign posted above it. Sink is functional, but leaking. Handsink must always be
accessible, available and functional. Sign removed. If sink CANNOT be used, provide temporary handwash set up at alternate sink.
Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Liquid egg in container in prep table insert: 44F
Ham on prep table at 46F
Pastrami on prep table at 50F
Chicken salad on prep table at 50F
Tuna on prep table 49F
Cream cheese in container for self service at 46F
Cut melon cup for self service at 47F
Maintain all cold potentially hazardous foods at or below 41F at all times.
Note: ambient air temperature is 79F. Many refrigeration units require cooler ambient air temperatures to maintain proper food
temperatures.
Temperatures will be checked at reinspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Prep area space on prep refrigerator has large quantity of debris. When wiped, towel contained 0 ppm sanitizer.
Ensure that prep surfaces are maintained clean and wiped only with towels containing sanitizer. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: No hot water available at prep sink. Repair hot water supply to this sink. Correct 30 days. EMD plan review must

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

Laminate has peeled away in many areas leaving uncleanable surfaces. Counter is bowing/cracking and has moisture damage.
Replace this counter and cabinet with approved commercial equipment. Ensure that handsink remains in current location. Sink
may be replaced, but must remain in same location and be operational whenever facility is open.
Reinspection will be conducted in approx 30 days to verify correction.
Provide e mail within 5 days outlining correction plans. Talusikj@saccounty.net

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Light is not functional in janitorial closet. Repair so that light is working properly. Correct 2 weeks.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Towel used for wiping counter retrieved from sanitizer bucket. Towels has 0 ppm sanitizer. Sanitizer replaced in
bucket. Towel at 200 ppm QA.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
