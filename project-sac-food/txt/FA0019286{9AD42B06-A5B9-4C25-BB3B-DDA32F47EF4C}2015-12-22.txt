#9

COOLING METHODS

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: Won ton in covered container inside the reach-in refrigerator near the cook's line was observed being improperly
cooled. Per employee, this was cooked yesterday and had not been out of refrigeration but was measured at 49 F using a
thermocouple. Operator discarded the won ton (about 2 lbs) voluntarily during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Tofu and raw chicken inside the undercounter reach-in refrigerator near cook's line were measured at 44 to 45 F
using a thermocouple. Ambient temperature inside the unit was measured at 44 F. Adjust thermostat of the unit to enable it to keep
contents at 41 F or below. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Ice buildup observed on top of food containers coming from leaks inside the walk-in freezer (no evidence of
contamination found). This was temporarily mitigated by putting catch pans under the leaks during the inspection. Correct
condensate leakage issue within 15 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Sensor of built-in monitoring thermometer for the reach-in refrigerator was found installed right in front of one of
the fans and was reading 37 F. Built-in monitoring thermometer for the open AFC display case was reading 26 F (location of sensor
not determined). Code requires monitoring thermometers for refrigeration units to be located at the warmest parts of the units and
where they are easily readable. Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 49

PERMIT REQUIREMENTS

OBSERVATIONS: Posted EMD permit is expired; current permit was not available. Provide and display current EMD permit within 7
days.
