Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Three employees food handler cards are expired or not available. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Hand wash station at bar lacks paper towels. Facility have napkins across hand wash station. Repair paper towels
dispenser within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed the following foods items measured between 45F - 50F:
- shredded cheese at cold top in kitchen
- diced tomatoes at cop top in kitchen
- roast beef at cop top in kitchen
- cooked macaroni, chicken breasts and wings at pull out drawer refrigerator
- marinated chicken breasts and wings inside walk-in refrigerator
- shredded cheese and cream inside walk-in refrigerator
- pork ribs and tri-tip inside walk-in refrigerator
- cooked dice potatoes inside True reach-in refrigerator at salad prep station
Ambient temperature at cold top refrigerator and inside walk-in refrigerator measured at 49F. Ensure all potentially hazardous foods
(PHF) are held cold at 41F or below, or hot at 135F or above. Adjust temperature dial or have unit service so that food will maintain
at 41F or below. Correct today

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: 1. Observed cleaned utensils and uncleaned utensils stored in uncleaned insert. Clean today.
2. Observed uncleaned potato chopper and uncleaned meat slicer. Clean today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Observed grease accumulation on hood filters. Clean within 7 days.
Observed uncleaned bulk food containers and bulk spice containers on cooks line. Clean today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: 1. Ambient temperature inside walk-in refrigerator and cold top refrigerator at cook's line measured at 49F. Repair
or adjust thermostat refrigeration as needed. Correct today.
2. Household ice chest freezer (Haier) in use inside kitchen. Replace with commercial equipment within 60 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Observed dumpster lids open and grease spilled around tallow bin. Always keep lids closed when not being filled
to prevent vermin infestation and possible dirty water (leachate) run-off to storm water drain. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Missing and damaged cove base along wall of dish washer ara, prep table, and along dry storage walls. Floor at
dish washer area are deteriorating and not easily cleanable. Repair within 45 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
