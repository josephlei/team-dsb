Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Two food handler cards are unavailable. REPEAT VIOLATION. Maintain on site at all times. Repeat of this
violation will result in all employees being required to attend mandatory food safety education provided by EMD at a cost. Class
does not satisfy food handler requirement.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

# 5a

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee wipe counter and then put on gloves to prepare a sandwich. Employees must wash their
hands prior to puttinig on gloves, in between different tasks (even if gloves are removed) and/or whenever contaminated (even if
gloves are worn). Employee asked to wash their hands.
Observed employee on two occasions rinse their hands at the prep sink. Hands may only be washed at the handsink in the front
where the soap and paper towels are located. Reviewed with employees.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Cut chicken in paper trays with liners in between observed at 43-47F and jack cheese filled above the fill line
observed at 45F. Paper trays and liners can prevent refrigeration from holding food 41F or below. Stock food below fill lines of
containers to aid in temperature maintenance.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water at 3-comp sink and prep sink only reached 110F. Adjust to provide a minimum of 120F at all times.
Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor below shelving in walk-in freezer unclean in hard to reach areas. Clean within 5 days.
Dust accumulation behind fan grill at condenser unit in walk-in. Clean within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Particle board shelving observed in back area by desk. Seal to make smooth, easily cleanable and non-absorbent
within 30 days. Bare wood or particle board finishes are not approved unless sealed with approved materials.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 37

SATELLITE FOOD SERVICE

OBSERVATIONS: Several cutting boards observed on floor in back area with floor unclean. Food and related equipment must be
stored off of the floor by at least 6 inches at all times. Clean equipment and then store off of floor today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Satellite food service shall be operated by a fully enclosed permanent food facility that meets all requirements for
food preparation and service. The operator of the permanent food facility shall submit written standard operating procedures to the
enforcement agency prior to operation. All food preparation shall be conducted within a food compartment or within a fully enclosed facility.
Satellite food service areas shall have overhead protection that extends over all food handling areas. Satellite food service operations that
handle non-prepackaged food shall be equipped with approved handwashing and warewashing facilities. (114067)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Soda machine waste lines drains to directly connected to handsink in prep area which is a direct connection.
Indirect waste drainage to a floorsink is required for any food related equipment. J. Smalley consulted at EMD. Sale of soda must
be discontinued until drainage can be relocated to an approved floorsink with a minimum of 1-inch air gap. Installation of a floorsink
or any plumbing changes requires permit from city building department and approved plans by EMD plan review. For questions for
plan review, call 874-6010.
Soda residual not draining from pan at soda machine. Clear today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44b

APPROPRIATE USE OF EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Observed employee on two occasions rinse their hands in the prep sink. Whenever hands washing is required,
the handsink only must be used. Preparation sinks are only for the preparation of food and warewash sinks are only for
warewashing of equipment. Review with employees immediately.

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Damaged wall behind door entering kitchen from dining area. Repair and leave finish to be smooth, easily
cleanable and non-absorbent within 14 days.
