#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Chow mein noodles in the back reach in (by back door) at 44 deg. F. Cooked pork in the chef prep on north end at
47 deg. F. Maintain cold potentially hazardous foods at or below 41 deg. F at all times when food is in storage. The chef prep on
the north end was turned down during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Approx. 5 rodent droppings in the back storage area. The monitoring trap is empty. Clean and sanitize this area.
Continue with pest control - note: active rodent infestation is grounds for closure.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: 1. Bulk bins have heavily encrusted scoops. Clean all scoops within 48 hours.
2. Can opener dirty. Corrected by washing at the time of inspection.
3. Raw chicken stored above beff in the freezer. Use the same storage guidelines for freezer as in the refrigerator. Correct within 5
days.
4. Rice scoop, left on counter with rice debris, can grow bacteria and contaminate rice. Store scoop in water with ice, in a solution
of water and a little vinegar, or wash frequently. Correct within 1 day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: 1. The floor behind and below some equipment, especially at the juncture of the wall, where it is difficult to reach,
has accumulation of grease/food debris. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
2. Gaskets, door frames, and interior surfaces of refrigerators have build up of spilled food. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
3. Food debris on the crates, which are difficult to clean. Routinely wash, rinse, sanitize crates or replace with cleanable tables
within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Unapproved scoops (bowls and cups) without handles being used in bulk storage in the kitchen area, stored in
contact with foods such as sugar, salt, flour, backing soda. Use approved scoops with handles and keep handles out of food.
Correct within 3 days (repeat violation)

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Mop sink has faucet-style anti-siphon device and has a hose with a nozzle with controller on the end to turn

water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
