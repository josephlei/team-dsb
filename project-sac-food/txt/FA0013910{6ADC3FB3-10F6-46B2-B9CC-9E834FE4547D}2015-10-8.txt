Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Pieces of uncooked meat being thawed in stagnant water at the meat department. Per store manager meat is
placed in container with water for approx 5 minutes before they are worked on. Use approved thawing methods such as placing in
refrigerator over night to allow item to thaw.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed two plastic containers of frozen meats stored directly on the floor in meat department. No food
container should be stored directly on the floor. Use movable carts to aid in filling containers with water and don't place any food
container on the floor. Corrected at time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Found mop containers with mops stored in various areas of the retail area by the meat department which were not
being used. Store all mops with containers in designated areas (where mop sink is located). Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Produce preparation area and walk in cooler floors are not sealed. Concrete must be sealed using approved
materials. Inspector will discuss options with previous inspectors as well as supervisor to see what options are available to bring
floors in both units and produce preparation area up to current code.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)

NOTES

Re inspection conducted today to verify that major violations observed during October 6, 2015 routine inspection remain corrected. All
major violations remain corrected and no new major violations observed.
The hand wash stations at the meat and seafood department were accessible and fully stocked with soap and paper towels. Education
was provided to store manager to translate to employees the importance of hand washing and maintaining hand sinks fully accessible
and stocked at all times.

tags.
Ground pork (longaniza) was placed in separated sections with guards to prevent cross contamination between whole and ground pork.
