#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Major Violation
-Observed one insert of roast beef (50F-52F), one insert of cooked mushrooms (51F-53F), one insert of caramelized onions (52F),
one insert of sliced turkey (53F) and one insert of sliced tomatoes (54F) in the danger zone at the cold top refrigerator by the pizza
brick oven. Per operator, all above foods were brought out 1.5 hours ago. Packages of ice were stored directly above all above
items during time of inspection.
NOTE: Ambient temperature inside this unit was 42F. Due to unit being so closed to the brick oven, recommend that unit is lowered
to approximately 36F and remain cover to compensate for heat coming from the brick oven. If unit is lowered and food items are still
above 41F, then relocate all items inside the unit and discontinue use of cold top inserts.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Repeat Violation.
-Observed dark food residual on the can opener tooth. Clean before next use. All food contact surfaces must be washed a minimum
of once every 4 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: -Hot water for the dishwasher by the ice machine observed at 102F-108F. Correct within 24 hours. Minimum hot
water for this dishwasher unit must be at 125F or above.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 33

-Dust accumulation observed on the fanguard of the reachin refrigerator below the coffee station.
Clean and maintain all above areas within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: -Air filter section of the pizza two door reachin refrigerator by the wood oven is loose from the unit. Repair or

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: -Furthest right section of filters in the ventilation hood has a half inch gap. Correct so that all filters are flush
fitting within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed monitoring thermometers for the cook's line reachin refrigerator at the back of the unit and blocked by
inserts of food. Corrected during inspection. Thermometers were relocated to the front of the units.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed one side of the dumpster lid open during time of inspection. Correct immediately.
-Observed trash debris on the floor inside the dumpster area. Clean and maintain today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)
