HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Major
Observed mash potatoes at 113-127F in steamtable in cook's line and shredded cheese at 51F in cold top insert at end of cook's
line. Reheated and ice bathed items during inspection.
Minor
Cream at 132F in steamtable in cook's line, soup at 133F in warmer near cook's line, raw chicken and meat at 43-44F in basement
walk-in refrigerator and cheese at 45F in cold top insert of island prep refrigerator. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Observed water at 124F for 1 of 2 handwash sinks in basement women's restroom, 65F for both handwash sinks
in basement men's restroom, 115F for both handwash sinks in men's restroom and 124F for 2 of 3 handwash sinks in women's
restroom. Maintain water at 100-108F for pre-mixed valve sinks. Correct within 24 hours. -Repeat Violation-

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: 10 rodent droppings on lower ledge of ice cream freezer stand near cook's line, 4 droppings on floor next to ice

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
