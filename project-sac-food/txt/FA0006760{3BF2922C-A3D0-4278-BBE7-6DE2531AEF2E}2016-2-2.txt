#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: -Sausage links under heat lamps are at 109-142F. The sausage links on the bottom were not in temperature. Item
was reheated on flat grill to 165F. It was then placed on on flat grill with water below to form a steam table (3rd REPEAT VIOLATION).
-Cooked potatoes at 75-78F. This part of the steam table is not on. Cooks place potatoes there and then place on flat grill to reheat.
Discontinue practice. All potentially hazardous food must be kept hot at/above 135F at all times. Potatoes were reheated on flat grill
to 168F and then placed in container on flat grill to keep hot (2nd REPEAT VIOLATION).
Facility can use Time as a Public Health Control (TPHC). Food can be left at any temperature if properly documented. The discard
time which is 4 hours after food is removed from temperature control must be documented on a log like the one provided or a
sticker can be placed on container stating the discard time. After the 4 hours the food must be discarded. It can not be saved for use
or reheated.
Failure to correct this violation in future routine inspection will result in re-inspections and/or compliance conference; fees apply.
MINOR VIOLATION:
-Whipped cream in server's prep refrigerator at 46F. -Sliced cheddar cheese at cook's line prep refrigerator at 45F. Sliced provolone
at 46F in cook's line prep refrigerator. Keep all cold holding potentially hazardous foods at/below 41F.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Ice scoop stored directly on top of ice machine. Store scoop in a sanitary manner in a container that can be sent
to the dishwasher. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Shatterproof cover missing on ceiling lights in between office and clean dish storage rack. Provide within 14
days.
Inoperable light on cook's line. Replace within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Drainage line from ice machine lacks an air gap. Provide a minimum of one inch from end of pipe and top rim of
floor sink. Correct within 3 days (REPEAT VIOALTION).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Two tiles in between two compartment sink and dishwasher are loose with water pooling under them. Secure tiles
to prevent water buildup. Correct within 60 days.
-FRP around mop sink is coming off the wall. Seal with adhesive and provide corner seal to close gaps. Correct within 60 days.
-Small corner side to the left of walk-in refrigerator door is rough and not easily cleanable. Provide FRP or other smooth material
that is durable and easily cleanable. Correct within 60 days (REPEAT VIOLATION).
-Corner seals for the FRP are missing on most of the walls that form a corner. This open gap is a potential for vermin harborage.
Seal all gaps in FRP within 60 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
