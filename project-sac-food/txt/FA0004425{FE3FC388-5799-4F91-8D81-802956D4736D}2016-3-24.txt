# 11

REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

OBSERVATIONS: Meatballs w/sauce, broccoli cheddar soup, and chicken noodle soup are improperly reheating inside the steam
table at 126F, 128F, and 121F, respectively. Corrected; food items were reheated in microwave to above 165F during inspection.
Note: Precooked food in hermetically sealed packaging must be reheated to a minimum temperature of 135F prior to hot holding; all
other precooked food must be reheated to 165F prior to hot holding.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)

OBSERVATIONS: Employees lack adequate knowledge of food reheating temperatures and probe thermometer calibration.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION

SUBMIT COPY OF CERTIFICATION TO EMD VIA FAX ATTN: TOUA YANG TO AVOID REINSPECTION. (REPEAT VIOLATION-4TH

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee
who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Produce slicer and chopper stored on the clean dish storage rack are unclean with food debris. Thoroughly clean
and sanitize food processing equipment today. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Water in men's and women's restroom measured 118F; sinks have auto run faucets without cold/hot water
adjustment for user. Adjust sinks to provide water between 100F-108F to prevent scalding. Correct within 24 hours. (REPEAT
VIOLATION-2ND NOTICE)

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: A spray bottle storing degreaser is stored above an open box of avocados. Relocate chemical bottle away from
food storage today. Note: Food contamination was not observed.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Several whole onions are stored directly on the kitchen floor. Corrected. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: White and pink build-up is observed on exterior side of soda machine ice chute. Clean and sanitize today.
(REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Back door is missing door knob. Replace within 7 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Large CO2 cylinder is not secured. Secure cylinder to a rigid structure to prevent from tipping over. Correct within
2 days. Note: A chain used to secure cylinder is available, but not in-use. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: The 2-door reach-in cooler is missing a thermometer. Provide within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Toilet in women's restroom is inoperable. Repair within 72 hours. Note: Until repaired, the men's restroom shall be
a unisex restroom.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Back door is missing a self-closing device. Provide within 3 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION-4TH NOTICE)

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Several cove base tiles area detached from wall behind the 3-compartment warewash sink. Re-attach tiles using
proper grout so that no gap is observed between tile and wall. Correct within 14 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION-2ND NOTICE)
Fiberglass reinforced wall panel is detached from sheet rock near back door. Re-attach wall panel and seal gap within 14 days.
(REPEAT VIOLATION-2ND NOTICE)
