# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed pot of lard stored on floor in prep walk-in cooler. Keep all food items stored 6" off floor. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged food
may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health

NOTES

Reinspection conducted to follow up on repeated major violation observed during reinspection conducted on 1/12/16. No cooling violations
were observed. Facility has purchased multiple 2" deep metal hotel pans to be used for cooling.
Ambient temperature of walk-in unit measured 39F. Recommend removing items from upper shelf, directly below top shelf inside walk-in
cooler near condenser to a lower level. Place sheet pans on top shelf to provide overhead protection and use shelf directly below for
cooling all items. Do not tightly cover any item until it has reached 41F/below. Use probe thermometer to monitor all cooling potentially
hazardous foods (rice, beans, meats, etc.) to ensure items cool from 135F to 70F within 2 HOURS and then from 70F to 41F/below within 4
HOURS.
Supervisor Z. Ruiz translated in Spanish this procedure over the phone to the manager on duty on 1/12/16. The above set up of the walk-in
was also demonstrated and set up by inspector on 1/12/16 and explained again on 1/13/16.
Other major violations observed during routine inspection conducted on 1/11/16 also remain corrected. Minor violations have been
corrected except those noted above.
