Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Deep containers with cheese and meat ravioli 43F-55F, stuffed pasta 46F-51F stored on top of ice bath. Per
operator, he placed food on ice during lunch time. Operator placed food inside upright cooler. Container with food must be
submerged in ice and level of ice shall be same as level of food.
Minor Violation:
- Lemon cream spread 46F, blue cheese dressing 49F, ranch 46F-47F stored on top of ice. Submerge containers with food in ice.
Correct immediately.
- Blue cheese crumbles 43F inside reach-in cooler across from steam table. At time of arrival this cooler door observed to be
slightly open. Door was closed during inspection.
Maintain all cold food items at/below 41F at all times.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Old food residue build up on meat slicer. Clean and sanitize meat slicer today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Observed chicken thawing improperly inside tub with water. This practice is not allowed by law. Potentially
hazardous food must be thawed in an approved method: under refrigeration, under cold running water while submerged in water, in
microwave, or during the cooking process. Corrected by moving tub with chicken inside glass door cooler. Thawing sticker was
provided.

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: - Container with hot water for pasta cooking was stored inside handwash/prep sink and handwashing sink was
used for handwashing. (Note: During last inspection noodles also were observed draining inside handwash sink.) All food must be
protected from contamination during storage and preparation. Handwash sink must be clear and easily accessible at all times.
Further non-compliance will require separate handwash sink or prep sink installation with required permits. Warewash sink use for
preparing foods was discussed as an option after properly washing and sanitizing before each food prep.
- Ice for beverages at bar storage bin stored directly next to cooling device tubes. Provide separation to prevent ice from
contamination. Correct by placing ice in a bucket. Install separation within 14 days. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Large pot with ground pork with spices was stored next to soiled dishes at kitchen area. All food prep must be conducted away
from contamination area. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Unlabeled spray bottles with orange/pink residue stored on metro shelf behind 3-door glass cooler. Label all spray
bottles today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: - Dust build up on ceiling vents and light tubes at cook's line.
- Old food residue and debris build up on the floor beneath all equipment especially beneath ice machine, pizza reach-in cooler,
cooking equipment, dish machine, 3-door reach-in coolers and storage shelves (by handwash sink).
- Dust build up on shelves storing cups.
- Rusty water standing inside beer cooler.
- Old food residue build up inside oven.
Clean and maintain 5 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed gap beneath rear door that was larger than 1/4 inches. Eliminate gap to prevent vermin entry within 14
days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: - Found gaps in wall beneath dish machine, and next to two 3-door upright glass coolers. Also, deteriorated wall
observed on the left side of 3-door glass coolers. Seal all holes/gaps within approved material within 30 days. REPEAT VIOLATION
- More than 25% of flooring observed to be deteriorated so it cannot be properly cleaned throughout kitchen/warewash (exposed
concrete) and pizza prep area (deteriorated vinyl). Also, deteriorated flooring vinyl at dry storage room storing beer and carpet
flooring observed by ice machine. Approved flooring must be provided within one year - handout provided. Provide estimates within
3 months.
- Cardboard covering hole in ventilation hood. Provide approved cover within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
