HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS:
Observed half & half (57F) stored in carafe at self-service counter. Item was discarded at the time of inspection.
Observed 2% milk (68F) stored on counter top above drive-thru reach-in #3 (see #27). Item was relocated to reach-in.
NOTE: Facility utilizes a 30 minute timer for the Customer Support Cycle, in which levels of condiments are observed every 8, 10, 15
and 30 minutes, depending on business. According to employee, temperature of creamer inside carafe is not being monitored and
carafe is only being changed when it is low or out of product.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Observed a total of a couple dozen drain flies in back area next to dry storage racks, in drive-thru next to floor
sinks and in men's restroom. Eliminate flies immediately.
NOTE: Facility last received treatment for flies by Copesan pest services on 12/17/15. According to manager, a fog knockdown
service has been requested but not yet scheduled. According to manager, facility is cleaning floor drains 3 times per day to address
the problem. Highly recommend using a 2-3 ft drain brush to scrub the inside of pipes to disrupt any biofilm that is used as a
breeding site for flies. Facility may also make traps to catch adult flies using a small cup, soy sauce, and plastic wrap as discussed.
Keep all traps below/away from food or food related items.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Facility is in the habit of leaving several gallons of milk products on the counter during and between orders. Keep
items refrigerated when not in use so that 41F/below is maintained for all potentially hazardous foods. Correct immediately.
<<REPEAT VIOLATION>>

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))
