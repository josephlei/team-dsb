Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed watery soap that is nearly empty in pump-style bottle of soap. REPEAT VIOLATION. Provide liquid hand
soap in pump-style bottle today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed packaged, refrigerated noodles in refrigerator at 43 F. Refrigerator (2-door upright) is holding food
items at 44 F. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed spray bottle of chemical being stored on top of drinks in storeroom. Discontinue storing chemicals with
food. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 32a

FOOD PRESENTATION AND LABELING

OBSERVATIONS: Observed unlabeled packaged whole chickens and fish in reach-in freezer. Owner provided receipts/invoices for
food items. Owner states he buys chickens and fish and reportions meat into individual plastic bags. Provide label on bags that
states the following information:
* food item (e.g. fish)
* name of facility item purchased from (e.g. Lao Market)
* business City, State, Zip Code
* business phone number
Correct today. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food offered for human consumption shall be honestly presented in a way that does not mislead or misinform the
consumer. Food packaged in a food facility shall bear a label that complies with the labeling requirements prescribed by the Sherman Food,
Drug, and Cosmetic Law which include: common name of the food, ingredients list, quantity of contents, name and address of
manufacturer, nutritional information as specified in 21 C.F.R. (114087, 114088, 114089, 114089.1(a, b), 114090, 114093.1)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Observed spider webs behind produce case. Clean and maintain within 2 days.

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed 2-door upright refrigerator holding potentially hazardous foods such as prepackaged cooked noodles
foods at 44 F. Correct today so that foods are held at 41 F or less.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Observed toilet paper being stored on top of wall-mounted dispenser. Store toilet paper in dispenser. Corrected
on site.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Observed wet mop head in bucket. Store mop upright to facilitate drying. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 49

PERMIT REQUIREMENTS

OBSERVATIONS: Observed container of pickles with salt and long kebab sticks. Facility is not permitted to sell open food items.
Discontinue selling pickles. Pickles removed from sale during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall not be open for business without a valid permit. Permit shall be posted in a conspicuous place
within the food facility. (114067(b, c), 114381, 114387)
