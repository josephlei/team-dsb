Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Toilet paper in the men's restroom was not available. Toilet paper must be provided in all restrooms at all times.
Correct immediately. Provided during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Staff does not check food items that are reheated before determining food items are reheated. Reheated food
items must reach at least 135F before placing it in a hot holding unit for sale. Correct immediately. Verify with a probe thermometer
(that has been calibrated to 32F in heavily iced water).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 1b

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION

OBSERVATIONS: Food safety certificate could not be located during the inspection. A current certificate must be available for one
employee. Register to an approved class within 14 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.
NOTE: If this certificate is not available at the next routine inspection, a compliance conference will be scheduled (charged at
approximately $400).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee
who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Diced tomatoes in the condiment top insert was out of proper holding temperature at 43F. Maintain potentially
hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: Pizza, mini tacos, chicken wings and mozzarella tacos in the hot case had already expired but had not been
discarded. Staff stated that the food items were no longer good for service. It was discarded during the inspection. These food
items must be discarded once the time has expired and removed from the display case immediately. Failure to comply will result in
a MAJOR VIOLATION.

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water in the men's restroom is too hot at 116F. Adjust temperature to dispense between 100-108F at all times.
Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Containers of bagged chips are stored on the floor near the men's restroom. Store all food items at least 6" off the
floor at all times. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor under the self serve beverage stations is in need of cleaning due to an accumulation of debris and beverage
covers. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Probe thermometer could not be located at the time of inspection. Provide thermometer 0-220F within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 47c

INSPECTION REPORT AVAILABILITY

OBSERVATIONS: Copy of the most recent routine inspection report could not be located during the inspection. Correct
immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A copy of the most recent routine inspection report conducted to assess compliance with this part shall be
maintained at the food facility and made available upon request. The food facility shall post a notice advising consumers that a copy of the
most recent routine inspection report is available for review by any interested party. (113725.1)
