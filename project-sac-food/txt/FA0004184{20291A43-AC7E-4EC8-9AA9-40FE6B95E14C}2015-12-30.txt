#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed 3 packages of deli meats inside the one door refrigerator next to the sliding door refrigerator at 47F.
Temperature of unit was lowered during time of inspection. If unit is unable to maintain all potentially hazardous foods at 41F and
below, relocate all potentially hazardous foods to working unit in the ware washing area.
-Observed one package of chicken at 49F storing at room temperature above the back one door refrigerator. Chicken was relocated
into the refrigerator during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: -Faucets at the three compartment sink was removed. Per operator, faucets were leaking and a work order has
been put in with the maintenance man for repair. Correct within 24 hours. In the meantime, obtain hot water from the nearby hand
wash sink which is dispensing hot water at 121F for warewashing. Facility has extra clean utensils if needed.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: -Dust accumulation observed on the ventilation hood above the back one door reachin refrigerator. Clean and
maintain within 48 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: -Ambient temperature inside the front one door refrigerator observed at 49F. Temperature of the unit was lowered
during time of inspection.
-Ambient temperature inside the glass sliding door refrigerator observed at 48F. Replace or repair unit within 7 days.
NOTE: Eggs storing in this unit were observed at 43F. Per operator, eggs have been in this unit since 9am (4 hours ago). Operator
believes unit may be defrosting. Relocate raw shell eggs into the back one door refrigerator if this unit is not able to maintain all
potentially hazardous food at 41F or below. If only raw shell eggs are stored here, unit may be at 45F or below.
-Observed paper towels being used to line clean trays storing utensils. Discontinue practice immediately and replace paper towels
with a smooth non absorbent material within 48 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
