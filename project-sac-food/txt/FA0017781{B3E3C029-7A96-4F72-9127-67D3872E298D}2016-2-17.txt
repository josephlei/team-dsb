HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following food items are stored out of proper holding temperature:
-barbecue pork bao in heated case at 118F-121F
-curry chicken bao in heated case at 124F
Corrected; food items were reheated in oven to above 165F during inspection. Thermostat for heated food case was adjusted. Per

may be conducted to verify that heated food case can effectively maintain food temperature at or above 135F. (REPEAT
VIOLATION-2ND NOTICE)
Minor violation:
-chicken feet dimsum on steam table at 130F
-raw liquid egg in large cold top at 45F

containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Observed a large tightly covered bowl of cooked chicken (4"-6" deep) improperly cooling inside the walk-in
refrigerator at 98F; per operator, food has been cooling for 1 hour. Corrected; food was portioned into shallow containers and
placed back inside walk-in refrigerator to facilitate rapid cooling.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

# 13

FOOD IN GOOD CONDITION, SAFE, AND UNADULTERATED

OBSERVATIONS: Heavy mold-like growth is observed on a plate of food inside the small reach-in cooler. Corrected; food was
voluntarily discarded by operator during inspection.
Black build-up is observed on plastic baffle plate inside ice machine. Clean and sanitize within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not commit any act that may cause the contamination or adulteration of food or food contact
surfaces. Food shall be produced, prepared, stored, transported, and served, so as to be free from adulteration and spoilage. Food is
considered adulterated if it bears or contains any poisonous or deleterious substance that may render it impure or injurious to health.
Containers previously used to store poisonous or toxic materials shall not be used to store food, utensils, or single-use articles. Utensils
used for tasting shall be discarded or washed, rinsed, and sanitized between tastings. Ice used as a medium for cooling food and
beverages shall not be offered for consumption. Food shall be inspected upon receipt and prior to any use, storage, or resale. (113967,
113976, 113980, 113988, 113990, 114035, 114167, 114254.3)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Old meat debris is observed on meat slicer blade housing. Clean and sanitize immediately. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

Open containers of canned bamboo, sauce, and broths are stored in reach-in coolers uncovered. Keep food containers covered to
prevent contamination. Correct today. (REPEAT VIOLATION)
Raw chicken is stored above raw shrimp in the walk-in freezer. In addition, raw chicken is stored above lotus leaf rice wraps in
same unit. Rearrange food according to EMD food storage handout to prevent cross contamination. Correct within 2 days. (REPEAT
VIOLATION)
