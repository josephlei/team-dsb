#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Garlic in oil (67F) in the back prep line was out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all potentially hazardous
foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. This item was voluntarily discarded.
Many food items in the cook's line were out of proper holding temperature ranging from 44-57F due to gaskets throughout cold
drawers being replaced during the inspection. Maintain PHF at proper holding temperature at all times. Ambient temperature
measured at/below 41F within these units.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Men's handwash station lacked paper towels in the dispenser. Keep dispenser adequately filled at all times.
Kitchen handwash station has an indequately covered paper towel dispenser. Provide dispenser that is adequately covered to
prevent contaminating the paper towels. Correct within 7 days.
Handwash station by the dishwashing area is equipped with a strainer causing the water to pool within the sink. Remove or
replace the strainer to prevent water from filling the handsink at this area. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas.
Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and
sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953,
113953.1, 113953.2)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Trays of hot food items are being placed below cooled food items on the cooling rack. Store hot foods above all
cold or cooling foods to prevent warming such food items. Implement immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as
an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002,
114002.1)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water in the restroom handwash stations are too hot. These units that lack separate hot and cold water
valves must dispense water between 100-108F. Water in these stations peaked at 117F. Adjust temperature of these units. Correct
within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: One large fly was observed hovering in the dry storage area. Eliminat this fly within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Ground meat was stored above steaks in the walk-in cooler. Store food items accordingly to prevent cross
contamination. Correct immediately.
Open bag of sugar and bread spices were observed in the storage room. Store all open packages of food items in container with a
tight fitting lid. Correct within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Spray bottles of oils and water in the cook's line and container of prosciutto were lacking labels. Provide labels
to all containers with food items within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Gaskets for cold drawers were being replaced throughout the kitchen causing the temperature of the PHF items
to rise. Perform repairs to the refrigeration units when food items have been removed to maintain proper holding temperature.
Implement immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: One hood light fixture above the grill was lacking a light bulb, light fixture under the pizza oven and inside the
dessert refrigerators were not functional and light fixture in the prep hood is lacking a shatter proof light guard. Repair these light
fixtures within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient ventilation
to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or light-switch
activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in areas where open
food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with light shields. (114149,
114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)
