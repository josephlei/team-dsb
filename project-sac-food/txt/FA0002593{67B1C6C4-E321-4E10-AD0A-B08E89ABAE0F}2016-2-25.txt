#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Container was labeled 2/24 @2pm with meatballs 55F-57F inside walk-in cooler. Two employees were not aware
why meatballs were reading high and were unable to answer any questions regarding this product. Food was voluntarily discarded
during inspection. (Note: By the end of inspection employee found out that food was mislabeled with date since currently used
meatballs had same label, and likely food was defrosted today before being placed inside walk-in cooler.)
Minor Violation:
- Container with meatballs 131F-133F on top layer. Stir food more often to ensure temperature is at/above 135F at all times.
Corrected. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Sliced tomatoes 46F, swiss cheese overfilled 47F, chicken strips and sliced roast beef 43F at cold top cooler of makeline. Most
other food items were stored at/below 42F. Ensure cold food items stored at/below 41F at all times.
NOTE: Multiple food items in various coolers measured 42F. Turn thermostats of all coolers to colder setting today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: - Black residue build up on soda nozzles.
- Old vegetable residue build up on cutter.
Clean properly and maintain today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)
