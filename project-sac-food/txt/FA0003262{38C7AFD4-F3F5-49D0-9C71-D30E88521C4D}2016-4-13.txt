# 11

REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

OBSERVATIONS: Observed food reheated on the stove and then transfered to the steam table at the following temperatures:
Beans 127 deg. F to 150 deg. F
Chicken in red sauce at 138 deg. F
(Enchilada sauce at 134 deg. F)
Heat food to 165 deg. F PRIOR to putting on the steam table to eliminate all bacteria that may have grown during the cooling step
last night. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Grilled chicken in the True reach in "Freezer" used as a refrigerator in the back at 56 deg. F. Other grilled
chickens at 49 deg. F. Maintain cold potentially hazardous foods at or below 41 deg. F. NOTE: other foods in this refrigerator met
standard. This is a process problem - remove only what is needed to fill inserts to keep remainder cold.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: 1. Ice machine has slight pink mold/build up on the white plate. Clean within 14 days.
2. Plastic wares for take out are stored with the mouth portion up and handle down. Protect the area that the food and mouth
contacts by putting handles up.
3. Repeat violation: Observed one refrigerator (True "Freezer" in back) with raw eggs above sour cream. Keep ready-to-eat foods
ABOVE raw animal products to protect from cross contamination if an egg breaks. Correct within 2 day.
4. Blue take-out bags used in direct contact with frozen meats in the freezer. These are not food-grade. Use food-grade wrappings
only.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Repeat violation: Hose on the mop sink faucet has a spray head controller. Faucet has an atmospheric vacuum
breaker. Place an approved anti-siphon device between the hose and the faucet OR remove the hose after each use. See handouts
provided at last inspection, 12/10/15 to aid in correcting this violation. Correct within 7 days.

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL
