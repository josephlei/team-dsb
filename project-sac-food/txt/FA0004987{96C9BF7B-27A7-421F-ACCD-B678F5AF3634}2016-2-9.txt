#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Two trays covered with chow mein noodles 60F and one bag with sliced bbq pork 67F stored on bottom shelf at
cook's line without time or temperature control. Per employees, they forgot to place these containers back inside reach-in cooler.
Food was voluntarily discarded during inspection.
Minor Violation: Pooled eggs 44F and chow mein noodle 43F inside reach-in cooler. All other food at this cooler was 41F or below.
Note: Found egg rolls 144F stored inside fryer basket without temperature or time control.
Do not store food items on the counter top without temperature control. Bring out on a needed bases and store rest in refrigerator
unit. Store foods at/below 41F or at/above 135F. TPHC handout provided & discussed with operator.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Employee beverage was stored on top shelf above cold top cooler. All employee beverages must be stored below
food prep areas. Corrected. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Found covered container with cooked beef 76F improperly cooling on the counter top of cook's line (on top of
cutting board). Cooked food items must be rapidly cooled in shallow uncovered container and must be cooling in a cooler location
(away from wok). Food items shall be cooled properly in approved manner and location or facility shall place food on TPHC log if
stored on the counter top and used for food prep. Corrected. REPEAT VIOLATION
Note: Further noncompliance may result in a temperature violation and food may need to be discarded. TPHC in Cantonese and
education was provided. Per operator, he will keep cooling meats separately from meats that are in use during lunch time and will
start using TPHC.
Cooked chicken was just cooked 166F and was placed to cool in shallow tray beneath fan.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

# 13

FOOD IN GOOD CONDITION, SAFE, AND UNADULTERATED

OBSERVATIONS: Found two severely dented cans with vegetable and soy sauce on top shelf above 3-comp sink. Employee
removed during inspection. Handout on how to inspect dented cans was provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not commit any act that may cause the contamination or adulteration of food or food contact
surfaces. Food shall be produced, prepared, stored, transported, and served, so as to be free from adulteration and spoilage. Food is
considered adulterated if it bears or contains any poisonous or deleterious substance that may render it impure or injurious to health.
Containers previously used to store poisonous or toxic materials shall not be used to store food, utensils, or single-use articles. Utensils
used for tasting shall be discarded or washed, rinsed, and sanitized between tastings. Ice used as a medium for cooling food and
beverages shall not be offered for consumption. Food shall be inspected upon receipt and prior to any use, storage, or resale. (113967,
113976, 113980, 113988, 113990, 114035, 114167, 114254.3)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Improper food storage:
- Raw chicken stored above cream cheese wontons inside 1-door freezer
- Pooled eggs stored above open cans with vegetables at reach-in cooler
- Raw chicken stored above vegetables inside 2-door upright cooler.
Store food in the following order from bottom to top: raw chicken, raw beef, raw pork, seafood, vegetables then ready to eat food.
Proper food storage sticker was provided. Corrected.
Open container with tofu and vegetables stored inside several coolers. All food shall be stored covered to prevent from potential
contamination. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: - Rice scoopers stored inside stagnant water 54F-72F next to steam table and rice cooker. Store in-use utensils at
or below 41F (in ice) or in ph adjusted water 4.6 or below (vinegar). Per operator, vinegar was in use but pH was measure 7.0 during
inspection. Corrected. Obtain pH test strips to measure vinegar concentration within 7 days.
- Tongs hanging on oven handle. Also, cutting board stored behind faucet of 3-comp sink. Store all equipment in hygienic manner.
Correct today. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Food residue build up on knives and knife rack. Store only clean equipment. Clean immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Loose ceiling light fixture above water heater. Repair in approved manner within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Both restroom doors do not self-close completely. Repair to self-close within 5 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.

local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Found several holes/gaps in rear exit door that were greater than 1/4 inch. Seal gaps/holes with approved material
to prevent vermin entry. Correct within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: - Found holes/gaps on ceiling in multiple locations especially above reach-in cooler, above dry storage shelf, etc.)
Also, holes/gaps observed in wall beneath handwash sink, dish machine, by reach-in cooler, etc. One gap was closed with tapped
cardboard. Seal all holes/gaps with approved material within 30 days. (Note: Expanding foam is not approved. All surfaces must be
washable.) REPEAT VIOLATION
- Chipped/broken/missing floor tiles observed beneath fryer, coolers and freezer. Some flooring had exposed concrete. Provide
approved flooring within 3 months. Flooring handout was provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
