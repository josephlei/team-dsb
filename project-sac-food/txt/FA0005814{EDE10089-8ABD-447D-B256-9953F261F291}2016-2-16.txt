#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Measured the following potentially hazardous foods out of proper holding temperature:
-precooked turkey inside Traulsen branded refrigerator closest to front area at 46-48F.
This unit had an ambient temperature of 45-46F and all potentially hazardous foods were moved into the working Traulsen
refrigerator. Only items left in above mentioned refrigerator were whole raw shell eggs and un open milk. (SEE #35 FOR DETAILS).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: Found ham and cheese croissants at the front display area that lacked Time as a Public Health Control (TPHC) tag
(per owner, the front batch is the same as what is on the pans in back area which did have time stamp). Post time stamp on all items
under Time as a Public Health Control.
(Ham and cheese croissants are the only potentially hazardous food under TPHC).

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be
marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within
the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was
used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found whole raw shell eggs stored over ready to eat foods inside both Traulsen branded reach in refrigerators.
Store eggs at bottom shelf today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: The following areas were observed unclean:
-bottom shelf of front area reach in refrigerator
-all along the coving and floor starting from the entrance into the back preparation area and going all around.
-mold like residue observed on bottom shelf of non used True branded reach in refrigerator.
Clean and maintain all areas within 24 hours. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
