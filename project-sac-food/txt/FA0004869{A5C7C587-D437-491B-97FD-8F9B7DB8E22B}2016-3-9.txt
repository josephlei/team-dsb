#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION:
Alla pana sauce on steam table was measured from 107 to 143 F using a thermocouple. Cook stated that he reheated this to 165 F
before putting it on the steam table about an hour before. All other items on the steam table were temped at 1576 to 170; hot water
under the pans was measured at 162, F. Alla pana sauce was reheated to over 165 F during th inspection.
Minor Temperature Holding Violations:
a. Shredded cheeses and sausage in inserts on top of larger cold top at cook's line were 46 to 47 F. Ambient temperatures inside
the unit were at 40 to 41 F. Thermostat was adjusted during the inspection.
b. Sauces in inserts on top of smaller cold top at cook's line and shrimps and mussels inside the is unit were measured at 44 to 48
F. Thermostat was also adjusted during the inspection. Manager that this unit ices up when adjusted too low and they are trying to
find the correct setting.
c. Pastas inside the one-door upright refrigerator were temped at 44 to 46 F.
d. Potentially hazardous food (PHF) items inside the walk-in refrigerator, including the tri tip, scallops, fish and pastas were at 46 to
49 F. Ambient temperature inside the unit was measured at 48 F. Door to adjoining walk-in freezer was temporarily propped open to
get the ambient temperature down.
- Their refrigeration technician was called and was already on his way to the facility at end of inspection. Have the refrigeration
technician test and/or service the refrigeration units mentioned above.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Spray bottle of all-purpose cleaner was found stored above and beside food contact items in cabinet under the
front counter. Chemicals shall be stored separately from food, utensils, packing material and food-contact surfaces. Corrected
during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Ice cream scoops were found in cloudy water at 70 F. Ice scoops were put in ice-filled container during the
inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Monitoring thermometer in larger cold top at cook's line was defective. Provide functioning thermometer and
situate at the warmest part of the unit and where it is easily readable. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Gap of more than Â¼ inch observed at lower corner of rear side door providing possible entry for vermin. Correct
within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
