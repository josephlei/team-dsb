Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Vegetable broth (used as a soup base) on the stove (not turned on) was out of proper
holding temperature at 116F. Packages of thawed/portioned flat noodles on the bottom rack across the cook's line handwash
station were out of proper holding temperature at 48-54F. Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding
temperature at all times. Noodles were placed in the walk-in cooler and vegetable broth was reheated during the inspection.
MINOR VIOLATION - Sliced tomatoes (48F) above top inserts, cooked chicken (48F) in the makeline top inserts, cooked chicken
(50F) in the cook's line reach-in were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain PHF at proper holding temperature at all times.
Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Open employee beverage observed in the server's line to the right of the handwash station and across the
dishwasher. All employee beverages must be equipped with lid and straw. Provide proper employee beverage container
immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

OBSERVATIONS: Employee was observed to adjust hair to put on a hat and proceeded to his job in the dishwashing area. Hands
must be washed with soap and dried prior to handling food and food related items such as clean plates. Employee was requested to
wash hands immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Cooked sliced white meat chicken is still being cooled in a deep container inside the cook's line reach-in. Utilize
shallow containers in a large refrigerator to cool within a timely manner. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Edge of the black deflector inside the ice machine is displaying an accumulation of slime. Clean and sanitize this
area within 24 hours. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Many bags of frozen food items are left on side of the sink in the dishroom remain at room temperature while
thawing. Cease this practice immediately. Utilize on of the four approved thawing procedure immediately. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Container of sliced tomatoes are stored above the top insert. Such practice will not allow proper holding
temperature to be maintain proper holding temperature. Maintain container directly in contact with the cold source. Transfer
tomatoes inside top insert containers to maintain proper holding temperature.
Container of flat noodles stored on the mobile cart observed without any temperature or time control. Utilize one of these two
methods to maintain food safety within 7 days.

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Chemicals are observed on rack to the right of the side entry door into the restroom hallway. Store all chemicals
under or away from all food and food related items. Correct immediately.
Bottles of soap and jugs of bleach are stored above boxes of oil in the room beyond the dishwashing area. Store all chemicals
under or away from all food and food related items. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Containers of raw fish and container of lemon grass are stored on the walk-in floor. Maintain all potentially
hazardous foods (PHF) at least 6" off the floor at all times. Correct within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Buckets of bulk food items in the hallway across the upright freezer are lacking labels. Provide labels to all
containers wiith food within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor throughout the facility is in need of cleaning especially under the top load freezer in the storage room, under
the rack outside of the storage room (with upright freezer), under the rack with styrofoam soup containers. Clean these areas
immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Chef Mate inside the storage room is domestic grade. Remove this unapproved equipment within 14 days. 2ND

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Clean knives are stored inside an empty canister of bread crumbs. Cease the re-use of absorbent material to
store clean utensils. Utilize only cleanable and non-absorbent material. Remove this container immediately.
Used kitchen utensils (e.g. tongs, ladles, etc.) were stored within two containers with stagnant 75F water with food remnants. Store
utensils in a manner that prevents bacterial growth such as icy water. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Wet wiping cloth was stored on the oven handle. Store all wet wiping cloths in sanitizer at all times when not in
use. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Open dumpster lid observed. Keep lids closed to prevent vermin infestation. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Employee food items are stored above food items for the business in the walk-in cooler, in the upright freezer, in
the glass case refrigerator in the server's line. PROVIDE A SPACE FOR EMPLOYEE FOOD ITEMS under food for the business in
these locations. Food items out of this location will be subject to regulation (i.e. it may be marked as violation and may need to be
discarded). Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.
