REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

MAJOR

OBSERVATIONS: CLAM CHOWDER HELD IN STEAM TABLE INSERT MEASURED AT 107 DEGREES. PER STAFF, IT WAS PLACED
ON THE STEAM TABLE 30 MINUTES AGO. A MICROWAVE WAS USED TO REHEAT IT PRIOR TO HOLDING IT ON THE STEAM
TABLE. IMPROPER REHEATING. ENSURE THAT TEMPERATURE REACHES 165 DEGREES F PRIOR TO PLACING FOOD ITEM ON
THE STEAM TABLE. USE A MONITORING THERMOMETER TO MONITOR FOOD TEMPERATURE WHEN REHEATING. CORRECTED
DURING INSPECTION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)
