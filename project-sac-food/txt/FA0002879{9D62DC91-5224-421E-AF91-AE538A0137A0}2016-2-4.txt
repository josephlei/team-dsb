#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Saute onions 95F-118F and saute peppers 130F inside hot bath on the stove. Food items have been reheated
above 165F.
- Overfilled container with sliced cheese 54F-56F inside cold top cooler across from handwash sink. Extra food was removed.
- Tub with whipped butter 55F-56F stored on top of melted ice at server's area. Butter was relocated inside reach-in cooler.
Minor Violation:
- Overfilled container with sliced tomatoes, blue cheese crumbles and feta cheese 46F stored inside cold top cooler across from
handwash sink.
- Overfilled container with grilled chicken 50F, sliced turkey 50F, and boiled eggs 46F stored inside salad prep cold top cooler.
- Overfilled container with alfalfa sprouts 46F stored inside cold top cooler next to flat grill.
- Polenta, chicken breast, diced meat, ham, several containers with pasta were at 48F at reach-in cooler across from stove top. Also,
meatballs, diced sausages, diced ham, chorizo, and chicken 44F-46F at cold top cooler across from stove top. Ambient temperature
of this cooler was 50F-54F. (Also, see violation #35)
All cold potentially hazardous foods must be at/below 41F or at/above 135F at all times to prevent bacterial growth. Keep cold top
lids closed when not in use and keep inserts only ¾ full to help maintain temperature. DO NOT DOUBLE PAN FOOD ITEMS INSIDE
COLD TOP COOLERS. Employee removed extra food from cold top inserts. All food from reach-in cooler across from stove top has
been relocated inside functional coolers and some where submerged inside ice bath.
2ND REPEAT VIOLATION (3rd notification)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: - Food residue build up on meat slicer. Employee started cleaning during inspection.

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Facility had hot water off at kitchen handwash sink. Per employees, they turned hot water off due to leak. Hot
water was turned on during inspection. Repair leak within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 22

SEWAGE AND WASTEWATER DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Missing drainage plumbing for soda gun holsters at bar area. Install with required air gap within 3 days. Do not
store ice for consumption beneath soda gun holster. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Liquid waste shall be disposed of through the approved plumbing system that discharges into the public sewerage
or into an approved private sewage disposal system. Grease traps and grease interceptors shall be easily accessible for servicing. (114197,
114201)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: - Soda gun tube nesting on top of ice for consumption at bar area. Ice was melted and new ice was placed inside
inserts away from contamination. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Raw meats, ground meats and fish was stored haphazardly inside freezer. Corrected during inspection. Proper food storage
sticker was provided. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Unlabeled spray bottles with purple residue were found at bar area. Corrected during inspection. REPEAT
VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)
