#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION
Pierogis with cabbage and potato fillings (measured at 62 to 65 F using a thermocouple) were found on top of deli case with no
temperature or time intervention. Potentially hazardous foods (PHF) shall be kept at 41 F or below, or at 135 F or above. Pierogis (5
pcs) were voluntarily discarded by the operator.
Note: The option of and procedure for using time as a public health control (TPHC) were discussed with the operator. Under TPHC,
PHFs may be kept in the temperature danger zone (42 to 134F) but are discarded after 4 hours. Time logs or stickers are used to
keep track of the times the PHFs have been taken out of temperature control. Handouts on TPHC and the form for preparing written
procedures were also provided.

Minor Violations:
a. PHFs including bologna, rice pilov, potato salad, crepes, dishes with eggplant, chicken, eggs inside the deli case were
measured at 44 to 48 F using a thermocouple. Ambient temperature inside the case was at 46 F.
b. Milk and eggs (also ambient temperature) at the bottom of the two-door upright refrigerator were measured at 48 F.
- Owner arrived at the facility later on and adjusted the thermostats of the units. Ambient temperatures inside the deli case was at
37 F and inside the upright refrigerator was at 40 F at end of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Food worker water bottles with screw top caps observed on food prep counters. Designate approved employee
eating/drinking areas away from food preparation and food storage areas. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Maximum temperature of hot water at warewash sink was measured at 115 F. Provide hot water at warewash sink
at 120 F minimum at all times. Owner adjuster the water heater thermostat. Hot water at 123 F was available at the 3-compartment
sink at end of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Some food containers in main food prep kitchen were not labeled (pointed out to operator). Corrected during the
inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the

# 32a

FOOD PRESENTATION AND LABELING

OBSERVATIONS: Labels of repacked waffles still do not indicate the quantity of the contents and the name and place of the
manufacturer, packer or distributor. Correct within 24 hours.
- Second Repeat Violation -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food offered for human consumption shall be honestly presented in a way that does not mislead or misinform the
consumer. Food packaged in a food facility shall bear a label that complies with the labeling requirements prescribed by the Sherman Food,
Drug, and Cosmetic Law which include: common name of the food, ingredients list, quantity of contents, name and address of
manufacturer, nutritional information as specified in 21 C.F.R. (114087, 114088, 114089, 114089.1(a, b), 114090, 114093.1)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Household-grade microwave oven in use by the facility. Operator stated that this was temporarily brought to the
facility since their commercial unit broke down. Remove from facility or replace with a commercial unit (ANSI-certified) within 4
months.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Tongs for the cabbage rolls found stored inside the container with handle in the product. Scoops shall be stored
in a more sanitary manner to prevent hand contamination. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: One of two probe thermometers was out of calibration (by +5 F). Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Soiled wiping cloths with no sanitizer found on counter tops. Multi-use wiping cloths used to wipe service
counters or other surfaces that may come in contact with food or food contact items shall be kept in clean water with sanitizer.
Prepare wiping cloth sanitizer bucket before starting food prep. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
