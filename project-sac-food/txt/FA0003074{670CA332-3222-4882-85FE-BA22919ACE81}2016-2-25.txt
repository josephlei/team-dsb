Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700.
All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the
potential to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: *NEW MAJOR VIOLATION* Some dim sum items (egg tarts, fried meat-filled puffs) that were on time as a public
health control (TPHC) were saved after the expiration time of 2:30p and placed in a covered pan on a dining room table with a sign
that stated they were for sale. Corrected; items were voluntarily discarded. Items on TPHC must be discarded after 4 hours.
Education provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be marked
to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within the
four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was used,
shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 1b

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION

OBSERVATIONS: Food safety manager certificate is expired. Correct within 60 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee
who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Ice machine (secondary server station) inside top panel and side has dark residue growth. Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use
with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact
surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS:
The following areas are unclean with grease/residue/particle/debris/splatter: (REPEAT VIOLATION)
-sides of the main cooks line fryers
-floor and floor sinks under the entire main cooks line (fryers, wok line, gas range), dim sum cooks line, dim sum equipment line
(stacked ovens, fryer, steamer and BBQ oven)
-inside bottom of the dim sum prep cooler
-sheet pan underneath hot water dispenser in the secondary server station
Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS:
Microwave in the cooks line is household grade. Correct within 60 days.
Bus tub with equipment under the microwave is heavily deteriorated (scratched off plastic). Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized
surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and
dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid
structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS:
Two drain lines under the 2-compartment waresink are not aligned to the floor sink and drains onto the floor surrounding it.
Correct within 14 days.
*New minor violation* New flex tubes attached to the drain lines under the dim sum prep sink are extended into the floor sink.
Provide 1-inch air gaps within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS:
The following areas need repair:
-Floor tile grout lines behind the rice cookers shelf. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION.
-FRP under the 2-compartment prep sink is detached, creating a large pocket and gap between the wall. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION
-Corner floor tiles under the 2-compartment waresink and tile grout lines in the warewash room are deteriorated. REPEAT
VIOLATION.
Correct within 2 weeks.
Damaged ceiling spots observed throughout dry storage room. CHRONIC REPEAT VIOLATION. Ceiling has exposed raw wood and
appears to be from water damage. Correct within 2 months. Failure to correct by next routine inspection will require further
enforcement action including a compliance conference with a fee of $268.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving
shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture
of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1,
114271, 114272)
