Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Plastic six-pan containers and half pan covers from the dishwasher was put away while still
displaying food particles. The containers and covers from the load were placed back into the dishwasher to be washed again. Make
certain the dishwasher has cleaned the equipment and utensils once the cycle is complete before putting away. Implement
immediately.
MINOR VIOLATION - Slime/mold was observed on the deflector plate and around the ice chute inside the ice machine. Heavy mold
accumulation observed on the ice dispenser chute in the soda dispenser. Clean and sanitize these surfaces immediately. REPEAT
VIOLATION.
Raw ground beef particles were observed on the scale and on the base of the vegetable slicer (near the water heater). Clean and
sanitize these surfaces immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Shredded cheddar (43F) and blue cheese (44F) in the makeline top inserts were out of proper holding temperature.
Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water from the 3-comp sink peaked at 118F. Repair to provide at least 120F available at all times. Correct
within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open bag of OREOS observed in the cupboard below the ice cream area counter. Store all open packages in
containers with a tight fitting lid. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Container of batter powder under the breading station was lacking a label. Provide label to all containers with
food. Correct within 24 hours. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Chlorine test strips for the dishwasher could not be located. Provide within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Leakage from the compressor falling onto the bowls (turned upside down) in the upright reach-in freezer to the
right of the breading station. Repair leak immediately. Any observed leakage onto an uncovered food item of "clean" container for
use will be cited as a MAJOR VIOLATION and impacted food items will be discarded.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Wet wiping cloth was stored on top of the ice cream freezer in the front register area. Store all wet wiping cloths
in sanitizer at all times when not in use. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Toilet paper dispenser in the restroom was empty. Roll of toilet paper was available. Keep dispenser adeqautely
filled at all times. Correct immediately.

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Hole (about 2" in diameter) was observed under the prep table left of the office area. Eliminate this hole under the

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage

or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
