Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Potentially hazardous foods (PHF), including soya milk, thickened dairy drink and chef's salads inside the two
door upright refrigerator were measured at 48 to 50 F using a thermocouple. Ambient temperature inside the unit was measured at
49 F. Soya milk (3 glasses) and dairy drink (< 1 qt) were unilaterally discarded by the operator. Chef's salads (3 plates) were moved
into the walk-in refrigerator during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Maximum temperature of hot water at warewash sink was measured at 117 F. State code requires hot water at 120
F minimum at the warewash sink at all times. Correct today.
- REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Spray bottle with unidentified liquid contents found at mop sink area. Label all chemicals and bottles. Correct
today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination
of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food
facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall
be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Bulk container for flour (beside bulk container for powdered sugar) was not labeled. Corrected during the
inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Exit door does not self-close consistently. Correct within 3 days.
- Note: Operator has already eliminated the gap under the door.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Broken floor tiles observed just outside the walk-in freezer door. Correct within 14 days.
- REPEAT VIOLATION -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base

juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
