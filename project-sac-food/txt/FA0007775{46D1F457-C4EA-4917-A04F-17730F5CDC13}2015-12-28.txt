Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700.
All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the
potential to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Observed bottle of water stored on prep table by ice machine next to cutting board. All employee beverage must
be stored in a cup with lid and straw, and below food prep areas. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent
contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to
eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination.
(113977, 114256 (a))

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Overfilled bowl with sliced tomatoes 47F-50F stored in ice bath at salad bar. Employee removed extra tomatoes
during inspection.
- Package with oven roasted turkey breast 46F was stored on top of cold top insert in front food prep area. Operator relocated meat
inside reach-in cooler. All potentially hazardous foods must be stored at/below 41F at all times.
Note: All food inside front reach-in cooler was stored at 42F. Operator lowered thermostat to colder setting during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: - Black residue build up on panel inside ice machine. Remove all ice, wash, rinse, sanitize and air dry within 24
hours AND maintain clean at all times. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Old food residue build up on tooth of can opener. Clean immediately.
- Food debris build up on the cutting board next to pizza prep line and pizza slice area. Cutting boards and other food contact
surfaces should be washed at least once every 4 hours and whenever contaminated. Clean immediately. (Also, see violation #40)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use
with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact
surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Pizza boxes, soda syrup box, and a box with small portion cups stored on the floor at water heater storage area.
All food and food related items must be stored at least 6 inches off the floor. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged food
may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: - Dust build up on fan guards inside walk-in cooler.
- Dust and other debris build up on the floor beneath all equipment especially beneath ice machine, upright freezer, dish machine,
near water heater.
- Unclean shelf beneath prep area that is located between pizza oven and ice machine.
Clean and maintain within 5 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Observed unclean brown squared boards beneath prep area and some where used to store prepared pizza.
Operator noted that they cannot be clean in dish machine or sanitized with bleach because they will deteriorate immediately. All
surfaces must be smooth, durable, and easily washable. Replace these boards with approved equipment within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Unclean equipment stored on metro shelf next to pizza prep cooler. All equipment shall be stored clean. Correct
within 48 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized
surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and
dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid
structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Soiled cloth towel observed on the cutting board of pizza prep cooler. Soiled cloth towels observed near 2-comp
sink with sprayer. Cutting boards were observed unclean and sanitizer bucket was not set up. Education was provided. Store
multi-use cloth towels in sanitizer bucket between use with 100 ppm chlorine. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Drain pipes on top of floor sink beneath salad bar. Also, drains in floor sink beneath kitchen handwash sink
located next to pizza oven. Drain pipes shall be at least 1 inch above floor sink. Correct within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

# 44a

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Unused equipment stored behind rear door. Unused and not secured to rigid structure CO2 tanks stored behind
facility next to metro shelf. Remove all unused equipment within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of the
facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Employee cell phone was stored on prep table next to cutting board that was used to dice tomatoes. All personal
items must be stored below and away from food prep areas. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers or
other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: - Loose, broken, chipped cove base tiles observed next to mop sink, beneath dough prep table, and font floor
sink. Repair within 30 days. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Hole observed on ceiling above metro shelf that is located next to pizza prep cooler. Also, bulging ceiling panel observed above
dish wash machine. Repair and seal with approved material within 14 days. REPEAT VIOLATION
- Loose metal escutcheon observed below ceiling hole that is located above water heater. Secure within 7 days. REPEAT
VIOLATION
- Perforated ceiling panels observed above sandwich prep station, pasta reheating unit, bread storage, cookie display at front area.
Smooth and easily cleanable ceiling panels shall be located above all food prep areas. Provide approved ceiling panels within 1
year. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving
shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture
of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1,
114271, 114272)
