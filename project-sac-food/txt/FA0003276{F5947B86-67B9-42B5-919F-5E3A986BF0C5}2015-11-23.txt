#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following cold holding temperatures where observed:
1. Tofu in the chef prep refrigerator at 48 deg. F. Ice added to quickly chill
2. All foods in the front side dish refrigerator read 51 - 58 deg. F. According to owner/chef these were made this morning.
Keep all foods at or below 41 deg. F Do not stack foods above chill line in the salad refrigerator as it will not be chilled or
maintained cold ("Calamarie" at 58 deg. F).
All foods that did not contain vinegar were moved to the walk in for quick cooling.
Ensure that this refrigerator can maintain cold PHFs at or below 41 deg. F PRIOR to putting side dishes of cooked vegetables or
animal products that do not contain vinegar into this unit (Okay to place side dishes with vinegar inside.)
Recommend putting hot foods, uncovered, in the walk in while cooling so that this unit is not overwhelmed, then moving to the
front as needed.
Corrected by placing PHFs in walk in. A refrigerator repair man has also been called. Note: Thermometer inside read 56 deg. F.
Other foods measured 51 -53 deg. F. Note: Foods that are cold held above 51 deg. F are a major violation and will effect placarding
and must be corrected immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Bowls and containers ready for food service on the shelf next to the 2 door chef prep have accumulation of
debris.
Knives in the back work area, hanging up clean, have food debris near hilt.
Clean and maintain within 1 day. Do NOT store dishes in a dirty condition where they may easily be used by food servers or food
preparer.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use
with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact
surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: REPEAT VIOLATION: Raw shelled eggs in the walk in above spinach and 5 gallon containers of sauces. Keep raw
animal products, including eggs, below/separate from ready-to-eat foods, or lightly cooked products to prevent cross
contamination. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Household "Sharp" brand microwave in the back work area. Provide NSF approved equipment if used for
restaurant.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Mop sink has atmospheric vacuum breaker and a "Y - connection fitting below with a hose attached below the lip
of the mop sink. The Y-fitting has off/on. The Y fitting can cause the atmospheric vacuum breaker to fail, UNDETECTED. Provide a
hose bib vacuum breaker OR remove the Y fitting. See handouts from the City of Wichita and EMD. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Coffee maker, for employee use only, is not labeled. Label all equipment that is for employee use "Employee Use
Only" within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers or
other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)
