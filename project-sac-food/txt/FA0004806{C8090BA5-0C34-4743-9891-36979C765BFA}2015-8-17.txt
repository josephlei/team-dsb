Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700.
All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the
potential to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Both meat slicers that had been in use have accumulated old meat residue and debris that were not from today,
on the underside of the slicer panels. When observing area on the bottom, a foul meat odor was emanating from the vicinity. In
addition, an employee used a wipe cloth with no detectable sanitizer when directed to clean the unit. Per employee interview, unit
is cleaned sometimes after use but typically at the end of the day. Ambient of the meat department was at 65F-70F. Correcting
onsite; employee is taking apart unit to clean.
Unit must be taken apart and thoroughly washed/rinsed/sanitized every 4 hours.
Note: facility may look into lowering the ambient temperature of the meat department to below 55F to decrease the frequency of
washing/rinsing/sanitizing equipment.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Balut in the egg refrigerator measured at 48F-49F. Ambient of the unit was at 42F. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Numerous flies are hovering around the meat department, walls and above equipment. REPEAT VIOLATION.
Contact pest control service and maintain within 7 days. Noncompliance may result in additional reinspections with a fee of $268
and/or compliance conference with a fee of $402.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 32a

FOOD PRESENTATION AND LABELING

OBSERVATIONS: Some pastries sold by Sunshine Bakery are inadequately labeled. Correct immediately. Note: Assistant Manager
stated they will remove from shelf and return to supplier. Other products by same manufacturer had adequate labeling.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food offered for human consumption shall be honestly presented in a way that does not mislead or misinform the
consumer. Food packaged in a food facility shall bear a label that complies with the labeling requirements prescribed by the Sherman Food,
Drug, and Cosmetic Law which include: common name of the food, ingredients list, quantity of contents, name and address of manufacturer,
nutritional information as specified in 21 C.F.R. (114087, 114088, 114089, 114089.1(a, b), 114090, 114093.1)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: A light tube is improperly stored on the top of the speed rack above meat trays next to the 3-compartment sink.
Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized
surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and
dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid
structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4,
114185.5)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Meat case is missing a monitoring thermometer. Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Sanitizer buckets are not adequately set up for use when using multi-use cloths. Cloths are placed in hot soapy
water in-between uses. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Back mop sink with the Y-splitter is missing an anti siphon device on the connected spray hose. Correct within 1
week.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS:
Back warehouse double door that was not in use was left open. Corrected.
Self-closing device on the back warehouse double door is broken. Correct within 1 week.
A large 1-inch gap is observed between the back warehouse double doors. Seal within 1 week.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS:
Floor tiles next to the meat slicer and around floor sinks/channel drain are chipped and deteriorated. Correct within 30 days.
FRP across the back double doors near the restrooms is detached from the wall. Correct within 14 days.
Cove base tiles along the seafood side nearest the restrooms are missing. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
