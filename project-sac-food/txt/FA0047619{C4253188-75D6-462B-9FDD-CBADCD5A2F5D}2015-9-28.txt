#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following potentially hazardous food items were stored at improper holding temperature:
- cottage cheese (47F), canned garbanzo beans (47F), canned corn (46F), potato salad (50F) in salad bar
- cheese sticks (130F) at self serve hot plate
- overfilled chicken pieces (45F) at pizza line cold top
Maintain all potentially hazardous food at/below 41F for cold food and at/above 135F for hot food. Keep cold top lids closed when
not in use. Keep inserts only ¾ full to help maintain temperature. Corrected by relocating all items noted above to walk in cooler.
Note:
- ambient temperature of the salad cold top was measured at 45F at time of inspection. Per owner, this cold top cooler was repaired
recently and was told by the repair technician that it should be working properly and keep cold temperature at 41F. Per owner, she
will use ice to keep cold temperature at/below 41F for noted items above. Also, a cover for this cooler will be purchased soon.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 13

FOOD IN GOOD CONDITION, SAFE, AND UNADULTERATED

OBSERVATIONS: One pineapple can observed dented at seam/seal in back prep area. Corrected by removing from the rack storing
bulk cans.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not commit any act that may cause the contamination or adulteration of food or food contact
surfaces. Food shall be produced, prepared, stored, transported, and served, so as to be free from adulteration and spoilage. Food is
considered adulterated if it bears or contains any poisonous or deleterious substance that may render it impure or injurious to health.
Containers previously used to store poisonous or toxic materials shall not be used to store food, utensils, or single-use articles. Utensils
used for tasting shall be discarded or washed, rinsed, and sanitized between tastings. Ice used as a medium for cooling food and
beverages shall not be offered for consumption. Food shall be inspected upon receipt and prior to any use, storage, or resale. (113967,
113976, 113980, 113988, 113990, 114035, 114167, 114254.3)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Areas observed with old food/grease/residue like accumulation:
- wall behind trash bin by salad bar
- crevice between both cold top coolers in the back prep area
- floor sink underneath sada machine
- floor sink underneath 3 compartment sink
- floor underneath 3 compartment sink and dish machine
Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
