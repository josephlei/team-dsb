#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION
Tri tip and mashed potatoes on steam table (right side) were measured at 112 F and 115 F, respectively, with a thermocouple.
Measure food temperatures regularly to ensure that these are at 135 F or above, or at 41 F or below. Operator unilaterally discarded
the tri tip (~1 lb) and mashed potatoes (~1.5 lb) during the inspection.
Minor Violation
Meat loaf inside the right side of the left cold top was at 48 F. Ambient temperature on the right side of the unit was at 48 F; 37 F on
the left side of the unit. Operator called his refrigeration technician to have this corrected. In the meantime, store potentially
hazardous food (PHF) items on the left side of the cold top.
- Repeat Violation -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Trays were observed not being allowed sufficient contact time in the sanitizing solution during warewashing.
Minimum contact time with quaternary ammonia sanitizer is one minute before air drying. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Box of cookie dough was found stored directly on the walk-in freezer floor. Food and food contact items shall be
stored at least 6 inches above the floor. Corrected during the inspection.
- Repeat Violation -

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 32b

COMPLIANCE WITH TRANS FAT BAN

OBSERVATIONS: Five small containers of Duncan Hines whipped frosting found in dry storage found to contain 2.0 gm of trans fat
per serving. Discontinue use immediately.

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Heavy dust accumulation of ceiling vent above area between make line and service areas. Correct within 24
hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Sanitizer level in wiping cloth bucket beside cook's line was measured at less than 200 ppm quaternary
ammonium. Use the test strips to check sanitizer level regularly and maintain at 200 ppm minimum at all times. Corrected during
the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
