# 14b

WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Sanitizer in the automatic dishwasher was observed at 0ppm chlorine. Maintain sanitizer at least 50ppm chlorine
at all times. Cease its use until repaired to be fully functional. Utilize 3-comp sink to manually sanitize all items that have been run
through the machine. Implement immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or
completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least
100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact
with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in
the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After
cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101
(b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Open bag of chips observed near the salsa at the right side of the cook's line. Cease all eating in all food prep
area to prevent cross contamination. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Sliced tomatoes (47F), mozzarella cheese (46F) in the cook's line top insert, cut melons (45) on the cook's line
counter, shrimp (47F) in the upright reach-in left of waffle iron, minestrone soup (44F), half and half (47F), corned beef (45F), cut
potatoes (46F), cheese sauce (44F) in the cook's line upright reach-in were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all
potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Slime/mold growth was observed in at least two ice machines. Clean and sanitize these surfaces inside the ice
machine within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 19

CONSUMER ADVISORY FOR UNDERCOOKED FOOD

OBSERVATIONS: At least one menu item is lacking a consumer advisory specifying the food item is not fully cooked. Provide
consumer advisory at the next printing of menu. Provide verbal notice until new menus have been printed.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Undercooked or raw food may be served to the comsumer if either of the following conditions are met: the consumer
specifically orders the food prepared raw or less than throughly cooked, or the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the
time of ordering, that the food is raw or less than throughly cooked. Confectionery food containing more than 0.5% alcohol may be served to
the consumer if the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the time of ordering, that it contains more than 0.5% alcohol.
(114012, 114093)

# 23

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: At least 50 rodent droppings were observed inside the rightmost cabinet under the defunct omelet station and the
two right cabinet, and left most cabinet under the soda dispenser in the buffet room. Clean and sanitize these areas immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open boxes of powdered sugar and cornstarch, open bag of granola in the cook's line, open bags of rice,
oatmeal, dried cranberries, and other food items observed in the storage room. Store all open packages of food items in containers
with a tight fitting lid. Correct immediately.
Uncovered containers of food items in the upright reach-in cooler to the left of the waffle irons. Provide cover to all stored food
items to prevent from becoming contaminated. Correct immediately.
Containers of food item being kept in the ice bath are improperly stored. Maintain container surrounded by ice to the same level as
the food within the container to maintain proper holding temperature. Correct immediately.
Container of cut melons was removed from the ice bath to fill an order but the container was not placed back in the ice bath. Keep
containers within a temperature controlled environment at all times. Correct immediately.
Raw shell eggs are stored in a paper egg crate inside a plastic container. At the time of inspection, no eggs were stored but was
told it generally sits on the ice. Such practice may not allow food items to hold proper holding temperature. Find alternative
method of maintaining eggs at proper holing temperature and or utilize 4-hour hold time (time as a public health control. Correct
immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 29

IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES

OBSERVATIONS: Spray bottle of cleaner was stored on top of clean glassware in the server's room. Store all chemicals under or
away from all and food related items at all times. Corrected during the inspection.

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Ambient temperature in the cook's line (non-banquet side) of reach-in #2 was observed at 48F at the time of
inspection. Repair to maintain ambient temperature of all refrigeration units at 41F or below. Correct within 3 days. All food items
were removed during the inspection and transferred to a functional refrigeration unit.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Foil used as a liner in the cook's line across the lowboy. Remove this unapproved liner immediately. Corrected.
Tongs are stored on the oven handle at the cook's line and on the drawer handle in the omelet station. Store these utensils in a
manner protected from contamination. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: At least two wiping cloths in the cook's line were observed on the counter. Store in sanitizer at all times when not
in use. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Cup of coffee was stored above the prep sink in the cook's line. Store all employee items including beverages to
prevent cross contamination.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Floor of cabinet under the soda station in the buffet room is deteriorated. Repair flooring to prevent the
entry/infestation of vermin. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
