Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Spicy chicken (69F) on the metal table to the right of the grill was out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all
potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. This item was voluntarily discarded.
Cooked shrimp (52F) was stored on top of the container of tempura vegetables. Cease such practice of nesting (stacking
containers) that prevent food to be maintained at proper holding temperature. Shrimp was relocated to the reach-in cooler.
MINOR VIOLATION - Cooked tempura vegetables (45F) were stored above top insert containers. Maintain food items at proper
holding temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Steamed broccoli is placed directly into the top insert containers in the makeline. Cease this practice
immediately. Top inserts are not designed to cool food items within the alloted time. Utilize other means of cooling food items.
Implement immediately.
Chow mein noodles is stored in a deep pan with a cover in the walk-in while cooling. Do not cover food items that are cooling.
Such practice prolongs the cooling process. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor under the reach-in across the grill and under the cooking equipment wheels/legs in the makeline station is in
need of cleaning due to heavy accumulation of debris. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Domestic grade flat grill (similar to a George Foreman grill) is observed next to the grill. Remove this unapproved
equipment within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Knives for the sushi rolls are stored in stagnant, room-temp water. Cease this practice immediately. Store
utensils in a manner protected from bacterial growth. Correct immediately.
Plastic ramekin used as a scoop for the sesame seeds on the rack left of the cook's line handwash station. Provide scoop with
handle within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean
sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled,
displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Food item for the staff is stored directly above a container of sauce to the left of the handwash station in the
cook's line. Store all personal items (including food and beverages) under or away from all food and food related items for the
facility. Correct immediately. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Slight damage on floor observed in front of the deep fryer. Further damage to the floor may result in requiring
repair to flooring throughout area. Patch this damage within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
