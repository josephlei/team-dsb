#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: - Overfilled container with polenta 45F at cold top cooler across from fryer. Chef removed extra polenta during
inspection.
- Ahi tuna, sliced turkey, grilled eggplants and diced jack cheese 43F-44F inside reach-in cooler of salad prep. Ambient temperature
was 44F. Food was relocated inside walk-in cooler. (Also, see violation #35)
- Shredded cheese 45F at cold top drawer of pizza prep unit. Food was relocated inside walk-in cooler. (Also, see violation #35).
Maintain all cold potentially hazardous food items at/below 41F at all times. REPEAT VIOLATION
NOTE: Most food items inside reach-in coolers were at 42F. Turn thermostat of reach-in coolers to colder setting to ensure PHFs
stored at/below 41F AT ALL TIMES. Facility was closed for service at time of kitchen inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: - Old food residue build up on wooden pizza shovels that were stored on top of mixer. Per employee, they are
washed once a week. Pizza shovels were cleaned during inspection. (Also, see violation #35)
- Ice scoop stored on top of ice machine on rubber mat. Store ice scoop in hygienic manner. Corrected.
- Ice scoop stored inside water with standing water on top of soda dispenser. Store in clean/empty container. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use
with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact
surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 19

CONSUMER ADVISORY FOR UNDERCOOKED FOOD

OBSERVATIONS: Consumer advisory was not provided for Nicoise salad that comes with undercooked ahi tuna. Provide verbal
consumer advisory immediately. Consumer advisory handout was provided.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Undercooked or raw food may be served to the comsumer if either of the following conditions are met: the consumer
specifically orders the food prepared raw or less than throughly cooked, or the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the
time of ordering, that the food is raw or less than throughly cooked. Confectionery food containing more than 0.5% alcohol may be served to
the consumer if the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the time of ordering, that it contains more than 0.5% alcohol.
(114012, 114093)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Raw beef stored behind vegetables at cold top cooler across from grill. Store raw beef in front of ready-to-eat
food items. Corrected by relocating inside reach-in cooler.

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Old food, residue build up on the floor beneath pasta reach-in cooler, beneath all equipment at bar area, and
inside water heater room. Also, dust build up behind pizza oven. Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: - Ambient temperature inside pizza and salad prep reach-in cooler was 44F. Per chef, units are off during night
time and are turned on at 9am. Do not place any food items inside these coolers until ambient temperature reaches at/below 41F.
Employee turned thermostat to colder setting during inspection. Ambient temperature reached 41F by the end of inspection
(11:15am). REMEMBER THAT ALL POTENTIALLY HAZARDOUS FOOD ITEMS SHOULD BE AT/BELOW 41F AT ALL TIMES.
- Oster blender in use for salsa making is household grade. Provide commercial grade ANSI/NSF approved blender within 4
months.
- Several wooden pizza shovels observed to be deteriorated where wooden parts have been falling out easily. Also, found two
uncleanable cutting boards (with black residue/mold build up). Cutting boards were voluntarily discarded during inspection.
Ensure all food contact surfaces are smooth, non-absorbent, durable and easily washable. Correct within 7 days.
- Pizza reach-in cooler missing one fan guard. Install within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Ceiling light missing shatter proof cover/guard above bread storage area. Also, ceiling light is missing above
walk-in cooler. Provide required covers and lighting within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient ventilation
to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or light-switch
activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in areas where open
food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with light shields. (114149,
114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Black and iron pipe nesting on floor sink at bar area. Provide at least 1 inch air gap within 7 day.

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Shared dumpster bin lids were open when not in use. Keep dumpster lids closed between use.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be kept
in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at all
times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and other
conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 44a

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Cargo storage container observed to be cluttered and unorganized. Complete inspection could not be conducted
during inspection. Organize cargo storage container to permit regular cleaning and inspection within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of the
facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Grout missing between coving tiles and wall behind newly installed ice machine. Grout within 2 months.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving
shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture
of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1,
114271, 114272)
