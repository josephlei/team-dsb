#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION
Logs were filled in when inspector showed up (11:30 am). Facility opens for business at 11:00 am. Supervisor Jeff Czapla was
consulted to allow food not properly tracked to not be discarded. Per operator, all food on the line was cooked at 11:00 am, 30
minutes prior to when inspected entered. If TPHC is being used, ensure that accurate times are tracked. This is this facility's one
time warning. Improper tracking of food in future inspections will result in food being discarded.

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be marked
to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within the
four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was used,
shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 1a

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Operator stated that they leave food to cool outside at room temperature for two hours then food is relocated into
the walkin refrigerator. Education was provided during time of inspection. Ensure that food in the cooling process is cooled from
135F to 70F within 2 hours and from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Food cooling at room temperature for the first two hours may not
reach 70F within 2 hours. Food should be relocated into the walkin refrigerator within 30 minutes of cooling at room temperature
to ensure that both critical control points are met.
NOTE: Food is cooled on thin metal sheet pans (correct/approved method)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: -Handle of ice scoop is directly touching ice in the front soda machine. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use
with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact
surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: -Observed spilled rice and sugar inside the dry storage room in the hallway by the restrooms. Clean and
maintain today.
-Observed old food/grease accumulation in between the deep fryer and stove, between the woks and stoves and below the three
door reaching refrigerator. Clean and maintain within 3 days.
-Observed grease accumulation in the middle section of the ventilation hood. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
-Observed dust accumulation on the small black portable fan being used in the kitchen. Clean and maintain before using to avoid
potential cross contamination. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Tongs were hung on the handle of the oven on the cooks line where employee clothing may potentially brush up
on tongs. Tongs were relocated to upper middle section of the wok away from potential contamination with clothing.
