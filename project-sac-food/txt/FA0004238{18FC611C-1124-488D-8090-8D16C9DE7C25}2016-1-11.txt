Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700.
All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the
potential to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 11

REHEATING PROCEDURES FOR HOT HOLDING

OBSERVATIONS: Pinto beans (124F), refried beans (64F) in the steam table are being reheated improperly before placing in a hot
holding unit. Cease this practice of placing food items removed from a refrigeration unit directly into the steam table. All food
items that are to be placed into the steam table or another hot holding unit must be reheated to at least 165F before placed in hot
holding unit. These items were reheated to at least 165F during the inspection.
NOTE: Steam tables are not designed to be used as a reheating equipment.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods that are cooked, cooled, and subsequently reheated for hot holding or service, shall be
heated to a temperature of 165F for 15 seconds. Ready-to-eat food taken from a commercailly processed hermetically sealed container, or
from an intact package from a food processing plant shall be heated to a temperature of 135F for hot holding. (114016)

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Cook staff is placing cold food items from the walk-in directly into the steam table to reheat. Cease this process
immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Open beverages above prep sink observed in the back area and bar. All employee beverages must be equipped
lid and straw at all times. Correct immediately.
Open container of cookies observed in the bar (where ceviche items are stored and served). Restrict eating to areas away from
food storage or food preparation. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent
contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to
eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination.
(113977, 114256 (a))

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Cut, cooked octopus (45F) in the bar and pinto beans (44F) in the walk-in cooler were out of proper holding
temperature. Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water in the 3-comp sink is not adequate at 110F. Repair to provide at least 120F available at all times.
Correct within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water

handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Prep sinks (2 compartments) are being used to thaw two different items (prawns and octopus) that only has one
faucet. Running cold water must be flowing into the containers for proper thawing. Implement proper practice immediately. Utilize
other approved methods of thawing if more than one item is to be thawed. Correct immediately.
Container of frozen tilapia was placed on the counter above the prep area reach-in. Cease this practice immediately. Utilize one of
four approved methods of thawing. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Left side of the salsa bar is still lacking a sneeze guard. Provide sneeze guard on this side (that is accessible to
customers) within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.
Container of cooked rice in the steam table at the cook's line is overfilled. Do not overfill the containers to maintain proper holding
temperature. Correct immediately.
Containers of cooked pork and beef were placed on the mobile rack next to the cook's line without any temperature control. Ice
bath was provided during the inspection.
Container of cooked pork and beef was placed in an ice bath but the ice level is only at the bottom of the container with meat.
Container of cooked octopus in the bar had an inadequate amount of ice to maintain proper holding temperature. Ice bath must be
at the same (or higher) level of the level of the food items being kept cold. Implement proper practice immediately.
Containers of salsa and other food items in the walk-in cooler were lacking labels. Provide label to all cold food items while stored.
Correct immediately. (NOTE: Do not cover food items that are still cooling.)
Container of raw beef is stored over cooked meats in the cook's line reach-in and frozen seafood is stored over cooked meat in the
prep area reach-in. Store food items accordingly to prevent cross contamination. Raw meats/seafood must be stored under ready
to eat items. Correct immediately. Handout was provided.
Container of par-cooked beef was placed on top of a cooling rack above the flat grill. This is not an adequate method of holding or
reheating. Reheat this item properly and maintain proper holding temperature or place into refrigeration unit immediately.
Bags of dried chili are being opened in the cargo container. Cease this practice immediately. Remove closed items from the cargo
container to be used and open inside the facility to prevent any open food items in the cargo container. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open bag of cinnamon sticks was observed in the cargo container. All non-PHF items stored within this unit
must be in tightly sealed containers or stored within its original packaging. Store cinnamon sticks in container with a TIGHT
FITTING cover. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged food
may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Spilled dried peppers were observed on the cargo container. Clean the spillage immediately to prevent vermin
infestation.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Glass case refrigerator is designed to only store packaged, non-PHF items. Remove all open packages of PHF
out of these units. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Bowl used as scoop for dry beans and paper cup used as scoop for the salt. Handle of scoop in the bulk
containers is in contact with food items. Cease this practice immediately. Provide scoop with handle for bulk food items and
maintain handle upright to prevent coming in contact with food. Implement immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized
surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and
dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid
structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Two hood filters are deteriorated. Repair or replace these filter units within 14 days.
Gas stove is stored outside of the hood. All gas equipment must be operated fully under the hood with a 6-inch clearance. Modify
the equipment to fit under the hood. Correct within 30 days. (Potential alternative is to provide a sheet metal overhang that
encompass the stove.)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Mops are stored in bucket. Hang mops after use to allow drying. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for
the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet
separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After
use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Open cups of coffee for employees are stored above the prep sink. Cease this practice immediately. Store all
personal items (including beverages) under or away from all food and food related items at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers or
other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Cargo container was not fully closed at the time of inspection. This unit must be tightly closed at all times to
prevent the entry/infestation of vermin. Cargo container is free standing and located outside of the facility. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device and
have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food or
utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if required.
(114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
