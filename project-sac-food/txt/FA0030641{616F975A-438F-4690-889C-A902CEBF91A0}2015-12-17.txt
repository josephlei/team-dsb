Routine

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 1a

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Staff was not aware of the final cooking temperature of chicken (165F), reheating requirements (135F for
prepackaged, pre-cooked foods and 165F for cooled food then reheated item for hot holding) and proper concentration of sanitizer.
Education was provided a the time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Seven food handling staff were lacking current food handler certificates. Register these employees to an
approved class within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Sliced tomatoes (40-47F) in the makeline to the left of the deep fryer, grilled chicken nuggets (130-147F) in the
makeline hot holding unit and diced cooked eggs were sitting above top inserts were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain
all potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Food debris observed on the spinning cutting equipment in area outside the office. Clean equipment thoroughly.
Correct immediately.
Racks in the walk-in cooler is in need of cleaning due to molding. Clean and sanitize the racks within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water in the warewash station was not adequate at 115F. Provide hot water at least 120F in the warewash
station. Correct within 3 days.
Water dispensed at men's handwash station is too hot at 115F. Repair to maintain dispensed water at 100-108F in all handwash
station that lacks separate hot and cold water valves. Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Container of diced eggs is stored above the top insert. Cease this practice immediately as it does not maintain
proper holding temperature. Store container inside the reach-in to maintain proper holding temperature at all times. Correct
immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Floor under the ice machine, under the upright reach-in by the swing door, under the cold top makeline reach-in,
under the breading station were displaying accumulation of food and non-food debris. Clean floor within 24 hours.
Cabinets under the makeline counters in the kitchen especially under the pass through window is displaying old food debris
accumulation. Clean these cabinets free from food debris accumulation at all times. Correct to prevent vermin infestation within 24
hours.
Counter under/behind the spinning, cutting machine in area outside the office is in need of cleaning due to cut food accumulation.
Clean counter within 24 hours.
Ice build-up observed on walk-in freezer floor under the crates. Clean floor within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Ice accumulation observed around the compressor in the walk-in freezer. Remove ice accumulation within 3 days.
Determine cause of build-up and repair as deemed necessary.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: At least one sanitizer was observed at 50ppm quat ammonia. Maintain solution at least 200ppm quat ammonia at
all times. Solution was changed during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
