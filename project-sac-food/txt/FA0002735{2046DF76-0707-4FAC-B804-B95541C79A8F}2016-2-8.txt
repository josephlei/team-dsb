#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Shredded cheese on ice in front of flat grill was at 53F.
MINOR VIOLATION:
WALK-IN REFRIGERATOR:
-Refried beans at 44F.
-Rice at 43F.
Keep all cold holding potentially hazardous foods at/below 41F. These foods are located near door to walk in refrigerator (warmest
part of walk-in refrigerator). Relocate pickles and mayonnaise near door and place beans, rice etc on this shelving near back of
walk-in refrigerator. Correct within 2 days.
PREP REFRIGERATOR ACROSS FROM COOK'S LINE:
-Diced tomatoes at 48F.
-Shredded cheese at 50F
-Taquitos at 46F
-Raw hamburger at 46F.
Ambient temperature of this refrigerator was at 48F. Thermometer inside unit was also at 48F. Repair refrigerator immediately.
Submit proof of correction within 2 days by email provided on business card. Failure to provide documentation showing that
refrigeration unit has been repaired and holding temperature will result in a re-inspection.
ON ICE:
Shredded cheese between 45-48F near microwaves. Keep all cold holding food at/below 41F. Ensure that ice comes up to the same
height as the food it is trying to cool.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#6

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Bucket with a small amount of water is stored in basin of hand sink. Do not store anything inside basin of hand
sink. This includes sanitizer bucket. Keep sink clear and accessible at all times. Correct immediately (REPEAT VIOLATION).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing
areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap
and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair.
(113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Grease accumulation on floor and wall behind and under cooking equipment. Clean and maintain free of
grease/food within 7 days (REPEAT VIOLATION).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Pieces of raw wood used to prop up fryer. Provide stainless steel legs or similar material that is smooth, easily
cleanable, and durable. Correct within 30 days (REPEAT VIOLATION).
Handle of prep refrigerator is broken and held together by duct tape. Remove tape and repair handle within 30 days.
Insert holding shredded cheese is broken and bottom is duct taped. Remove from service today.
Sprayer wrapped in plastic. Remove plastic and repair sprayer if needed. Correct within 2 days.
Cabinet under soda machine has water damage and is no longer smooth and easily cleanable. Replace shelving or line with FRP
that is secured with adhesive and all cracks and gaps are sealed. Correct within 60 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Damp cloths in front of flat grill are at 0ppm chlorine bleach. No sanitizer bucket was set up. Bucket was set up
during inspection and all cloths placed inside bucket.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Women's restroom has a strong sewer type odor. Investigate cause, clean or treat floor drain. Correct within 14
days (REPEAT VIOLATION).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Yellow nail polish bottles stored on shelf with to-go boxes. Relocate all personal belongings in designated areas
away from food prep and food storage. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Cracked tiles in front of 2 door refrigerator used as a storage unit. Six cove base tiles missing inside walk-in
refrigerator. One missing at end of cook's line. Replace or repair tiles within 60 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
