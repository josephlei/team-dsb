Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700.
All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Open employee beverage cup of water near the handsink (below towel dispenser). This allows hand contact with
the areas of the cup that the mouth contacts. Provide covered employee beverage containers with lids within 2 days. Suggest
straws ("to go cups")

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent
contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to
eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination.
(113977, 114256 (a))

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following cold holding temperatures were observed in the "danger zone."
1. Tofu in the chef prep (kitchen) at 45 deg. F. (repeat violation)
2. Bagged sprouts in the walk in at 45 deg. F
3. Cooked breaded jalapeno peppers in the front merchandiser at 45 deg. F
Maintain cold potentially hazardous food at or below 41 deg. F. Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 19

CONSUMER ADVISORY FOR UNDERCOOKED FOOD

OBSERVATIONS: Salad served with raw egg on the salad greens. Provide consumer notification - see handout, within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Undercooked or raw food may be served to the comsumer if either of the following conditions are met: the consumer
specifically orders the food prepared raw or less than throughly cooked, or the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the
time of ordering, that the food is raw or less than throughly cooked. Confectionery food containing more than 0.5% alcohol may be served to
the consumer if the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the time of ordering, that it contains more than 0.5% alcohol.
(114012, 114093)

# 26

THAWING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Observed garbage can used for thawing stomach by placing water and stomach in the bin. This is an approved
method of thawing. Use ONLY:
1. under refrigerations
2. as part of the cooking process
3. in the microwave just prior to cooking
4. under RUNNING water sufficient to flush particles off frozen food.
Discontinue allowing foods to sit in bloody water to thaw. Note: food was still frozen during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under
refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the
cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Repeat violation (3): Raw shelled eggs in the walk in above 5 gallon containers of sauces. Keep raw animal
products, including eggs below/separate from ready-to-eat foods, or lightly cooked products, to prevent cross contamination.
Correct within 24 hours.
Suggest designating a place in the walk in that meets requirements for raw shelled eggs.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Repeat violation (3): Attention to cleaning detail is poor in difficult to reach areas, around the legs of equipment,
behind seldom-moved equipment, including but not limited to:
1. Mold growth at the 3 compartment sink, at the wall junction
2. Behind/below the 3 compartment sink, undersides of sink and between sink bays.
3. Wall at the handsink has peeling paint
4. Wall between the shelf for dishes and the cookline has splatters
5. Fan guard in the walk in has white fluffy mold growth and black mold growth
6. Wood shelving in the walk in has black mold growth
7. Floor below shelves in the walk in has spilled food accumulation.
Clean and maintain clean within 7 days

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: 1. Household "Sharp" brand microwave and coffeemaker are not approved for commercial food facilities. Remove
from property, or use for employees only and LABEL "For Employee Use Only." REPEAT VIOLATION (3) Correct within 3 days.
2. Tongs are stored on the handle of the cookline. These come in contact with the apron of chef. Additionally, the tongs can grow
bacteria and contaminate foods. Store tongs in a sanitary manner within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Repeat violation (2): Mop sink has atmospheric vacuum breaker and a "Y" connection fitting below with a hose
attached. The Y-fitting has off/on switch that can cause the atmospheric vacuum breaker to fail, UNDETECTED. Provide a hose bib
vacuum breaker OR remove the Y fitting. See handouts provided on inspection dated 11/23/15. If you need additional handouts,
contact Debbie Katz at (916) 875-8448. Correct to protect potable water supply within 30 days.
