#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION
-Observed one dozen raw shell eggs (63F) storing on counter top opposite back wok station at room temperature. Per operator,
cook was cooking and used half a dozen but forgot to relocate remaining eggs back into the walkin refrigerator. Cooking was done
approximately 45 minutes prior. Ensure that only the quantity used is brought out each time to avoid potentially hazardous food
being in the danger zone. Eggs were relocated back into the walkin.
-Observed chicken and fried pork at 45F-47F inside the upright refrigerator opposite the middle wok station. Unit was lowered
during time of inspection. Per operator, unit is out of temperature due to frequent use. Regardless of use, all refrigeration units
must be able to maintain food at 41F or below. If this unit is not able to maintain food at 41F during next routine inspection, unit
must be repaired or relocated to an area and used for storage that does not require frequently opening the unit throughout the day.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#8

TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL

OBSERVATIONS: -Time log being used for sushi rolls (43F-49F) lacked entries for two items. Per operator all rolls were brought out
at 11am when they first opened (2.5 hours prior.) Times were added to log during time of inspection. Ensure that all logs tracking
time for time as a public health control is completed at all times for all items. Any remaining food items on time as a public health
control must be discarded after four hours. Old food from first batch that has exceeded fours cannot be added to any new trays of
food brought out.

CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be
marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within
the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was
used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: -Pink slime-like accumulation observed on both panels inside both ice machines. Clean and maintain
immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: -Ambient temperature for upright refrigerator opposite the deep fryers observed at 45F. Unit was lowered during
time of inspection. Ambient temperature after correction is 41F.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: -Furthest right filter is not fitting flush inside the ventilation hood above the middle wok station. Correct today.
-Both light covers in the ceiling lights above the dishwasher are beyond repair. Replace within 14 days. Operator has been taping
cracks with transparent tape.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: -Both the upright refrigerator in the cook's line and the upright refrigerator by the dishwasher lack thermometers.
Correct within 48 hours. Both units have built in thermometers but both thermometers are not accurate to what ambient
temperatures inside the unit really are.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: -Paper towels were storing directly on top of dispenser inside the women's restroom. Ensure that paper towel is

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: -Back door was propped open with a cardboard box during time of inspection. Ensure that back door is closed at
all times when not in used. Corrected during inspection.
-Observed 4 sticky fly trap tapes hanging from the ceiling above the prep table by the three compartment sink. Discard sticky fly
traps immediately and consult with your pest control company for an approved fly trap.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
