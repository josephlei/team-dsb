HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Sliced and diced tomatoes inside non-working prep refrigerator at 67F. Operator stated they were cut about 30
minutes prior. Item was relocated into one door stand up refrigerator. Non-working refrigerator is not working but is used to store
non-potentially hazardous foods like peppers, mustard, cucumbers, raw onions.
MINOR VIOLATION:
Rice left on counter at 50F. It was brought out for an order and didn't get immediately put back in. Food must remain in constant
temperature control. If removed from refrigerator it must be put back after immediate use.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Employee observed wiping hands no cloth hanging from the waist. Discontinue practice. Any times hands become
contaminated or there is a change in task, hands must be washed in hand sink with soap, warm water and dried with single use
paper towels. Education provided during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Plastic bucket like container of cut potatoes stored inside 2 door milk reach in refrigerator was at 58F. It was
cooked around 12pm. Food height should be no more than 2 inches high to allow food to cool rapidly. Operator placed them in chest
freezer to cool it down quickly and once at 41F it can be moved into refrigerator. Another batch of potatoes were properly cooling
inside chest freezer was cooled down to 44F.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice
as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.
(114002, 114002.1)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Container of chocolate powdered and sugar are not covered. Keep covered to prevent contamination. Correct
within 24 hours (REPEAT VIOLATION).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

with its contents. Correct within 3 days (REPEAT VIOLATION).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)
