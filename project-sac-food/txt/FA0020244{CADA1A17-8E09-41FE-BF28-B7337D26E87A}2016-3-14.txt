#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following food items are stored out of proper holding temperature:
-ravioli on prep table at 63F
-raw calamari stored in 3-compartment sink at 58F
Minor violation:
-feta cheese in prep line cold top at 44F
-blue cheese in prep line cold top at 46F
-cooked pasta in cook's line cold drawer at 46F
-shredded cheese in pizza prep cold drawer at 45F
-sliced ham in pizza prep cold drawer at 46F
-cooked sausage in pizza prep cold drawer at 45F
Corrected immediately. Raw calamari was relocated inside the walk-in refrigerator. Ravioli was voluntarily discarded by operator
during inspection. Cold drawer and cold top refrigerators appear to be holding ambient air temperature at 41F or below. Ensure that
cold drawer units are shut after use to prevent cold air loss. Lids are highly recommended for cold top food inserts. Do not
overstack food in cold top inserts. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Hot water is not available at the kitchen hand sink due to a loose faucet knob. Corrected on-site.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Open trays of corn bread are stored on racks in dry food storage area uncovered. Provide lids to cover food
containers today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Excessive water accumulation is observed on the floor in dry food storage area. Corrected; floor was mopped
during inspection.
Heavy dust build-up is observed on portable fan. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
Minor debris build-up is observed on top of the automatic dish machine. Clean and maintain within 2 days.
Wall behind the automatic dish machine is unclean with black build-up. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
Dust build-up is observed on fan guards inside the 2-door upright vegetable refrigerator. Clean and maintain within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Chlorine and quaternary ammonia sanitizer test strips are stored in office and not easily accessible for use.
Corrected. Note: Test strips shall be made easily accessible for employees and available at bar and kitchen.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used.
Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical
warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Several plastic food containers and lids used in kitchen are damaged. Replace within 2 days. (REPEAT
VIOLATION-4TH NOTICE)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Cook's line 2-door reach-in cooler is missing a thermometer. Correct within 2 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 40

WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE

OBSERVATIONS: Quaternary ammonia sanitizer solution in wipe cloth bucket at pizza prep station measured 150 ppm. Correct
immediately. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water
with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact
surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).

# 47a

SIGNS POSTED AS REQUIRED

OBSERVATIONS: Restroom hand sinks are missing handwashing signs. Provide within 3 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing signs directing employees to wash hands after using the restroom shall be posted in each toilet room
used by food employees. No smoking signs shall be posted in food preparation, food storage, warewashing, and utensil storage areas.
Food facilities constructed without a public toilet prior January 1, 2004, shall post a sign in a prominant area within the food facility, stating
that toilet facilities are not provided. (113953.5, 113978, 114276 (f)).
