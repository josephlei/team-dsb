Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential
to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Garlic in oil (60F) was observed in the expo line counter. Maintain all potentially hazardous
foods at proper holding temperature at all times. This item was made just prior to the start of the inspection. Discard by 2:00pm.
MINOR VIOLATION - Various food items on the right side of the expo line (shredded cheeses, cooked chicken, spicy chicken, ham,
sliced tomatoes at 43-48F) and cooked breaded onions (48F) in the grill area were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all
potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION

OBSERVATIONS: Food safety certificate on hand was a copy. Maintain original food safety certificate on site at all times. Correct
within 14 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee
who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: One expired food handler certificates and one certificate lacked an expiration date. Register these individuals to
an approved class within 14 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#4

EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Employee beverages in the salad area (one inside the microwave and another on rack right of the dessert
refrigerator) were lacking lid and straw. Provide lid and straw to all employee beverages at all times.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and
utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to
prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for
employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from
contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Slime/mold was observed around the ice chute inside the ice machine by the office. Clean and sanitize this
surface immediately.

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All
violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Handwash stations in the women's restroom and front area by the soups were not adequate as they dispensed
water at 70-97F and 118F, respectively. Adjust all handwash stations to dispense water between 100-108F for units lacking separate
hot and cold water handles. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 25

PERSONAL CLEANLINESS AND HAIR RESTRAINT

OBSERVATIONS: Staff in the kitchen is observed with long hair. Provide a more thorough hair restraint for staff with long hair.
Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall wear clean outer clothing to prevent contamination of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and
single-use articles. Employees preparing, serving or handling food or utensils shall wear hair restraints such as hats, hair coverings, or nets.
Food employees shall keep their fingernails trimmed, filed, and maintained or shall wear gloves when contacting food and food-contact
surfaces. (113968, 113969, 113971, 113973)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Container of ice cream in the freezer was lacking a cover. Provide cover to all food items in storage. Correct
immediately to prevent cross contamination.
Container of battered onion strips was stored inside a six pan container in the grill makeline. Cease this process immediately.
Store containers with PHF that allow contact with circulating cold air. Corrected during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Food debris and trash is accumulating behind the wheels of the rack and behind the door in the dry food storage
room and inside the cabinet located in the middle of the expo line. Clean these areas within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Irreversible temperature recording device or thermolabels was not available for the high temp dishwasher.
Provide either of these items within 3 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Food items in the right side of the expo line were maintaining temperature at 42-48F. Provide service to this area
of the expo line to determine if ambient temperature required adjusting, more freon, or better air circulation to maintain proper
holding temperature. Correct within 3 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: One of two light fixtures in the upright freezer across the ice machine was not functional. Repair within 3 days.
Hood filters above the grill and pizza oven are deteriorated and/or displaying gaps. Repair filters to eliminate gap and maintain in
proper condition to prevent fire hazard. Correct within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.
Vent covers are missing and/or broken and/or in dire need of cleaning in the surplus storage room and liquor room. Repair, clean
these covers immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 44c

MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA

OBSERVATIONS: Wet mop stored in the mop sink. Hang mops after use to allow drying. Correct within 24 hours. REPEAT
VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located
for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or
cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and
supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279,
114281, 114282)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Purse was stored on box of chips and umbrella stored on top of soda boxes in the front expo area. Store all
personal items under or away from all food and food related items at all times. Correct immediately.

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

damage.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure

juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
