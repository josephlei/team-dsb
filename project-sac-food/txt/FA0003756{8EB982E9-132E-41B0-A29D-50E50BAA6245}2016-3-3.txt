Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Magnetic knife holder under pass thru window above prep cooler in kitchen found with old food debris. Clean and
sanitize today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: No hot water available at 2 restrooms near kitchen. Provide hot water min. 100 F. Correct within 7 days.
Rust/sand and brownish water comes from faucet when hot water is turned on in 2 restrooms near kitchen. When cold water is
turned on, water appears clear. Determine source of rust/sand and repair immediately. Turn off hot water supply in those two
restrooms. These restrooms are on a different water heater that is located in one of the restrooms. Email inspector proof of
correction to avoid reinspection and fees. Hot and cold water from kitchen faucets appear clear.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Boxes of tortillas stored on floor in walk-in cooler. Maintain all food products min. 6 inches above floor. Correct
today.
Salt and pepper containers stored outside uncovered in satellite bar. Satellite bar has straw top. Weather-break in rain. Transfer
food products into kitchen to prevent contamination.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged
food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: Foam insulation found around piping on table/shelf near microwave and Hatco toaster. Remove excess foam and
provide smooth surface (seal foam) within 7 days.
Debris on floor under warewash sink. Grease on wall and floor in hallway for kitchen near wall mounted fire extinguisher. Clean
within 72 hours and maintain.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 36

EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Several cracked/damaged lids found on food products in prep cooler and walk-in. Replace damaged equipment
within 2 weeks.

violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an
approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be
exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and
food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean

displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely
fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3,
114185.4, 114185.5)

# 38

VENTILATION AND LIGHTING

OBSERVATIONS: Missing end caps over lights in kitchen near hood. Provide within 7 days.
Light inside 1-door freezer is non-functional. Correct within 7 days. Repeat.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health
and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient
ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or
light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in
areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with
light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Drain pipe from ice machine an drain pipe from ice bins in bar lack required air gap for floor sinks. Provide air
gap min. 1 inch from drain pipe to rim of floor sink within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: FRP behind prep cooler in kitchen is not secured to brick wall. Also metal siding on wall next to flat grill has small
gaps. Seal within 2 weeks. 2nd NOTICE.
Electrical cover behind rack in server station is held in place with tape. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
