#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR- Measured the following potentially hazardous foods out of proper holding temperature:
-marinated cooked chicken at Win Holt branded warmer (99-102F)
-pizza (cheese) on rolling cart in pizza area (60F)- unattended
Maintain all potentially hazardous foods at proper holding temperature. Corrected by reheating chicken in steamer to 170F and
pizzas moved to walk in cooler. REPEAT VIOLATION . Education provided on alternatives to ensure proper food holding (TPHC).

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found 3 rodent droppings below the shelves in the dry storage room near the double door entrance/exit doors.
Remove and sanitize area now. Manager contacted pest control company at time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS: Found raw frozen beef stored over french fries which was moved to below the raw breaded chicken. Store food
items in proper order as stated on decal provided at time of inspection. Post decal in refrigerator/freezer. Corrected at time of
inspection
Found open food containers in the grill area reach in refrigerator (tomatoes) and in the walk in freezer (dough for cookies). Keep
covered during storage to protect from contamination. Correct now.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze
guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially
hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from
public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS: The following areas are unclean:
-debris found in between the cooking equipment and refrigerated drawers.
-dust build up observed on vent of HVAC just above the walk in freezer.
-dark residue build up observed on floor behind the 3 door Pepsi branded merchandiser.
Clean and maintain within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Ambient air temperature at the sandwich prep. area reach in refrigerator measured at 55F. Refrigerator was not in
use at time of inspection (no food items but turned on). Correct to an ambient temperature of 41F or below PRIOR to use. Send proof
of correction such as an invoice or work order to the following email: estradam2@saccounty.net within 2 days).
Condensation (ice build up) observed just below the cooling unit inside the walk in freezer. Repair unit to prevent ice build up within
7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS: Floor sink just below the water heater appears to be blocked (standing water observed). Repair unit to ensure
drain works correctly.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Employee personal items (coffee mixer) stored above food items intended for public consumption in the
refrigerator in main kitchen. Keep employee personal food items in designated areas. Corrected at time of inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)
