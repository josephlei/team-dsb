#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Container with Sapphire rice and and white rice at 70F- 71F was stored inside microwave oven. Operator
previously noted that they no longer store sapphire rice at room temperature. Potentially hazardous foods may not be stored at room
temperature unless time as a public health control (TPHC) is used. Store foods at/below 41F or at/above 135F. TPHC handout
provided & discussed with operator. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return
to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

HANDWASHING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Observed helper donning gloves and handling dry dates at front area near cash register without washing hands
first. Hands must be always washed before handling food. Facility is not approved to handle open food at front area. (Note front
sinks are not approved.) All food at front display area shall be prepackaged. Manager and helper was educated during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after
touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled
equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw
to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate
hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Deep black crevices observed on cutting boards at kitchen area. Deep clean cutting boards or replace. Correct
today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each
use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred.
Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 33

OBSERVATIONS: - Dry food residue build up inside commercial microwave oven.
- Dust build up on fan guard inside True reach-in cooler.
Clean and maintain within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS: Soiled household grinder observed inside walk-in cooler. All equipment must be commercial grade ANSI/NSF
approved. Remove today. Facility is not approved to cook food items at this time.
Observed broken wooden counter top with crevices in the back area by large 3-comp sink. Replace or remove within 7 days. Do not
use for any food prep.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare
foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching
and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2,
114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: Facility is missing probe thermometer. Provide within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available
to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their
accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines.
(114157, 114159)

# 42

GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL

OBSERVATIONS: Shared dumpster observed to be overfilled. Contact landlord regarding dumpster service to obtain larger bin or
increase pick up frequency. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be
kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at
all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and
other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6,
114245.7, 114257)

# 43

RESTROOMS MAINTAINED, SUPPLIED, AND ACCESSIBLE

OBSERVATIONS: Both restroom doors do not self-close completely. Adjust to self-close within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Toilet facilities shall be maintained and kept clean. Toilet rooms shall be separated by a well-fitting self-closing door.
Toilet tissue shall be provided in a permanently installed dispenser at each toilet. The number of toilet facilities shall be in accordance with
local building and plumbing ordinances. Toilet facilities shall be provided for patrons: in establishments with more than 20,000 sq. ft.;
establishments offering on-site consumption of food. (114250, 114250.1, 114276)

# 44a

PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT

OBSERVATIONS: Observed multiple unclean and unused equipment inside facility like upright coolers, open air coolers, reach-in
cooler, display unit, ice machine, shelvings, etc. Food code requires that the premises shall be free of litter and items that are
unnecessary to the operation and maintenance of the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. Remove all
unnecessary equipment/clutter, organize and clean affected areas to deny vermin of possible harborage. See Plan Review
inspection report for details SR#29471.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of
the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)

# 44d

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS

OBSERVATIONS: Employee personal food items stored on top shelf above food for facility inside walk-in cooler. Relocate to lower
shelf within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers
or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other
suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles
cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS: Rear door leading to bbq area does not self-close completely. Repair to self-close completely to prevent vermin
entry within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage
of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device
and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food
or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if
required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: - Observed holes/gaps in ceiling above small 3-comp sink, above oven, display freezer, and around plumbing in
different areas throughout the store.
- Missing floor tiles beneath small reach-in cooler storing beverages located between retail and restaurant. Missing cove base tiles
behind floor sink beneath small 3-comp sink.
See Plan Review inspection report for additional details and corrective time frame SR#29471.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base
coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the
juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268,
114268.1, 114271, 114272)
