Routine

Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700.
All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the
potential to cause foodborne illness. All major violations must be corrected immediately. Non-compliance may warrant immediate closure of the food facility.

# 1a

DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Staff was observed placing cooked chicken in a deep container, cover the container and place in the walk-in
cooler. Education was provided regarding proper cooling practice during the inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 1c

FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: One food handler certificate could not be located and one has expired. Register these two individuals to an
approved class within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food
Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)

#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Fried polenta (48F) in the right makeline top insert, shredded cheddar (44-48F) in the front server's "ice bath",
provolone (48F) in the top insert to the right of the oven were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all potentially hazardous
foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

#9

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Container of cooked bowtie pasta that is cooling was packed tightly in a deep container with a cover in the
walk-in cooler. Cease this practice immediately. Do not pack hot food items tightly in a deep container as it prolongs the cooling
process. Utilize one or more approved methods of cooling (e.g. shallow uncovered containers, ice bath, ice sticks/wands, blast
chiller, adding ice as an ingredient, etc.) to cool within a timely manner. Correct immediately.
Trays of hot food items are stored under cooled food items in the cooling rack in the walk-in cooler. Cease this practice
immediately to prevent warming already cooled food items. Implement immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as
an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002,
114002.1)

# 14a

FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED

OBSERVATIONS: Slime/mold was observed on the surface around the ice chute inside the ice machine. Clean and sanitize this
area immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use
with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and
potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact
surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: Water dispensed in the left restroom peaked at 80F. Adjust setting to provide water to peak at 100-108F. Correct
within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 30a

FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION

OBSERVATIONS: Container of lemons are stored on the walk-in floor. Store all food and food related items at least 6" off the floor
at all times. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed
permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged food
may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging.
Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: Containers of food items in the storage room and hallway were lacking labels. Provide labels to all containers
with food. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 34

WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Test strips could not be located during the inspection. Provide both types of sanitizer strips within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments
with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in
approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used. Testing
materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical warewashing.
(113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)

# 45

FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES

OBSERVATIONS: Deteriorated grout observed under the recycling bins near the swing door. Repair grout to prevent floor damage.
Correct within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION.
Peeling cove base tiles (or deteriorated grout) obseved under the prep sink and to the left of the ice machine. Repair and seal
these tiles/coving to prevent vermin harborage. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure
that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving
shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture
of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1,
114271, 114272)
