#7

HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS:
1. Observed the following cold-held foods above 41 deg. F in the chef prep.
a. cut melon at 45 deg. F in chef prep - insert
b. sliced tomato at 45 deg. F in the chef prep - insert
c. diced tomato at 43 deg. F in the chef prep - insert
d. ground beef at 44.9 deg. F in the chef prep -inside
Note: Raw shelled eggs are over stacked above the chill line of the insert. Other foods measured 39 deg. to 41 deg.
Walk in temperatures:
e. Brown rice in the walk in at 44 deg. F
f. Potato salad in the walk in at 43 deg. F
Keep cold held potentially hazardous foods at or below 41 deg. F within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be
held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed
containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to
the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 19

CONSUMER ADVISORY FOR UNDERCOOKED FOOD

OBSERVATIONS:
Menu lacks consumer warning for undercooked food - observed photos (Yelp) with undercooked whole eggs. Provide warning
within 7 days. See handout for different ways to comply.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Undercooked or raw food may be served to the comsumer if either of the following conditions are met: the consumer
specifically orders the food prepared raw or less than throughly cooked, or the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the
time of ordering, that the food is raw or less than throughly cooked. Confectionery food containing more than 0.5% alcohol may be served to
the consumer if the food facility notifies the consumer orally or in writing, at the time of ordering, that it contains more than 0.5% alcohol.
(114012, 114093)

# 27

FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION

OBSERVATIONS:
Raw steaks are above the ice cream and lesser cooked foods in the Spartan freezer. Reverse order so raw foods
of animal origins are stored below and separate from lesser cooked/ready-to-eat foods within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food
and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards
that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or
uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access.
(113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))

# 30b

FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED

OBSERVATIONS: White powders are found unlabeled in several locations due to new bulk storage bins. Label containers when
food is removed from packaging: Flour under the steam table at the cookline, square bulk containers in the storage room, within 14
days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the
common name of the food. (114051)

# 33

NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN

OBSERVATIONS:
1. Racks in the walk in box have some minor food debris. Clean racks within 30 days.
2. Back wall behind the dishwasher has some green/brown discoloration (slime/mold). Clean within 7 days

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other
debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 35

EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

OBSERVATIONS:
1. Observed a styrofoam take-out cup used as a scoop in the flour under the cookline steam table. Use scoops
with handles that are not going to break apart and leave materials such as styrofoam in the food. Correct within 1 day. Keep
handles out of food product.
2. Compressed gas cylinders for soda fountain are not restrained. Provide chain/restraint within 14 days.
Section 114172 of the Health and Safety Code states: All pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to a rigid structure.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be
properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods
shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and
scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3,
114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)

# 41

PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION

OBSERVATIONS:
Y-fitting on the mop sink is putting pressure on the atmospheric vacuum breaker. This can cause the
atmospheric vacuum breaker to fail without any signs of failure and allow waste water to enter the potable water system. Provide
approved anti-siphon device between the faucet and the Y-connect or remove the Y-fitting after each use. There should NOT be any
ability to turn the water off below the atmospheric vacuum breaker, where water "stands" in the pipe/hose.
See handouts. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other
similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and
plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable
water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved
materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)

# 44e

VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION

OBSERVATIONS:
Dachshund dog in a red sweater and bell leash in the dinning room. See handout and sign. Note: ONLY
Service animals are allowed inside. All animals are allowed to join diners on the patio, but not inside. Discontinue allowing patrons
to bring animals in the restaurant, serve patrons with dogs on the patio. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage of
animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device and
have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food or
utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if required.
(114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
