#9

MAJOR

COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Pans of ground beef filled ~3 inches, that were pulled from the oven at 10a today were improperly cooling at
room temperature without additional intervention and measured at 93F at 1215p. Center of one pan measured at 133F, while the
sides measured at 108F. Corrected by placing pans on ice bath and bags of ice over the top of meat to rapidly cool. Beef measured
at 66F within 10 minutes and both were placed in the walk-in, loosely covered, to continue the cooling process.
Minor Violation: A sheet pan of chicken strips (58F) that was baked at 10a today was improperly cooling in the walk-in tightly
covered with a plastic bag. Corrected- bag was opened up and loosened.
Provided cooling education and stickers. Recommend use of ice bath and also ice bags placed over the top to rapidly cool large
portions of foods. In addition, frequent stirring will facilitate proper and even cooling. Note: facility also cools whole turkey roasts.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within
4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as
an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002,
114002.1)

HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Second employee restroom paper towel dispenser was not consistently dispensing paper towels. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas.
Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and
sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953,
113953.1, 113953.2)

# 21

HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY

OBSERVATIONS: First and second employee restroom hand sinks water measured at a maximum of 75F and 69F, respectively.
Units are connected to instant water heaters. Correct within 7 days. Note: kitchen hand sink measured at 108F.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water
shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to
handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set
between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)

# 39

TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES

OBSERVATIONS: All probe thermometers were uncalibrated (read 20F at room temperature). 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available to
the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their accuracy. A
thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines. (114157,
114159)
